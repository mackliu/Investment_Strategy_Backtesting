3.1 殖利率比較3.1 Dividend Yield Comparison

0056 的平均殖利率（6.91%）顯著高於 0050（3.43%），差異約 3.48 個百分點。配對 t 檢定結果高度顯著（p<0.001），效果量 Cohen's d = 1.34（屬極大效果），Bootstrap 95% CI 不包含 0。0056 的殖利率優勢在每個分析年度都成立。

0056's mean yield (6.91%) is significantly higher than 0050's (3.43%), a difference of ~3.48 percentage points. The paired t-test is highly significant (p<0.001), with Cohen's d = 1.34 (very large effect). The Bootstrap 95% CI excludes 0. 0056's yield advantage holds in every analysis year.

上方互動圖清楚呈現 0056 在每個年度的殖利率（橘色）都高於 0050（藍色），平均差距約 3.48 個百分點。0056 的殖利率主要分佈在 3.5%-8% 之間，而 0050 多在 1.5%-4% 之間。值得注意的是，殖利率最高的年份通常出現在市場大跌之後（如 2009 年），因為分母（股價）下降但分子（配息）基於前一年盈餘而延遲反應。這意味著「高殖利率」有時是市場下跌的副產品，而非主動的股利政策成就。

The interactive chart above clearly shows 0056's yield (orange) exceeding 0050 (blue) in every year, with an average gap of ~3.48 percentage points. 0056's yield mainly ranges between 3.5%-8%, while 0050 stays mostly within 1.5%-4%. Notably, the highest yield years typically follow market crashes (e.g., 2009), because the denominator (price) drops while the numerator (dividend) lags based on prior-year earnings. This means 'high yield' is sometimes a byproduct of market declines, not an active dividend policy achievement.

從年度配息金額來看，0050 平均年配 2.62 元，0056 平均年配 1.82 元。但 0050 的股價較高（拆股前約 130-190 元），因此殖利率較低。值得注意的是 0056 於 2024 年起改為季配息，配息次數增加但每次金額降低——這主要是為了吸引散戶投資人，而非改變了基本面的配息能力。0050 則在 2025 年進行約 4:1 拆股，使每股價格從約 190 元降至約 47 元，但殖利率計算已根據拆股調整。

In absolute terms, 0050 averages NT$2.62 per share annually, while 0056 averages NT$1.82. However, 0050's higher share price (pre-split ~NT$130-190) results in a lower yield. Note that 0056 switched to quarterly dividends starting 2024, increasing frequency but decreasing per-distribution amounts — primarily to attract retail investors rather than changing fundamental distribution capability. 0050 underwent an approximately 4:1 stock split in 2025, reducing per-share price from ~NT$190 to ~NT$47, but yield calculations are split-adjusted.

3.2 除息日效果（左手換右手？）3.2 Ex-Dividend Day Effect (Left Hand to Right Hand?)

除息日股價跌幅與配息金額之比（drop ratio）在 0050 為 0.74、0056 為 1.00。兩者在統計上均不顯著偏離 1.0（0050 p=0.11, 0056 p=0.97）。這表示除息日的股價調整大致等於配息金額，即「你領到的股息幾乎等於股價的下跌」。

這個結果支持「配息是左手換右手」的觀點：配息並非「額外」收入，而是從持有的資產中提取出來的。對退休族而言，領息和賣股在經濟上是等價的。

The ex-dividend day price drop / dividend ratio (drop ratio) is 0.74 for 0050 and 1.00 for 0056. Neither is statistically significantly different from 1.0 (0050 p=0.11, 0056 p=0.97). This means the price adjustment on ex-dividend day approximately equals the dividend amount - 'what you receive in dividends is roughly what you lose in share price.'

This supports the "left hand to right hand" view: dividends are not "extra" income but rather extracted from your existing assets. For retirees, collecting dividends and selling shares are economically equivalent.

上圖展示每次除息事件的 drop ratio（股價跌幅 / 配息金額）。理論上若市場完全有效率，drop ratio 應恰好等於 1.0（紅色虛線）。實際數據顯示多數 drop ratio 落在 0.5-1.5 之間，平均接近 1.0。個別極端值（如某些年份 drop ratio 遠大於 1）可能受除息當天其他市場事件（如美股大跌）的干擾。整體而言，drop ratio 的分佈圍繞 1.0 波動的結果，與國際文獻的發現一致——在沒有顯著稅盾效應的市場中，除息日股價調整大致等於配息金額。

The chart above displays the drop ratio (price drop / dividend amount) for each ex-dividend event. In theory, if markets are perfectly efficient, drop ratios should be exactly 1.0 (red dashed line). Actual data shows most drop ratios fall between 0.5-1.5, averaging near 1.0. Individual extreme values (e.g., drop ratios far exceeding 1) may be distorted by other market events on the ex-dividend day (e.g., a major US market decline). Overall, the distribution of drop ratios fluctuating around 1.0 is consistent with international literature — in markets without significant tax shield effects, ex-dividend price adjustments approximately equal dividend amounts.

3.3 填息分析3.3 Fill-Gap Analysis

0050 的填息率為 96.7%（29/30），中位數填息天數 5 天；0056 的填息率為 87.0%（20/23），中位數填息天數 34 天。

填息率在 Fisher's exact test 中未達顯著差異（p=0.31），但填息速度有顯著差異：0050 填息顯著快於 0056（Mann-Whitney U p=0.003）。0050 中位數僅需 5 天即可填息，而 0056 需要 34 天。

填息只是回到除息前的價格水準，並不代表「賺到了配息」。真正重要的是含息總報酬（adj_close），而非是否填息。

0050's fill rate is 96.7% (29/30), median fill days 5; 0056's fill rate is 87.0% (20/23), median fill days 34.

Fill rates show no significant difference (Fisher's p=0.31), but fill speed differs significantly: 0050 fills gaps much faster than 0056 (Mann-Whitney U p=0.003). 0050 takes a median of 5 days to fill, while 0056 takes 34 days.

Filling the gap merely means returning to the pre-ex-dividend price level; it does not mean you "earned" the dividend. What truly matters is total return (adj_close), not whether the gap was filled.

上圖以箱型圖呈現兩檔 ETF 的填息天數分佈。0050 的中位數填息天數僅 5 天，且分佈相對集中；0056 中位數為 34 天，且離群值更多（某些年份需要超過 100 天才填息）。這個差異在統計上顯著（Mann-Whitney U p=0.003），反映市值型 ETF（受惠於台積電等成長股）的價格恢復力明顯優於高股息 ETF（以傳產、金融股為主）。

但我們必須強調：「填息」只是一個心理安慰指標，而非投資績效指標。即使 0056 填息天數較長，只要最終的含息總報酬（adj_close）為正，投資人仍是獲利的。過度關注填息天數會導致投資人忽略真正重要的全面報酬。

The box plot above shows fill-gap day distributions for both ETFs. 0050's median fill days is only 5, with a relatively concentrated distribution; 0056's median is 34 days with more outliers (some years taking over 100 days to fill). This difference is statistically significant (Mann-Whitney U p=0.003), reflecting that market-cap ETFs (benefiting from growth stocks like TSMC) have notably stronger price recovery than high-dividend ETFs (skewing toward traditional and financial sectors).

However, we must emphasize: 'filling the gap' is a psychological comfort metric, not an investment performance metric. Even if 0056 takes longer to fill, as long as the total return (adj_close) is ultimately positive, the investor has still profited. Excessive focus on fill-gap days causes investors to overlook what truly matters: total return.

3.4 總報酬分解3.4 Total Return Decomposition

在 2008~2025 共同期間，0050 總報酬 610.8%，其中價格報酬 305.3%、股息報酬 305.5%（股息貢獻 50%）。0056 總報酬 284.2%，其中價格報酬 102.6%、股息報酬 181.5%（股息貢獻 64%）。

這說明 0050 的報酬主要來自價格成長（市值型 ETF 的本質），而 0056 高度依賴配息。當一檔 ETF 的報酬主要來自配息時，它實質上是在「自己提領自己的資產」。

During 2008-2025, 0050's total return was 610.8%, split into 305.3% price return and 305.5% dividend return (dividend contribution: 50%). 0056's total return was 284.2%, with 102.6% price and 181.5% dividend (64% contribution).

This illustrates that 0050's returns come primarily from price appreciation (the essence of a market-cap ETF), while 0056 relies heavily on dividends. When an ETF's returns primarily come from dividends, it is essentially "withdrawing from its own assets."

上方互動圖以堆疊面積圖呈現總報酬的分解。0050 的兩個區域（價格報酬 + 股息報酬）中，價格報酬佔主導地位，反映市值型 ETF 以資本增值為核心的特性。0056 則呈現截然不同的結構——股息報酬佔 64% 的主導地位，價格報酬僅貢獻 36% 的總報酬。

這個結構性差異具有深遠的投資含義。當一檔 ETF 的報酬幾乎全來自配息時，它的「成長」本質上是在持續從資產中提取現金——如同一棵果樹，果實很多但樹幹不太長高。0050 則像一棵果實較少但樹幹快速生長的樹——對追求長期資本增值的投資人而言，後者可能更有利。選擇哪種取決於你是需要果實（現金流）還是需要更大的樹（資本成長）。

The interactive chart above uses stacked area to decompose total returns. In 0050's two areas (price return + dividend return), price return dominates, reflecting the capital-appreciation-centric nature of market-cap ETFs. 0056 shows a starkly different structure — dividend return accounts for 64% dominance, with price return contributing only 36% of total return.

This structural difference has profound investment implications. When an ETF's returns come almost entirely from dividends, its 'growth' is essentially continuous cash extraction from assets — like a fruit tree with abundant fruit but a trunk that barely grows. 0050 is like a tree with fewer fruits but rapid trunk growth — for investors seeking long-term capital appreciation, the latter may be more advantageous. The choice depends on whether you need fruit (cash flow) or a larger tree (capital growth).

扣除通膨後，0050 實質殖利率平均 2.22%，0056 為 5.73%。兩者均維持正值，表示配息至少能跟上通膨。

After inflation adjustment, 0050's real yield averages 2.22%, 0056's 5.73%. Both remain positive, indicating dividends at least keep pace with inflation.

上圖呈現名目殖利率與實質殖利率的時間序列。在大部分年份，0056 的實質殖利率維持正值（約 2-6%），表示扣除通膨後配息仍有實際購買力。但在通膨較高的年份（如 2022 年 CPI 年增率接近 3%），實質殖利率明顯收窄。0050 的實質殖利率在多數年份約為 0.5-2.5%，雖然較低但仍為正。這說明兩檔 ETF 的配息至少能維持購買力不縮水，但 0056 提供了更高的通膨保護空間。

The chart above shows time series of nominal and real dividend yields. In most years, 0056's real yield remains positive (~2-6%), meaning dividends retain purchasing power after inflation. However, in high-inflation years (e.g., 2022 CPI near 3%), real yields noticeably compress. 0050's real yield is ~0.5-2.5% in most years — lower but still positive. This shows both ETFs' dividends at least maintain purchasing power, though 0056 provides greater inflation protection margin.

3.6 退休模擬3.6 Retirement Simulation

投入 100 萬元，從 2008 年開始模擬 15 年：

  • 策略 A（0056 領息）：累計配息 751,456 元 + 期末市值 986,408 元 = 1,737,864 元
  • 策略 B（0050 賣股）：累計賣出 751,456 元 + 期末市值 589,627 元 = 1,341,083 元

在此基礎情境中，0056 領息策略的合計價值較高。但多起始年份穩健性分析（2008~2015 各 10 年）中，0050 賣股策略勝出率為 38%，差異在統計上不顯著。

With NT$1M starting from 2008, simulated for 15 years:

  • Strategy A (0056 dividends): Cumulative dividends NT$751,456 + final portfolio NT$986,408 = NT$1,737,864
  • Strategy B (0050 sell): Cumulative sales NT$751,456 + final portfolio NT$589,627 = NT$1,341,083

In this base scenario, 0056 dividend strategy has higher total value. However, in multi-start robustness analysis (2008-2015, 10 years each), 0050 sell strategy wins in 38% of scenarios, and the difference is not statistically significant.

上方互動圖呈現兩種退休策略的逐年比較。橫軸為模擬年份，左 Y 軸為累計現金收入（配息或賣股所得），右 Y 軸為期末持股市值。投資人可將滑鼠移至各資料點查看精確數值。需特別注意的是：0050 賣股策略的「累計賣出」金額是人為設定的（等於 0056 平均年配息），因此現金流部分是刻意對齊的——真正的差異在於期末持股市值。

這個模擬揭示了一個反直覺的事實：「領息」和「賣股」在現金流效果上幾乎相同，但因為 0050 的資本增值優勢，賣出等額股票後剩餘的持股市值往往更高。對退休族而言，這意味著「自製配息」（持有 0050 定期賣出）可能是比「被動領息」（持有 0056 等配息入帳）更有效率的策略——但前提是投資人有紀律執行定期賣出的計畫。

The interactive chart above compares both retirement strategies year by year. The x-axis shows simulation years, left Y-axis shows cumulative cash income (dividends or sale proceeds), right Y-axis shows ending portfolio value. Hover over data points for precise values. An important note: the 0050 sell strategy's 'cumulative sales' amount is deliberately set equal to 0056's average annual dividend, so the cash flow component is intentionally aligned — the real difference lies in the ending portfolio value.

This simulation reveals a counterintuitive fact: 'collecting dividends' and 'selling shares' produce virtually identical cash flow effects, but because of 0050's capital appreciation advantage, the remaining portfolio value after selling equivalent amounts tends to be higher. For retirees, this means 'homemade dividends' (holding 0050, selling periodically) may be more efficient than 'passive dividends' (holding 0056, waiting for distributions) — but only if the investor has the discipline to execute a regular selling plan.

3.7 稅後報酬3.7 After-Tax Returns

在三種稅務情境下的年化報酬比較：

  • 免稅：0050 11.54% vs 0056 5.24%
  • 退休族（5% 合併、8.5% 抵減、NHI 2.11%）：0050 11.48% vs 0056 5.18%
  • 高所得（28% 分離、NHI 2.11%）：0050 10.66% vs 0056 4.38%

由於配息金額越高，稅負越重，0056 在高稅率情境下的劣勢更加明顯。高配息策略對高所得者尤其不利。

Annualized returns across three tax scenarios:

  • Tax-free: 0050 11.54% vs 0056 5.24%
  • Retiree (5% combined, 8.5% credit, NHI 2.11%): 0050 11.48% vs 0056 5.18%
  • High income (28% separate, NHI 2.11%): 0050 10.66% vs 0056 4.38%

Since higher dividends incur higher taxes, 0056's disadvantage becomes more pronounced under high tax rates. High-dividend strategies are particularly unfavorable for high-income earners.

上圖以分組柱狀圖比較三種稅務情境下 0050 和 0056 的年化報酬。在免稅情境下，兩者差距主要反映總報酬差異。但隨著稅率提高（退休族 5% -> 高所得 28%），0056 的報酬被侵蝕得更嚴重——這是因為 0056 配息金額更高，被課徵的稅額也更多。高配息策略在高稅率環境下面臨雙重打擊：(1) 總報酬本來就較低，(2) 稅負還更重。對高所得者而言，0050 的資本利得（在台灣目前免稅）加上較少的配息稅負，提供了明顯更好的稅後效率。

這個發現對投資規劃的啟示是：高股息策略的適用性因稅率而異。退休族（低稅率）選擇 0056 的稅務代價相對有限；但高所得族群若追求高配息，可能在不知不覺中支付了可觀的「隱性稅務成本」。

The chart above uses grouped bars to compare 0050 and 0056 annual returns across three tax scenarios. In the tax-free scenario, the gap mainly reflects total return differences. But as tax rates increase (retiree 5% -> high income 28%), 0056's returns erode more severely — because 0056 distributes more dividends, resulting in higher tax amounts. High-dividend strategies face a double hit under high tax rates: (1) total returns are already lower, (2) tax burden is even heavier. For high-income earners, 0050's capital gains (currently tax-free in Taiwan) combined with lower dividend tax exposure provide clearly better after-tax efficiency.

The investment planning implication: high-dividend strategy suitability varies by tax bracket. Retirees (low tax rate) face limited tax cost from choosing 0056; but high-income earners pursuing high dividends may unknowingly pay substantial 'hidden tax costs.'

3.8 子期間分析：2017 年前後的差異3.8 Sub-Period Analysis: Pre vs Post 2017

2017 年後台積電權重飆升，台股結構性轉變。將分析期間以 2017 年為界拆分：

  • 2008-2016（科技股暴漲前）：0050 年化 5.21% vs 0056 年化 3.40%，差距僅 1.81 個百分點
  • 2017-2025（科技股暴漲後）：0050 年化 18.41% vs 0056 年化 12.45%，差距擴大至 5.96 個百分點

這顯示 0050 的報酬優勢高度依賴 2017 年後的科技股行情。在 2008-2016 兩者表現相近的環境下，退休模擬中 0056 領息策略實際上勝出（合計 1,270,680 vs 1,074,719）。這表明「0050 一定比 0056 好」的結論不適用於所有市場制度。

The surge in TSMC's weight after 2017 structurally transformed Taiwan's stock market. Splitting the analysis at 2017:

  • 2008-2016 (pre tech boom): 0050 annualized 5.21% vs 0056 3.40%, gap only 1.81 pp
  • 2017-2025 (post tech boom): 0050 annualized 18.41% vs 0056 12.45%, gap widened to 5.96 pp

This shows 0050's return advantage is highly dependent on post-2017 tech sector performance. In the 2008-2016 environment when both performed similarly, 0056 dividend strategy actually wins in retirement simulation (1,270,680 vs 1,074,719). The conclusion that "0050 always beats 0056" does not hold across all market regimes.

3.9 「左手換右手」的長期驗證3.9 "Left Hand to Right Hand" Long-Term Verification

除息日 drop ratio ~= 1.0 表明短期內配息確實等於股價下跌。但如果配息「真的」是左手換右手（如龐氏結構），那 0056 的淨值應持續歸零。實際情況如何？

從 2008 年開始追蹤：

  • 初始價格：25.75 元
  • 累計配息：29.05 元/股（已超過初始股價的 113%）
  • 如果是龐氏（本金侵蝕）：淨值應歸零（25.75 - 29.05 = 0.00）
  • 0056 實際收盤：35.15 元（成長 36.5%）
  • 含息總價值：64.20 元（成長 149%）

結論：0056 在 17 年間累計配出的金額已超過初始股價，但股價不僅沒有歸零，反而成長了。這證明 ETF 配的是「企業盈餘」而非「投資人的本金」。除息日的短期 drop ratio ~= 1.0 是機械性效果，不等於「配息沒有價值」。真正的左手換右手是本金逐漸耗盡（如圖中灰色虛線），與 0056 實際走勢截然不同。

The ex-dividend day drop ratio ~= 1.0 shows that short-term, dividends do equal price drops. But if dividends were truly 'left hand to right hand' (Ponzi structure), 0056's NAV should decline toward zero. What actually happened?

Tracking from 2008:

  • Initial price: 25.75
  • Cumulative dividends: 29.05 per share (113% of initial price)
  • If Ponzi (principal erosion): NAV should be 0.00
  • 0056 actual close: 35.15 (grew 36.5%)
  • Close + dividends: 64.20 (grew 149%)

Conclusion: Over 17 years, 0056 distributed more than its initial price in cumulative dividends, yet the price did not decline to zero — it actually grew. This proves ETF dividends come from corporate earnings, not investor principal. The short-term drop ratio ~= 1.0 is a mechanical effect and does NOT mean "dividends have no value." True "left hand to right hand" means principal gradually depletes to zero (gray dashed line in chart), which is fundamentally different from 0056's actual trajectory.
