V-003：高股息 ETF 是退休首選？V-003: Are High-Dividend ETFs the Best Choice for Retirement?

緒論Introduction

1.1 研究背景1.1 Background

「退休後靠股息過生活」是台灣投資社群中最受歡迎的退休規劃策略之一。以 0056（元大高股息 ETF）為代表的高股息 ETF，因其穩定的配息紀錄和相對較高的殖利率，被許多投資達人推薦為退休族的首選投資工具。

然而，這個論點忽略了幾個關鍵問題：配息是否真的是「額外收入」？除息後股價下跌（「左手換右手」效應）的影響有多大？高殖利率是否意味著更高的總報酬？稅負對配息收入的影響如何？這些問題需要嚴謹的數據分析來回答。

本研究建立一套科學思考框架，以客觀數據檢視「高股息 ETF 是退休首選」的論點。我們的目的不是批評任何人，而是希望投資人能建立獨立思考的能力，不盲從任何投資論述。這個框架本身也可能在未來被修正，但重要的是思考過程本身。

"Living off dividends in retirement" is one of the most popular retirement strategies in Taiwan's investment community. High-dividend ETFs like 0056 (Yuanta High Dividend) are frequently recommended by financial influencers as the top choice for retirees, thanks to their stable dividend history and relatively high yields.

However, this argument overlooks several critical questions: Are dividends truly "extra income"? How significant is the ex-dividend price drop ("left hand to right hand" effect)? Does a higher yield mean higher total returns? How do taxes impact dividend income? These questions require rigorous data analysis to answer.

This study establishes a scientific thinking framework to objectively examine the claim that "high-dividend ETFs are the best choice for retirement." Our purpose is not to criticize anyone, but to help investors develop independent thinking abilities rather than blindly following any investment narrative. This framework itself may be revised in the future, but what matters is the thinking process itself.

1.2 文獻回顧1.2 Literature Review

配息無關性理論：Modigliani-Miller (1961) 的「股利無關論」主張，在完美市場中，公司的股利政策不影響其價值。投資人可以透過賣出股票自製「股息」（homemade dividends），效果與領取配息相同。這個理論的核心邏輯是：配息只是把你口袋裡的錢從左邊移到右邊，總財富不變。雖然現實市場並非完美（存在稅負、交易成本、資訊不對稱），但這個基本洞見提供了分析配息策略的起點。

Arnott (2003) 的實證反思：Robert Arnott 在 Financial Analysts Journal 的研究指出，高殖利率股票在扣除再投資效果後，長期總報酬往往不如成長型股票。他的分析顯示，投資人之所以偏好高配息，主要是受到「鳥在手」謬誤（Bird-in-the-Hand Fallacy）影響——覺得拿到手的現金比帳面上的資本利得更「真實」。但在稅負不對稱的環境下（股息稅率通常高於長期資本利得稅率），這個偏好可能導致投資人選擇了稅後報酬較低的方案。

行為金融學觀點：Shefrin & Statman (1984) 提出「心理帳戶」理論，認為投資人傾向將配息視為「收入」而非「資本」，因此更容易消費配息而不願賣出持股。Black (1976) 更直接稱股利政策為「a puzzle」（一個謎），因為在存在稅負差異的世界中，配息對投資人通常是不利的——但投資人卻偏好它。這種認知偏誤在退休族群中尤為明顯，許多人堅持「只花利息不動本金」的心理框架，即使賣出等值的股票在經濟上完全等效。

台灣市場的特殊現象：台灣的 ETF 市場自 2003 年 0050 上市以來快速成長，高股息 ETF（如 0056, 00878, 00919）近年受到散戶投資人大量追捧，規模爆發性成長。台灣的退休金制度（勞退新制自提 6%）替代率偏低，促使民眾自行規劃退休投資——「靠股息過退休生活」因此成為最受歡迎的策略。然而，少有人深入探討高配息策略的稅務效率、總報酬代價，以及其在不同市場環境下的穩健性。

Dividend Irrelevance Theory: The Modigliani-Miller (1961) "dividend irrelevance" theorem posits that in perfect markets, a firm's dividend policy does not affect its value. Investors can create "homemade dividends" by selling shares, achieving the same effect as receiving dividends. The core logic: dividends merely move money from your left pocket to your right pocket, leaving total wealth unchanged. While real markets are imperfect (taxes, costs, information asymmetry), this insight provides the starting point for analyzing dividend strategies.

Arnott (2003) Empirical Reflection: Robert Arnott's research in the Financial Analysts Journal showed that high-yield stocks, after accounting for reinvestment, tend to underperform growth stocks in total return over the long run. He argued that investors' preference for high dividends is largely driven by the "Bird-in-the-Hand Fallacy" — feeling that cash in hand is more "real" than paper capital gains. But in tax-asymmetric environments (dividend tax rates typically exceed long-term capital gains rates), this preference may lead investors to choose lower after-tax return options.

Behavioral Finance Perspective: Shefrin & Statman (1984) proposed "mental accounting" theory, arguing that investors tend to treat dividends as "income" rather than "capital," making them more willing to spend dividends than sell holdings. Black (1976) more directly called dividend policy "a puzzle," because in a world with tax differences, dividends are typically disadvantageous to investors — yet investors prefer them. This cognitive bias is especially pronounced among retirees, many of whom insist on the "spend only income, never touch principal" mental framework, even though selling equivalent shares is economically identical.

Taiwan Market's Special Phenomenon: Taiwan's ETF market has grown rapidly since 0050's listing in 2003. High-dividend ETFs (such as 0056, 00878, 00919) have attracted massive retail investor interest in recent years, with explosive AUM growth. Taiwan's pension system (labor pension new scheme, 6% employer contribution) has a low replacement rate, pushing individuals to plan their own retirement investments — "living off dividends in retirement" thus became the most popular strategy. However, few have deeply examined the tax efficiency, total return costs, and robustness across different market regimes of high-dividend strategies.

1.3 研究假說1.3 Research Hypotheses

假說內容為什麼重要
H10056 的殖利率顯著高於 0050驗證高股息 ETF 的基本承諾是否成立——殖利率真的比較高嗎？
H2除息日 drop ratio = 1.0配息是否真的是「左手換右手」？短期看，配息等於股價下跌嗎？

H40050 賣股策略 >= 0056 領息策略核心問題：退休族到底該選哪種策略？

這四個假說形成一個完整的分析框架：H1 驗證前提（殖利率確實較高）、H2 檢驗機制（配息的經濟本質）、H3 比較恢復力（除息後表現）、H4 回答核心問題（退休適用性）。只有全面分析這四個層面，才能對「高股息 ETF 是退休首選」做出有依據的判斷。

These four hypotheses form a complete analytical framework: H1 verifies the premise (yield is indeed higher), H2 examines the mechanism (economic nature of dividends), H3 compares resilience (post-ex-dividend performance), H4 answers the core question (retirement suitability). Only by comprehensively analyzing all four dimensions can we make an evidence-based judgment on "high-dividend ETFs are best for retirement."
