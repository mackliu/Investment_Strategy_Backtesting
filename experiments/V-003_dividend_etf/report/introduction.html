緒論Introduction

1.1 研究背景1.1 Background

「退休後靠股息過生活」是台灣投資社群中最受歡迎的退休規劃策略之一。以 0056（元大高股息 ETF）為代表的高股息 ETF，因其穩定的配息紀錄和相對較高的殖利率，被許多投資達人推薦為退休族的首選投資工具。

然而，這個論點忽略了幾個關鍵問題：配息是否真的是「額外收入」？除息後股價下跌（「左手換右手」效應）的影響有多大？高殖利率是否意味著更高的總報酬？稅負對配息收入的影響如何？這些問題需要嚴謹的數據分析來回答。

本研究建立一套科學思考框架，以客觀數據檢視「高股息 ETF 是退休首選」的論點。我們的目的不是批評任何人，而是希望投資人能建立獨立思考的能力，不盲從任何投資論述。這個框架本身也可能在未來被修正，但重要的是思考過程本身。

"Living off dividends in retirement" is one of the most popular retirement strategies in Taiwan's investment community. High-dividend ETFs like 0056 (Yuanta High Dividend) are frequently recommended by financial influencers as the top choice for retirees, thanks to their stable dividend history and relatively high yields.

However, this argument overlooks several critical questions: Are dividends truly "extra income"? How significant is the ex-dividend price drop ("left hand to right hand" effect)? Does a higher yield mean higher total returns? How do taxes impact dividend income? These questions require rigorous data analysis to answer.

This study establishes a scientific thinking framework to objectively examine the claim that "high-dividend ETFs are the best choice for retirement." Our purpose is not to criticize anyone, but to help investors develop independent thinking abilities rather than blindly following any investment narrative. This framework itself may be revised in the future, but what matters is the thinking process itself.

1.2 文獻回顧1.2 Literature Review

配息無關性理論：Modigliani-Miller (1961) 的「股利無關論」主張，在完美市場中，公司的股利政策不影響其價值。投資人可以透過賣出股票自製「股息」（homemade dividends），效果與領取配息相同。

行為金融學觀點：Shefrin & Statman (1984) 提出「心理帳戶」理論，認為投資人傾向將配息視為「收入」而非「資本」，因此更容易消費配息而不願賣出持股。這種心理偏誤可能導致次最適的退休提領策略。

台灣研究：台灣的 ETF 市場自 2003 年 0050 上市以來快速成長，高股息 ETF（如 0056, 00878）近年受到散戶投資人大量追捧。然而，學術研究多指出高殖利率股票的總報酬通常低於成長型股票（Arnott & Asness, 2003）。

Dividend Irrelevance Theory: The Modigliani-Miller (1961) "dividend irrelevance" theorem posits that in perfect markets, a firm's dividend policy does not affect its value. Investors can create "homemade dividends" by selling shares, achieving the same effect as receiving dividends.

Behavioral Finance Perspective: Shefrin & Statman (1984) proposed "mental accounting" theory, arguing that investors tend to treat dividends as "income" rather than "capital," making them more willing to spend dividends than sell holdings. This behavioral bias may lead to suboptimal retirement withdrawal strategies.

Taiwan Research: Taiwan's ETF market has grown rapidly since 0050's listing in 2003. High-dividend ETFs (such as 0056, 00878) have attracted massive retail investor interest. However, academic research generally shows that high-yield stocks tend to have lower total returns than growth stocks (Arnott & Asness, 2003).

1.3 研究假說1.3 Research Hypotheses

H1: 0056 的殖利率顯著高於 0050（單尾配對 t 檢定）

H2: 除息日股價跌幅 / 配息金額比率 = 1.0（「左手換右手」檢驗）

H3: 0050 與 0056 的填息率無顯著差異

H4: 在退休模擬中，0050 賣股策略的總價值不遜於 0056 領息策略

H1: 0056 dividend yield is significantly higher than 0050 (one-tailed paired t-test)

H2: Ex-dividend day price drop / dividend ratio = 1.0 ("left hand to right hand" test)

H3: No significant difference in fill-gap rates between 0050 and 0056

H4: In retirement simulation, 0050 sell strategy total value is not inferior to 0056 dividend strategy
