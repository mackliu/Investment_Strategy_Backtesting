V-003：高股息 ETF 是退休首選？V-003: Are High-Dividend ETFs the Best Choice for Retirement?

討論Discussion

4.1 主要發現4.1 Key Findings

  1. 殖利率優勢確實存在：0056 殖利率顯著高於 0050（6.9% vs 3.4%），且每年皆然。但殖利率高不等於總報酬高。
  2. 配息確實是「左手換右手」：drop ratio 在統計上不顯著偏離 1.0，表示除息日的股價下跌幾乎等於配息金額。
  3. 0050 填息速度顯著更快：0050 中位數 5 天 vs 0056 34 天（p=0.003），反映市值型 ETF 的價格恢復力較強。
  4. 高股息 = 高股息貢獻：0056 的 64% 報酬來自配息，而 0050 僅 50%。這意味著 0056 的「成長」幾乎全靠配息，而非資本增值。
  5. 稅負拖累高配息：在高所得稅率下，0056 的稅後報酬劣勢更加明顯。
  1. Yield advantage is real: 0056's yield is significantly higher (6.9% vs 3.4%), holding in every year. But higher yield does not mean higher total return.
  2. Dividends are indeed 'left hand to right hand': Drop ratio is not significantly different from 1.0, meaning ex-dividend price drops nearly equal dividend amounts.
  3. 0050 fills gaps significantly faster: Median 5 days vs 0056's 34 days (p=0.003), reflecting stronger price recovery in market-cap ETFs.
  4. High dividend = high dividend contribution: 64% of 0056's return comes from dividends vs 50% for 0050. This means 0056's 'growth' comes almost entirely from dividends, not capital appreciation.
  5. Tax drag hurts high dividends: Under high income tax rates, 0056's after-tax return disadvantage becomes more pronounced.

4.2 重新理解「左手換右手」4.2 Rethinking "Left Hand to Right Hand"

本研究的初步分析確認了除息日 drop ratio ~= 1.0，即「除息當天是左手換右手」。但這個結論需要謹慎解讀。

短期機械效果 vs 長期經濟意義：除息日的股價下跌是機械性的市場調整機制，與「配息沒有價值」是完全不同的命題。就好比你從銀行領出存款，帳戶餘額減少不代表你的錢消失了。

長期數據的反證：如果配息真的是龐氏式的「左手換右手」（每次配息都在侵蝕本金），那麼 0056 在 17 年間累計配出 29.05 元/股（已超過初始股價 25.75 元的 113%）後，股價應該歸零。但事實上 0056 的股價從 25.75 元成長到 35.15 元，成長了 36.5%。

正確的理解：ETF 配的是「成分股企業的盈餘」而非「投資人的本金」。除息後淨值下降，但因為企業持續獲利，淨值會逐漸恢復並成長。ETF 的換股機制也在持續替換成分股，維持高殖利率的選股目標。

真正的左手換右手：投入 100 元，每月退還你 10 元，十個月後本金歸零，不再有任何收入——這才是龐氏結構。0056 的配息顯然不是這種模式。

Our initial analysis confirmed drop ratio ~= 1.0 on ex-dividend days — "dividends are left hand to right hand on the ex-date." But this conclusion requires careful interpretation.

Short-term mechanical effect vs long-term economic meaning: The ex-dividend price drop is a mechanical market adjustment mechanism, which is fundamentally different from "dividends have no value." It is like withdrawing cash from a bank — the balance decreases, but your money did not disappear.

Long-term data counterevidence: If dividends were truly Ponzi-style 'left hand to right hand' (eroding principal each time), 0056 should have reached zero after distributing 29.05 per share over 17 years (113% of initial price 25.75). Instead, 0056's price grew from 25.75 to 35.15, a 36.5% increase.

Correct understanding: ETF dividends come from underlying corporate earnings, not investor principal. After ex-dividend NAV drops, it recovers as companies continue to generate profits. The ETF rebalancing mechanism also continuously replaces constituents to maintain high-yield selection.

True "left hand to right hand": Invest 100, receive 10 back monthly, principal reaches zero in 10 months with no further income — that is a Ponzi structure. 0056's dividends clearly do not follow this pattern.

4.3 結構性斷點與制度依賴4.3 Structural Break and Regime Dependence

本研究最重要的發現之一是 0050 的報酬優勢高度依賴 2017 年後的科技股行情：

  • 2008-2016：0050 年化 5.21% vs 0056 年化 3.40%（差距 1.81%）
  • 2017-2025：0050 年化 18.41% vs 0056 年化 12.45%（差距 5.96%）

2017 年後台積電在 0050 的權重從約 20% 飆升至超過 50%，0050 實質上從「台灣大盤 ETF」變成了「半導體概念 ETF」。這使得 0050 在科技股上漲時表現優異，但也意味著：

  • 如果未來科技類股進入獲利穩定期或成長趨緩，0050 的優勢可能大幅縮小
  • 0056 的選股策略以「有實質盈餘可分配的企業」為主，在穩定市場中可能更具防禦性
  • 退休規劃的時間跨度通常是 20-30 年，不應假設目前的市場結構將永遠持續

外推偏誤警示：用 2017-2025 的科技股暴漲期數據推論「0050 永遠優於 0056」，犯了將特定市場制度外推至未來的邏輯錯誤。

One of the most important findings is that 0050's return advantage is highly dependent on post-2017 tech sector performance:

  • 2008-2016: 0050 annualized 5.21% vs 0056 3.40% (gap 1.81%)
  • 2017-2025: 0050 annualized 18.41% vs 0056 12.45% (gap 5.96%)

After 2017, TSMC's weight in 0050 surged from ~20% to over 50%, effectively transforming 0050 from a 'Taiwan broad market ETF' to a 'semiconductor sector ETF.' This means:

  • If tech stocks enter a mature, slower-growth phase, 0050's advantage may significantly narrow
  • 0056 selects companies with real distributable earnings, potentially more defensive in stable markets
  • Retirement planning spans 20-30 years; one should not assume current market structure persists forever

Extrapolation bias warning: Using 2017-2025 tech boom data to conclude "0050 always beats 0056" commits the logical error of extrapolating a specific market regime into the future.

4.4 注意事項4.4 Caveats

  • 樣本期間有限：0056 上市僅約 17 年，退休模擬的有效起始年份僅 8 組，統計效力有限。
  • 存活者偏差：0050 和 0056 都是存活至今的 ETF，不含已下市的標的。
  • 0056 結構性變化：0056 於 2020 年改變選股邏輯（納入 ESG 因子），2024 年改為季配息。歷史數據可能不完全反映目前的基金特性。
  • 簡化假設：退休模擬未考慮交易成本、稅務的再投入效果、通膨對提領金額的調整。
  • 歷史不等於未來：過去的報酬特徵不保證在未來重現。
  • 制度依賴：0050 vs 0056 的比較結果高度依賴分析期間的市場結構（2017 年後台積電權重急遽上升）。2008-2016 年兩者表現相近（0050 年化 5.2% vs 0056 3.4%），而 2017-2025 差距明顯擴大。用特定制度下的結論推論未來，需格外謹慎。
  • 「左手換右手」的邊界：除息日 drop ratio ~= 1.0 是機械性事實，但不應過度延伸為「配息完全沒價值」。長期數據顯示 ETF 配息來源是企業盈餘，而非本金侵蝕。
  • Limited sample period: 0056 has been listed for only ~17 years, with only 8 valid start years for retirement simulation, limiting statistical power.
  • Survivorship bias: Both 0050 and 0056 are ETFs that survived to present, excluding delisted funds.
  • 0056 structural changes: 0056 changed its stock selection logic in 2020 (incorporating ESG factors) and switched to quarterly dividends in 2024. Historical data may not fully reflect current fund characteristics.
  • Simplifying assumptions: Retirement simulation does not account for transaction costs, reinvestment of after-tax dividends, or inflation adjustment of withdrawal amounts.
  • Past is not future: Historical return characteristics are not guaranteed to repeat in the future.
  • Regime dependence: The 0050 vs 0056 comparison is highly dependent on the market structure of the analysis period (TSMC's weight surge after 2017). During 2008-2016 both performed similarly (0050 5.2% vs 0056 3.4% annualized), while the gap widened dramatically in 2017-2025. Extrapolating regime-specific conclusions to the future requires extreme caution.
  • "Left hand to right hand" boundaries: The ex-dividend drop ratio ~= 1.0 is a mechanical fact, but should not be over-extended to mean "dividends have no value at all." Long-term data shows ETF dividends come from corporate earnings, not principal erosion.

4.5 投資啟示4.5 Investment Implications

保守型投資人：如果重視穩定的現金流感受，0056 的定期配息確實能提供心理安慰，且在 2017 年前的穩定市場中，0056 領息策略實際上勝過 0050 賣股策略。0056 的配息來自企業盈餘，並非龐氏式的本金耗損。在低稅率（如退休族 5% 合併計稅）下，稅負影響有限。

積極型投資人：歷史全期間數據顯示 0050 的總報酬遠優於 0056，但此優勢主要集中在 2017 年後的科技股暴漲期。若不介意自行「賣股提領」，0050 策略在高稅率下尤其有利。但應認知此優勢具有制度依賴性，未來科技股若進入穩定期，0050 的超額報酬可能縮小。

核心認知：「高殖利率」和「高總報酬」是兩個不同的概念，但「配息是左手換右手」也需要更精確的理解。正確的框架是：除息日的股價調整是機械性的（短期 LH2RH），但長期來看，配息源自企業獲利而非本金侵蝕。投資選擇應同時考量總報酬目標、稅率結構、市場制度依賴性，以及個人對現金流的心理需求。

Conservative investors: If stable cash flow is psychologically important, 0056's regular dividends can provide comfort, and in the stable market of 2008-2016, 0056 dividend strategy actually outperformed 0050 sell strategy. 0056's dividends come from corporate earnings, not Ponzi-style principal erosion. Under low tax rates (e.g., 5% for retirees), the tax impact is limited.

Aggressive investors: Full-period historical data shows 0050's total return far exceeds 0056's, but this advantage is concentrated in the post-2017 tech boom. The 0050 sell strategy is particularly advantageous under high tax rates. However, this advantage is regime-dependent — if tech stocks enter a mature phase, 0050's excess return may narrow.

Key insight: "High yield" and "high total return" are different concepts, but "dividends are left hand to right hand" also requires more precise understanding. The correct framework: ex-dividend price adjustment is mechanical (short-term LH2RH), but long-term, dividends come from corporate profits, not principal erosion. Investment decisions should consider total return goals, tax structure, market regime dependence, and personal psychological need for cash flow.
