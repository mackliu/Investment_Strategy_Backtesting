結論Conclusion

5.1 核心結論5.1 Key Conclusions

本研究以信義房價指數（估計值）與 0050 ETF 進行全面比較，涵蓋約 22 年的數據，從持有成本、槓桿效應、風險特徵、租買比較等多個面向，系統性地檢驗「買房穩賺不賠」的信仰。核心結論如下：

This study comprehensively compared the Sinyi House Price Index (estimated) with the 0050 ETF over ~22 years, systematically examining the "real estate always wins" belief across holding costs, leverage, risk, and rent-vs-buy dimensions:

  1. 房地產名目報酬看似不錯，但淨報酬遠不如想像。全國房價名目年化 6.6%，但扣除每年 1.5% 的持有成本後，淨報酬僅 5.1%，低於 0050 的 11.3%。持有成本是最常被忽略、卻影響最深遠的因素。
  2. 「買房穩賺不賠」主要來自槓桿效應。房貸提供的 3.3x 槓桿將報酬放大至 17.4%——但這不是房地產「本身」優秀，而是「借錢」的效果。槓桿同時放大風險，Sharpe ratio 並未顯著改善。
  3. 房價波動度低於股票，但流動性風險被嚴重忽略。房價季度波動 3.5% vs 股票 18.6%，表面上房地產更「穩定」。但季度數據平滑了真實波動，且房地產難以迅速變現——急需用錢時可能被迫大幅折價出售。
  4. 租 vs 買取決於多重因素，非單純對錯。10 年期買房勝率 55%，20 年期 44%。買房在長期持有時有一定優勢，但優勢大小高度依賴房價漲幅、利率水準和進場時點。
  5. 持有成本對結論至關重要。敏感度分析顯示，在所有合理的持有成本假設（1%~2.5%）下，房地產淨報酬都低於 0050，結論相當穩健。
  6. Nominal returns look decent but net returns disappoint. National HPI nominal 6.6%, net after 1.5% annual costs: only 5.1%, vs 0050 at 11.3%. Holding costs are the most overlooked yet impactful factor.
  7. "Always wins" is mainly leverage. 3.3x mortgage leverage amplifies to 17.4% — not intrinsic performance but borrowing effect. Sharpe ratio does not significantly improve.
  8. Low volatility masks illiquidity risk. Housing 3.5% vs stocks 18.6%, but quarterly data smoothing and illiquidity underestimate true risk.
  9. Rent vs buy depends on multiple factors. 10-year buy win rate: 55%, 20-year: 44%. Buying has some long-term advantage, but magnitude depends heavily on price growth, rates, and timing.
  10. Holding costs are crucial. Sensitivity analysis shows net housing returns are below 0050 under all reasonable cost assumptions (1%-2.5%).

一句話總結：「買房穩賺不賠」是一個被持有成本和槓桿效應共同包裝出來的幻覺。房地產不是壞的投資，但它也遠非坊間所說的「穩賺不賠」。投資人應該以更全面的視角——計入所有成本、拆解槓桿、考量流動性——來評估房地產的真實報酬。

One-line summary: "Real estate always wins" is an illusion packaged by holding costs and leverage effects. Housing is not a bad investment, but it is far from the "guaranteed win" popular wisdom claims. Investors should evaluate with a comprehensive lens — accounting for all costs, decomposing leverage, and considering liquidity.

5.2 研究限制總結5.2 Summary of Limitations

本研究受限於以下因素，讀者在引用結論時應注意：

  • 信義房價指數為估計值，非官方精確數值，可能與實際有偏差
  • 房價指數僅反映「市場平均」，個別物件報酬差異極大（地段、屋況、運氣）
  • 未考慮房屋折舊——老屋維修成本遞增、價值遞減的影響
  • 租金假設簡化為固定報酬率，實際隨地區和景氣大幅波動
  • 未計入「居住效用」——自住房提供的安全感、穩定性等非財務價值
  • 0050 含息報酬 vs 不含租金的房價指數，比較基準不完全對等
  • 過去 22 年的低利率環境不可複製，未來報酬結構可能不同

This study is subject to the following limitations:

  • Sinyi HPI is estimated, not official — may deviate from actual values
  • Index reflects market averages; individual property returns vary enormously
  • No building depreciation — increasing maintenance costs and declining value of aging buildings
  • Simplified rent assumptions vs actual variation by location and economic cycle
  • No shelter utility — security, stability, and other non-financial benefits of ownership
  • 0050 with dividends vs HPI without rent — not perfectly comparable benchmarks
  • Past 22 years of low rates are unreplicable; future return structures may differ

5.3 未來研究方向5.3 Future Research

  • 取得官方數據：使用官方授權的信義房價指數精確數值進行驗證，確認估計值的偏差程度。
  • 區域細分：將分析拆解為六都（台北、新北、桃園、台中、台南、高雄），檢驗區域差異對結論的影響——直覺上台北的報酬應優於全國平均。
  • 納入租金收入：為投資用房產加入租金收益，計算「總報酬」（資本利得 + 租金），與 0050 的總報酬（價格 + 股息）進行更公平的比較。
  • REITs 比較：比較直接持有房地產 vs 投資 REITs（不動產投資信託）的報酬與風險差異，探討 REITs 是否為更有效率的房地產投資方式。
  • 擇時效應：分析不同房市週期階段（谷底、復甦、擴張、高峰）進場對報酬的影響，檢驗「房市也有擇時」的可能性。
  • 人口結構影響：納入少子化、高齡化趨勢的量化模型，預測未來 10-20 年人口結構改變對房價的潛在衝擊。
  • Official data: Verify with officially licensed Sinyi HPI values to confirm estimation accuracy.
  • Regional breakdown: Analyze the six major cities separately — Taipei's returns should intuitively exceed the national average.
  • Include rental income: Add rental yields for investment properties to compute total return (capital gains + rent) for fairer comparison with 0050 total return (price + dividends).
  • REITs comparison: Compare direct property ownership vs REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts) to evaluate whether REITs offer more efficient real estate exposure.
  • Timing effects: Analyze how entry timing across housing cycles (trough, recovery, expansion, peak) impacts returns.
  • Demographics: Incorporate quantitative models of declining birth rates and aging population to project housing price impacts over the next 10-20 years.
