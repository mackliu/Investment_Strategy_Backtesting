數據品質說明：本研究最大的數據挑戰在於信義房價指數。由於官方精確數據需付費取得，我們根據公開報導、新聞資料與政府統計進行估算。這個估算值可能與實際數值有所偏差，但整體趨勢（2003-2014 大漲、2015-2018 盤整、2019 後再漲）與公開報導一致。就像用溫度計測量水溫，即使溫度計有正負 1 度的誤差，你仍然能判斷水是熱的還是冷的。

Data quality notes: The biggest data challenge is the Sinyi HPI. Since official precise data requires a subscription, we estimate from public reports, news, and government statistics. Estimates may deviate from actual values, but the overall trend (2003-2014 surge, 2015-2018 consolidation, post-2019 rise) matches public reporting. Like using a thermometer with +/-1 degree error — you can still tell if water is hot or cold.

0050 ETF uses the project's unified reference data (dividend-adjusted prices), cross-validated against FinMind. CPI data from DGBAS (Directorate-General of Budget, Accounting and Statistics) is among the most reliable official statistics. Mortgage rates use central bank historical averages, though individual rates vary by credit conditions.

注意：信義房價指數為根據公開資料估算值，非官方精確數值。本研究結果僅供參考，不應視為精確計算。如需精確分析，建議取得官方授權數據。此外，房價指數反映的是「市場平均」，個別物件的報酬可能與平均值相差甚遠——就像平均身高 170 公分不代表每個人都是 170，買到好地段的人報酬可能翻倍，買到偏遠地區的人可能虧損。

Note: Sinyi HPI values are estimates based on public data, not official figures. Results are for reference only. For precise analysis, obtain officially licensed data. Additionally, the index reflects "market average" — individual property returns may vary dramatically, just as an average height of 170cm does not mean everyone is 170cm.

2.2 持有成本模型2.2 Holding Cost Model

為什麼持有成本如此重要？多數人買房時只考慮房價和房貸，卻忽略了每年持續發生的固定支出。這就像買了一輛車，只看車價而不考慮油錢、保險、停車費、維修費——長期下來，這些「小」支出可能累積到驚人的金額。

我們的持有成本模型包含以下項目：

  • 房屋稅+地價稅：約 0.2%/年。這是無論如何都要繳的，逃不掉。自用住宅雖有優惠稅率，但仍是每年的固定支出。
  • 管理費+維修費：約 0.5%/年。新大樓每月管理費可能 3,000-8,000 元不等；老公寓雖沒管理費，但水電管線、屋頂防水、外牆拉皮等維修支出更加不可預測。屋齡越高，維修費越驚人。
  • 保險費：約 0.05%/年。在台灣相對便宜，但地震頻繁的環境下，住宅火災+地震保險是基本保障。

基準合計 1.5%/年。為了測試結論的穩健性，我們進行了從 1% 到 2.5% 的敏感度分析。

交易成本是另一個經常被低估的因素。買入約需 1.6%（仲介費 1-2% + 契稅 6% x 房屋評定現值比例 + 印花稅 0.1% + 代書費等），賣出約需 10%（仲介費 2-4% + 土增稅 + 房地合一稅最高 45%）。一買一賣的總交易成本可能高達 10-15%，而 ETF 買賣合計不到 0.5%。這意味著房地產要漲 10-15% 才能打平交易成本，而 ETF 只需漲 0.5%。

Why are holding costs so important? Most buyers only consider purchase price and mortgage, ignoring ongoing annual expenses. Like buying a car and only looking at the sticker price while ignoring gas, insurance, parking, and maintenance — these "small" costs accumulate to staggering amounts.

Our holding cost model includes:

  • Property + land tax: ~0.2%/year. Unavoidable annual fixed expense.
  • Management + maintenance: ~0.5%/year. New buildings charge NTD 3,000-8,000/month in HOA fees; older buildings save on HOA but face unpredictable plumbing, roof, and facade repairs.
  • Insurance: ~0.05%/year. Relatively cheap in Taiwan, but fire+earthquake coverage is essential given seismic activity.

Baseline total: 1.5%/year. Sensitivity analysis ranges from 1% to 2.5%.

Transaction costs are another frequently underestimated factor. ~1.6% to buy, ~10% to sell. Round-trip costs of 10-15% vs ETF's under 0.5%. Housing must appreciate 10-15% just to break even on transaction costs, while ETFs need only 0.5%.

2.3 槓桿模型2.3 Leverage Model

為什麼要特別分析槓桿？因為這是房地產投資最容易被誤解的部分。當一個人用 30% 頭期款買房，等於用了 3.3 倍槓桿。舉例來說：如果你用 200 萬頭期款買了 1000 萬的房子，房價漲 10%（100 萬），你的投資報酬率是 100 萬 / 200 萬 = 50%，而非 10%。聽起來很美好對吧？但反過來想：如果房價跌 10%，你的損失也是 50%，而非 10%。更可怕的是，如果房價跌 20%，你的 200 萬頭期款就全部蒸發了（100% 損失）。

我們的槓桿模型模擬房貸期限 30 年，利率採歷史資料。槓桿報酬計算公式為：R_lev = L x R_asset - (L-1) x R_borrow，其中 L 為槓桿倍數（3.3），R_asset 為房價報酬率，R_borrow 為房貸利率。簡單來說，你的實際報酬 = 放大後的房價報酬 - 借錢的利息成本。

Why analyze leverage separately? Because it is the most misunderstood aspect of real estate investing. With a 30% down payment, you are using 3.3x leverage. Example: NTD 2M down payment on a 10M house. If price rises 10% (1M gain), your return is 1M/2M = 50%, not 10%. Sounds great — but if price drops 10%, your loss is also 50%. If it drops 20%, your entire down payment evaporates (100% loss).

Our model simulates a 30-year mortgage with historical rates. Leveraged return formula: R_lev = L x R_asset - (L-1) x R_borrow, where L is leverage ratio (3.3). Simply: your actual return = amplified housing return minus borrowing cost.

2.4 租 vs 買模型2.4 Rent vs Buy Model

模擬設計理念：「應該買房還是租房？」是許多人最糾結的財務決策。我們設計了一個公平的比較框架：兩個相同收入的人，一個選擇買房，另一個選擇租房並將省下的錢投資。經過若干年後，比較兩人的總資產。

買方假設：房價 1500 萬元，頭期款 30%（450 萬元），其餘貸款 30 年房貸。買方每季支付房貸本息 + 持有成本（稅費、管理費、維修費）。賣出時房屋價值 = 當初房價 x 房價指數成長率。

租方假設：租金報酬率 1.8%（年租金/房價），租金年成長率 1.5%。租方將頭期款一次投入 0050 ETF，每季再將「房貸月繳 - 租金」的差額投入 0050。如果某一季租金高於房貸月繳，則不額外投入。

模擬持有期間為 5, 10, 15, 20 年，每個起始季度都進行一次模擬（滾動窗口），計算買方勝出的比例。這種方法就像在日曆上每個月都畫一個記號，看看從這個月開始買房，十年後是不是真的比租房划算。

Simulation design: "Should I buy or rent?" is one of the most agonizing financial decisions. We designed a fair comparison: two people with identical income — one buys, the other rents and invests the savings. After N years, compare total wealth.

Buyer assumptions: NTD 15000K home, 30% down payment (NTD 4500K), 30-year mortgage. Buyer pays quarterly mortgage + holding costs.

Renter assumptions: Rental yield 1.8%, rent growth 1.5%/year. Renter invests the down payment lump-sum in 0050 and quarterly invests the "mortgage minus rent" difference.

Simulated periods: 5, 10, 15, 20 years. Each starting quarter runs a rolling window simulation, computing the buy-win rate.

2.5 統計方法2.5 Statistical Methods

為了確保結論的可信度，我們採用了多種統計方法，每種方法針對不同的問題：

  • Block Bootstrap（區塊自助法）：處理季度數據的自相關問題。一般的統計檢定假設每筆觀測值彼此獨立，但房價連續幾季的漲跌往往相關（漲的時候連續漲、跌的時候連續跌）。Block Bootstrap 以 4 季為一個「區塊」重抽 10,000 次，保留了季度之間的相關性，讓信賴區間的估計更加準確。就像問卷調查不能只問同一個家庭的成員（他們的意見不獨立），要整個家庭為單位抽樣。
  • 效果量（Cohen's d）：除了 p 值之外，我們同時報告效果量和 95% 信賴區間。p 值只告訴你差異是否「統計上顯著」，但不告訴你差異有多大。效果量則直接衡量差異的實際大小——就像醫生不只說「你有發燒」（p 值），還告訴你「燒到 39.5 度」（效果量）。
  • 敏感度分析：在不同持有成本假設（1%~2.5%/年）下重新計算所有結果，檢驗結論是否因假設改變而翻轉。如果只有在極端假設下結論才改變，代表結論是穩健的。
  • 二項檢定：租 vs 買的勝率是否顯著偏離 50%（純隨機）？如果買房在 80% 的滾動窗口中勝出，二項檢定可以告訴你這個 80% 是否真的代表買房較優，還是可能只是運氣好。

To ensure credible conclusions, we employ multiple statistical methods, each addressing different questions:

  • Block Bootstrap: Handles quarterly autocorrelation. Standard tests assume independent observations, but housing prices in consecutive quarters are correlated (trends persist). Block Bootstrap resamples 10,000 times in 4-quarter blocks, preserving inter-quarter correlation. Like surveys that sample entire families as units rather than individual members (whose opinions are not independent).
  • Effect sizes (Cohen's d): Beyond p-values, we report effect sizes and 95% CIs. P-values only tell you if a difference is "statistically significant," not how large it is. Effect size directly measures practical magnitude — like a doctor not just saying "you have a fever" (p-value) but "it is 39.5 degrees" (effect size).
  • Sensitivity analysis: Recalculates all results under different holding cost assumptions (1%-2.5%/year), testing whether conclusions flip under changed assumptions. Robust conclusions hold across a wide range.
  • Binomial test: Is the buy-vs-rent win rate significantly different from 50% (random)? If buying wins in 80% of rolling windows, this test determines whether 80% truly indicates superiority or could be luck.
