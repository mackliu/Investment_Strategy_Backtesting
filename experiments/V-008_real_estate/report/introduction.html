緒論Introduction

1.1 背景1.1 Background

「有土斯有財」、「買房穩賺不賠」是台灣社會根深蒂固的觀念。在家庭聚餐時，長輩最常問的問題之一就是「買房了沒？」——彷彿不買房就是理財失敗。許多人視房地產為最安全、最穩健的投資工具，認為房價只會漲不會跌。這個觀念推動了台灣的高自有住宅率（約 85%），也讓許多年輕人將「存頭期款」視為人生第一優先的財務目標。

但這個觀念就像一個從不翻面的煎餅——你只看到金黃酥脆的那面，卻忽略了可能已經焦黑的另一面。具體來說，它存在幾個重大盲點：(1) 只看房價漲幅，忽略持有成本（房屋稅、地價稅、管理費、修繕費、保險費）——就像只看餐廳營業額，不看水電房租人事成本；(2) 將「槓桿帶來的報酬」誤認為「房地產本身的報酬」——買房通常只付 20-30% 頭期款，等於用 3-5 倍槓桿投資，如果用相同槓桿買股票，報酬可能更驚人（當然風險也更大）；(3) 忽視流動性風險——房地產無法像股票一樣按一個按鈕就賣掉，急需用錢時可能被迫賤賣；(4) 倖存者偏差——人們只記得「我同事十年前買在新竹，現在翻倍」的成功案例，忘了日本 1990 年後房價腰斬、至今仍未完全恢復的慘痛教訓。

台灣近二十年的房價上漲（尤其雙北地區），讓「買房必勝」的信仰更加根深蒂固。但在全球低利率環境可能終結、人口結構改變（少子化、高齡化）、以及居住正義議題日益受關注的今天，重新用數據檢視這個信仰，顯得格外重要。本研究的目標不是要說「買房一定不好」或「租房一定好」，而是讓你在做出人生最大的財務決策之前，先看清楚完整的數據全貌。

"Owning land is owning wealth" is a deeply ingrained belief in Taiwan. At family dinners, the most common question from elders is "Have you bought a house yet?" — as if not buying one equals financial failure. Many view real estate as the safest investment, believing prices can only go up. This drives Taiwan's high homeownership rate (~85%) and makes saving for a down payment the top financial priority for young people.

But this belief is like a pancake you never flip — you only see the golden, crispy side while ignoring the potentially burnt underside. It has critical blind spots: (1) looking only at price appreciation while ignoring holding costs (property tax, land tax, management fees, maintenance, insurance) — like looking only at restaurant revenue without considering rent, utilities, and labor; (2) mistaking leveraged returns for intrinsic real estate returns — most buyers put only 20-30% down, effectively investing at 3-5x leverage; (3) ignoring illiquidity risk — you cannot sell a house with a button click like stocks; (4) survivorship bias — people remember "my colleague bought in Hsinchu 10 years ago and doubled," forgetting Japan's post-1990 housing crash that still hasn't fully recovered.

Twenty years of rising prices in Taiwan (especially in Taipei) have only deepened the "buy and win" belief. But with potentially rising interest rates, demographic shifts (declining birth rates, aging population), and growing housing justice concerns, it is especially important to re-examine this belief with data. Our goal is not to say "buying is always bad" or "renting is always better," but to show you the complete data picture before making the largest financial decision of your life.

1.2 文獻回顧1.2 Literature Review

Jordà et al. (2019) 的跨國長期研究《The Rate of Return on Everything, 1870-2015》是房地產報酬研究的里程碑。他們分析了 16 個已開發國家近 150 年的數據，發現住宅不動產的長期實質報酬（含租金）約 7.1%，與股票相當（7.3%），但波動度較低。這個發現令人驚訝——但關鍵在於「含租金」三個字。如果只看房價漲幅（資本利得），報酬率只有約 2-3%，和通膨差不多。用生活化的比喻來說：房地產就像一隻母雞，你買牠不只是為了等牠長大賣掉（房價漲幅），更重要的是每天下的蛋（租金收入）。如果你是「自住不收租」，那你只得到母雞長大的部分。

Shiller (2015) 在其經典著作《Irrational Exuberance》中提出警告：美國 1890-1990 年間房價的實質漲幅（扣除通膨後）幾乎為零。人們之所以覺得「房價一直漲」，很大程度上是通膨造成的貨幣幻覺——名目價格上升了，但購買力並沒有增加。這就像你的薪水每年漲 3%，但物價也漲 3%，實際上你並沒有變得更富有。

Himmelberg, Mayer & Sinai (2005) 提出「使用者成本模型（User Cost Model）」，認為房屋的持有成本（房屋稅、維修、折舊、機會成本、減去房租節省）是判斷房價是否合理的關鍵指標。若每年持有成本高達房價的 5-9%，則房產的投資吸引力大幅降低。本研究採用類似框架，但簡化了部分假設。

Campbell & Cocco (2003) 指出，房貸槓桿是多數家庭唯一使用的金融槓桿。他們發現槓桿在房價上漲時可放大報酬，但在下跌時也會導致負資產（underwater mortgage）——也就是房子的市值低於貸款餘額。這種情況在 2008 年美國次貸危機中大規模發生，數百萬家庭因此破產。

彭建文 (2016) 等台灣學者研究發現，台灣房地產的實質報酬率在不同區域差異極大：台北市遠高於其他地區，而部分偏鄉地區甚至出現實質負報酬。這提醒我們不能用「全國平均」來代表所有地區的經驗。此外，Tsai (2019) 研究台灣房市週期特性，發現台灣房價具有均值回歸傾向，但回歸速度極慢，平均一個房市週期長達 10-15 年，這意味著短期買賣房地產的風險遠比想像中大。

Beracha & Johnson (2012) 直接比較了「買房自住」與「租房+將差額投資股市」的長期結果。他們發現在多數時期，後者（租房+投資）的財富累積優於前者，尤其在房價相對於租金過高的時期。這個研究的框架與本研究的「租 vs 買」模擬高度相似。

Glaeser & Gyourko (2018) 從供給面分析房價，指出土地供給限制是房價長期上漲的主因之一。台灣的高人口密度和有限的可建築土地，確實支撐了部分地區的房價。然而，人口結構的轉變（預計 2025 年後總人口開始減少）可能在長期削弱這個支撐力道。

Jorda et al. (2019), in their landmark study "The Rate of Return on Everything, 1870-2015," analyzed 16 developed countries over 150 years. They found residential real estate has ~7.1% long-run real return (including rent), comparable to stocks (7.3%) with lower volatility. The key is "including rent" — capital gains alone are only ~2-3%, barely above inflation. Think of real estate like a hen: you buy it not just to sell when it grows (price gain), but for the eggs it lays daily (rental income). If you live in it (no rental income), you only get the growth part.

Shiller (2015), in "Irrational Exuberance," warns that US real home prices (inflation-adjusted) barely rose from 1890-1990. The perception of "prices always going up" is largely money illusion from inflation. Like a 3% annual raise when prices also rise 3% — you are not actually wealthier.

Himmelberg, Mayer & Sinai (2005) proposed the "User Cost Model," arguing that ownership costs (taxes, maintenance, depreciation, opportunity cost, minus rent savings) are key to evaluating housing affordability. With annual costs of 5-9% of home value, investment attractiveness drops significantly.

Campbell & Cocco (2003) note that mortgage leverage is the only financial leverage most households use, amplifying both gains and losses. Underwater mortgages (home worth less than loan balance) occurred massively during the 2008 US subprime crisis, bankrupting millions of families.

Peng (2016) and other Taiwan scholars found dramatic regional variation in real estate returns: Taipei far outperforms other areas, while some rural areas show negative real returns. Tsai (2019) found Taiwan housing prices exhibit mean reversion, but with extremely slow adjustment — average housing cycles of 10-15 years.

Beracha & Johnson (2012) directly compared "buy and live" vs "rent and invest the difference in stocks." They found renting+investing produced superior wealth accumulation in most periods, especially when home prices were high relative to rents — a framework closely aligned with our study.

Glaeser & Gyourko (2018) analyzed housing supply constraints, noting that limited land supply drives long-run price appreciation. Taiwan's high density supports some regional prices, but demographic shifts (population likely declining after 2025) may weaken this support long-term.

1.3 研究問題與假說1.3 Research Questions & Hypotheses

編號 研究問題 假說
Q1 房地產淨報酬（扣除持有成本）是否優於 0050？ H1: 房地產淨報酬低於 0050
Q2 槓桿是否改善風險調整報酬？ H2: 槓桿增加報酬但 Sharpe ratio 未必改善
Q3 持有成本假設對結論的敏感度？ H3: 持有成本超過 1% 時，房地產淨報酬低於股票
Q4 買房 vs 租房+投資，多長時間買房較划算？ H4: 持有 15 年以上，買房才可能較划算
# Research Question Hypothesis
Q1 Is net real estate return (after costs) better than 0050? H1: Net housing return is lower than 0050
Q2 Does leverage improve risk-adjusted return? H2: Leverage increases return but may not improve Sharpe
Q3 How sensitive are conclusions to holding cost assumptions? H3: Above 1% holding cost, net housing return < stock return
Q4 Buy vs rent+invest: how long until buying is better? H4: Buying may be better only after 15+ years

H1 的邏輯：文獻顯示房價資本利得的實質報酬僅 2-3%（Shiller 2015），加上台灣持有成本約 1.5%/年，淨報酬可能不到 2%。相比之下，0050 歷史年化報酬約 8-10%。

H2 的邏輯：槓桿會等比例放大報酬和風險。如果未槓桿的房地產 Sharpe ratio 不高，加槓桿後 Sharpe ratio 理論上不變（Modigliani-Miller 定理的精神）。但實務上，因為房貸利率低於一般借款利率，槓桿可能在房價上漲期間提供額外優勢。

H3 的邏輯：持有成本是「確定的損失」，而房價漲幅是「不確定的收益」。就像一個漏水的桶子，不管水龍頭開多大（房價漲幅），桶子漏水的速度（持有成本）決定了最終能裝多少水。

H4 的邏輯：買房的前期交易成本很高（約 5-7%），需要足夠的持有時間來攤薄。此外，房貸的前幾年幾乎都在還利息，本金還得很少。因此短期買賣幾乎一定不划算。

H1 logic: Literature shows real capital gains of only 2-3% (Shiller 2015). With ~1.5%/year holding costs, net return may be under 2%, vs 0050 historical ~8-10%.

H2 logic: Leverage amplifies both returns and risk proportionally. If unleveraged Sharpe is low, leveraged Sharpe theoretically stays the same (per Modigliani-Miller). In practice, cheap mortgage rates may provide extra advantage during rising prices.

H3 logic: Holding costs are "certain losses" while price gains are "uncertain gains." Like a leaky bucket — regardless of how fast the faucet flows (price gains), the leak rate (holding costs) determines how much water stays.

H4 logic: High upfront transaction costs (~5-7%) require sufficient holding time to amortize. Early mortgage payments are almost entirely interest, with little principal reduction. Short-term buying almost never pays.

1.4 研究貢獻1.4 Research Contribution

本研究的主要貢獻在於：(1) 首次以完整持有成本模型比較台灣房地產與股市報酬——多數坊間比較只看名目房價漲幅，本研究扣除了稅費、管理費、維修費等所有持有成本；(2) 拆解槓桿效應——清楚區分「房地產本身的報酬」和「槓桿放大的報酬」，讓讀者理解房貸在報酬中扮演的角色；(3) 提供「租 vs 買」的量化框架——在不同假設情境下模擬長期財務結果，而非依賴直覺或單一案例；(4) 敏感度分析——測試不同持有成本假設下結論的穩健性，讓讀者可以代入自己的實際成本做判斷。

Key contributions: (1) First comprehensive holding cost comparison of Taiwan housing vs stocks — most popular comparisons only look at nominal price gains; (2) Decomposes leverage effects — clearly separating intrinsic returns from leverage-amplified returns; (3) Quantitative rent-vs-buy framework — simulating long-term outcomes under various assumptions rather than relying on intuition; (4) Sensitivity analysis — testing robustness across different holding cost assumptions.
