1. 緒論1. Introduction

1.1 研究背景1.1 Background

「只要持有 0050/0056 五到十年，一定賺錢」是台灣投資社群中最常見的投資論述之一。在 YouTube、PTT、社群媒體上，這個觀點被廣泛傳播，鼓勵新手投入股市。這句話表面上有歷史數據支撐，但「一定」二字卻隱含了對風險的忽視。

這類過度簡化的論述存在兩個潛在危險：首先，它可能導致投資人低估短期波動的衝擊——若投資人在 2008 年高點進場後面臨 50% 回撤，即使最終獲利，中途的心理壓力是否能承受？其次，「一定」的措辭暗示零風險，這與投資的本質相悖。

因此，我們需要以科學方法嚴謹檢驗這個論點：不是僅看幾個成功案例，而是系統性地計算每一個歷史進場點的實際報酬，並進行統計檢定來量化「幾乎一定」和「真的一定」之間的差距。

"Just hold 0050/0056 for 5-10 years and you'll definitely make money" is one of the most common investment claims in Taiwan's social media. Across YouTube, PTT forums, and social platforms, countless financial influencers use this argument to encourage newcomers to invest. While the claim has some historical basis, the word "definitely" glosses over the reality of risk.

Such oversimplified claims carry two potential dangers: First, they may lead investors to underestimate the impact of short-term volatility — if an investor entered at the 2008 peak and faced a 50% drawdown, could they withstand the psychological pressure even if the investment eventually recovered? Second, the "guaranteed" framing implies zero risk, which contradicts the fundamental nature of investing.

This is why we need rigorous scientific testing: rather than cherry-picking a few success stories, we systematically compute actual returns for every historical entry point and apply statistical tests to quantify the gap between "almost certainly" and "truly certain."

1.2 文獻回顧1.2 Literature Review

長期持有股票的學術研究可追溯至 Jeremy Siegel 的經典著作《Stocks for the Long Run》（1994，至今已出六版）。Siegel 分析美國股市超過 200 年的數據，發現股票在 20 年以上的持有期間幾乎總是優於債券和通膨。然而，該研究的結論主要基於美國市場——一個在 20 世紀經歷了前所未有成功的經濟體，存在所謂的「美國例外論」(U.S. exceptionalism) 偏誤。

針對台灣市場，本土研究相對有限。台灣股市於 1962 年開市，但早期資料品質不佳；ETF 產品更是 2003 年才上市。台灣相關的長期投資研究多聚焦於個股或加權指數，對 ETF 的專門分析較少。本研究使用完整的 ETF 還原價格數據，有助填補此空白。

學術界也指出長期持有分析的方法論陷阱：Boudoukh et al. (2008) 警告重疊報酬率（overlapping returns）會嚴重膨脹 t 統計量，導致偽顯著。為此，本研究採用 Newey-West HAC 校正與區塊拔靴法來處理此問題。

Academic research on long-term equity holding traces back to Jeremy Siegel's classic Stocks for the Long Run (1994, now in its 6th edition). Siegel analyzed over 200 years of U.S. market data, finding that stocks almost always outperformed bonds and inflation over 20+ year periods. However, his conclusions are primarily based on the U.S. market — an economy that experienced unprecedented success in the 20th century, subject to so-called "U.S. exceptionalism" bias.

For Taiwan's market, local research is relatively limited. The Taiwan Stock Exchange opened in 1962, but early data quality is poor; ETF products only launched in 2003. Most Taiwan-focused long-term investment studies examine individual stocks or the TAIEX index, with few dedicated ETF analyses. This study uses complete dividend-adjusted ETF price data to help fill this gap.

The academic literature also highlights methodological pitfalls in long-term holding analysis: Boudoukh et al. (2008) warned that overlapping returns severely inflate t-statistics, leading to spurious significance. To address this, our study employs Newey-West HAC correction and block bootstrap methods.

我們採用三層階層式檢定架構，由寬鬆到嚴格逐步提高標準：第一層問的是最基本的問題——投資人是否能賺到正報酬（名目值）；第二層扣除通膨後，投資是否仍能保值增值；第三層則與替代方案（銀行定存）比較，檢驗 ETF 是否提供了超額報酬。只有三層全部通過，才能較有信心地說「長期持有 ETF 確實值得」。

We employ a three-layer hierarchical testing framework that progressively raises the bar: Layer 1 asks the most basic question — can investors earn a positive return (nominal)? Layer 2 tests whether returns still preserve and grow purchasing power after inflation. Layer 3 compares against the alternative (bank deposits), testing whether ETFs provide excess returns. Only if all three layers pass can we confidently say that long-term ETF holding is worthwhile.

1.4 研究貢獻1.4 Research Contribution

本研究與現有文獻的差異在於：(1) 使用完整的還原股價而非僅看價格報酬，正確反映股息再投入效果；(2) 採用 Newey-West HAC 校正處理重疊報酬的自相關問題，而非使用可能低估標準誤的傳統 t 檢定；(3) 以環形區塊拔靴法提供不依賴常態假設的信賴區間；(4) 同時分析市值型（0050）與高股息型（0056）兩檔 ETF，比較不同投資風格的長期表現。

This study differs from existing literature in several ways: (1) it uses fully dividend-adjusted prices rather than price-only returns, correctly reflecting the impact of dividend reinvestment; (2) it employs Newey-West HAC correction to handle autocorrelation in overlapping returns, instead of traditional t-tests that may underestimate standard errors; (3) it provides distribution-free confidence intervals via circular block bootstrap; and (4) it simultaneously analyzes both a market-cap ETF (0050) and a dividend-focused ETF (0056), comparing long-term performance across different investment styles.
