3.3 累積報酬3.3 Cumulative Returns

累積報酬圖呈現「價格報酬」（不含息）與「含息總報酬」的巨大差距。0050 的含息報酬終值約為價格報酬的 8.2 倍，說明股息再投入是長期報酬的重要來源——投資人若只看價格走勢，將嚴重低估實際報酬。圖中可見 2008 年金融海嘯（最深回撤）、2011 年歐債危機、2015 年中國股災、2018 年中美貿易戰、2020 年 COVID-19 等重大事件的衝擊，但每次跌幅最終都被後續上漲所覆蓋。

The cumulative return chart reveals the dramatic gap between price-only returns and total returns (with dividends). 0050's total return ending value is approximately 8.2x its price-only return, demonstrating that dividend reinvestment is a major component of long-term returns — investors who only watch price movements will significantly underestimate actual performance. The chart shows impacts from the 2008 financial crisis (deepest drawdown), 2011 European debt crisis, 2015 China stock crash, 2018 US-China trade war, and 2020 COVID-19, but each decline was eventually recovered.

Positive-return years outnumber negative ones for both ETFs, supporting the bullish long-term case, but the existence of negative years reminds investors that short-term risk cannot be ignored.

3.5 回撤分析3.5 Drawdown Analysis

回撤圖是理解長期持有「心理代價」的關鍵。即使長期報酬為正，持有過程中投資人可能面臨超過 40% 的帳面虧損。主要回撤事件包括：2008 年金融海嘯（0050 最大回撤逾 50%）、2020 年 COVID-19（約 30%，但恢復迅速）、2022 年全球升息（約 25-30%）。回撤是長期持有策略最大的心理考驗——許多投資人在回撤底部恐慌賣出，反而將紙上虧損轉為實際虧損。

The drawdown chart is key to understanding the 'psychological cost' of long-term holding. Even when long-term returns are positive, investors may face paper losses exceeding 40% during the holding period. Major drawdown events include: the 2008 financial crisis (0050 max drawdown over 50%), 2020 COVID-19 (~30%, but recovered quickly), and the 2022 global rate hikes (~25-30%). Drawdowns are the greatest psychological challenge of buy-and-hold strategies — many investors panic-sell at the bottom, turning paper losses into realized losses.

3.6 滾動績效3.6 Rolling Performance

滾動年化報酬率圖展示持有不同年期的報酬隨時間變化。關鍵觀察：(1) 持有期間越長，曲線越平滑，波動越小——1 年滾動報酬劇烈震盪於 -50% 到 +80% 之間，但 10 年滾動報酬集中在 5%-15% 之間；(2) 長期持有曲線幾乎全在零軸上方，與勝率接近 100% 的結論一致；(3) 即使是 10 年期，不同進場時點仍有數個百分點的差異，顯示「進場時機無所謂」的說法不完全正確——它「影響較小」但並非「完全不重要」。

The rolling annualized return charts show how returns for different holding periods evolve over time. Key observations: (1) Longer holding periods produce smoother, less volatile curves — 1-year rolling returns swing wildly between -50% and +80%, while 10-year returns cluster between 5%-15%; (2) Long-term curves remain almost entirely above zero, consistent with near-100% win rates; (3) Even at 10 years, different entry points still show several percentage points of difference, suggesting that 'timing doesn't matter' is not entirely true — it matters less, but is not completely irrelevant.

3.7 報酬分佈3.7 Return Distributions

報酬率分佈圖清楚呈現持有期間增長帶來的三個效應：(1) 分佈中心右移——均值從 1 年的約 10-13% 持續為正；(2) 左尾縮短——負報酬的機率和幅度都大幅降低；(3) 分佈偏離常態——紅色虛線為常態分佈擬合，實際分佈在 1 年期明顯呈左偏（負偏態），但隨持有期間增長逐漸對稱化。這支持使用 Bootstrap 方法（不假設常態）來建構信賴區間的合理性。

The return distribution charts clearly show three effects of increasing holding period: (1) Distributions shift right — the mean stays positive from ~10-13% at 1 year onward; (2) The left tail shrinks — both the probability and magnitude of negative returns decrease substantially; (3) Distributions deviate from normality — the red dashed line shows the normal distribution fit, and actual 1-year distributions are visibly left-skewed (negative skewness), but become more symmetric at longer horizons. This supports the use of Bootstrap methods (which don't assume normality) for constructing confidence intervals.

3.8 進場年份分析3.8 Vintage Year Analysis

進場年份分析模擬投資人在每年年初投入的實際報酬。每條線代表不同的持有期間。可以看到：(1) 最差進場年份如 2007-2008 年（金融海嘯前高點），短期報酬極差，但持有 7 年以上仍可轉正；(2) 最佳進場年份如 2008 年底至 2009 年初（底部附近），各期間報酬都非常亮眼；(3) 持有期間越長，各年度線條越收斂，顯示長期持有可有效削弱進場時機的影響——不同年份進場的 10 年期報酬差距遠小於 1 年期。

The vintage year analysis simulates actual returns for investors entering at the start of each year. Each line represents a different holding period. Key observations: (1) Worst entry years like 2007-2008 (pre-financial crisis peak) show very poor short-term returns, but holding for 7+ years still turns positive; (2) Best entry years like late 2008 to early 2009 (near the bottom) show excellent returns across all periods; (3) Lines converge with longer holding periods, demonstrating that long-term holding effectively dampens the impact of entry timing — the spread in 10-year returns across entry years is far smaller than for 1-year returns.
