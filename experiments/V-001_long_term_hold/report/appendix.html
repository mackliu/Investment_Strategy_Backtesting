A. 穩健性檢驗：交易成本敏感度A. Robustness: Transaction Cost Sensitivity

為確認結論是否受交易成本假設影響，我們在三種成本情境下重複分析：(1) 零成本（理論基準）、(2) 折扣券商費率（約 0.271%）、(3) 官定費率（約 0.385%）。

下表顯示不同情境下的勝率與平均報酬變化極小。以 0050 持有 1 年為例：零成本勝率 75.3%、折扣 75.0%、官定 74.8%，三者差距不到 1 個百分點。這是可預期的——對於買進持有策略，交易成本僅在進出場時發生一次，年化影響微乎其微。在 5 年以上的持有期間，三種情境的勝率完全相同，更進一步證明交易成本不是影響結論的關鍵因素。

To confirm that conclusions are not sensitive to transaction cost assumptions, we repeated the analysis under three cost scenarios: (1) zero cost (theoretical baseline), (2) discount broker rate (~0.271%), and (3) official rate (~0.385%).

The table below shows minimal variation across scenarios. For 0050 at 1-year holding: zero-cost win rate 75.3%, discount 75.0%, official 74.8% — less than 1 percentage point difference. This is expected — for a buy-and-hold strategy, costs only occur once at entry and exit, with negligible annualized impact. For 5+ year holding periods, win rates are identical across all three scenarios, further confirming that transaction costs are not a critical factor.

D. 月報酬率熱力圖D. Monthly Heatmaps

月報酬率熱力圖以顏色深淺呈現每個月份的報酬表現（綠色=正報酬，紅色=負報酬）。橫軸為月份（1-12 月），縱軸為年份。投資人可從中觀察是否存在季節性效應——例如台股是否有「五窮六絕」效應或「元月效應」。

從圖中可以看出，雖然個別月份有明顯的正負值（例如 2008 年的多個月份都是深紅色），但整體而言沒有一致的季節性規律。某些月份在某些年份表現極好，但在其他年份卻表現極差。這支持效率市場假說的觀點——如果季節效應真的可預測且持續存在，理性投資人會利用它直到效應消失。對投資人的實務啟示是：不需要糾結於「幾月份進場最好」，因為歷史上沒有任何月份一致地優於或劣於其他月份。

The monthly return heatmaps display each month's performance using color intensity (green = positive, red = negative). The x-axis shows months (1-12), the y-axis shows years. Investors can observe whether seasonal patterns exist — for example, whether Taiwan stocks exhibit a 'Sell in May' effect or a 'January Effect.'

The charts show that while individual months have notable values (e.g., multiple deep-red months in 2008), there is no consistent seasonal pattern overall. Some months perform excellently in certain years but terribly in others. This supports the efficient market hypothesis view — if seasonal effects were truly predictable and persistent, rational investors would exploit them until the effect disappears. The practical implication: don't agonize over 'which month is best to enter,' because historically no month consistently outperforms or underperforms others.
