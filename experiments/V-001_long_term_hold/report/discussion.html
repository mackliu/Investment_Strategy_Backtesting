V-001：長期持有是否一定獲利？V-001: Does Long-Term Buy-and-Hold Always Profit?

4. 討論4. Discussion

4.1 主要發現4.1 Key Findings

  • 發現一：「長期持有一定賺」部分成立。持有 0050 或 0056 達 7 年以上，歷史上確實達到 100% 正名目報酬率（含交易成本），平均年化報酬約 10.1%–9.9%，Bootstrap 95% CI 均不含零。Cohen's d 在 7 年期達 1.80（極大效果），證明這不僅是統計學上的巧合，而是具有實質經濟意義的系統性現象。
  • 發現二：短期持有風險顯著。持有 1 年的最差年化報酬為 -52.6%（0050），約有 25% 的機率出現虧損，5% VaR 為 -18.9%，5% CVaR 為 -32.6%。即使持有 3 年，仍有約 7% 的虧損機率。「一定賺」三個字在短期持有的語境下完全不成立。
  • 發現三：通膨調整後仍為正。0050 一年期實質報酬率平均 11.3%（名目 12.6% 減通膨約 1-2%），5 年期實質報酬勝率達 92.3%。三年以上持有期間的實質報酬率均為統計顯著——長期持有不僅能賺到名目報酬，更能真正提升購買力。
  • 發現四：效果量隨持有期間遞增。Cohen's d 從 1 年的 0.60（中等效果）增至 3 年的 1.33，再到 5 年的 1.37（極大效果）。這個遞增趨勢說明持有期間越長，「信號」（正平均報酬）相對於「雜訊」（報酬波動）的比率越高，投資人獲得正報酬的「確定度」也越高。
  • Finding 1: "Long-term holding always profits" is partially supported. Holding 0050 or 0056 for 7+ years has historically achieved 100% positive nominal returns (after costs), with mean annualized returns of ~10.1%-9.9%. Bootstrap 95% CIs exclude zero. Cohen's d at 7 years reaches 1.80 (very large), confirming this is not a statistical fluke but a systematic phenomenon with real economic significance.
  • Finding 2: Short-term risk is significant. The worst-case 1-year return is -52.6% (0050), with ~25% loss probability, 5% VaR of -18.9%, and 5% CVaR of -32.6%. Even at 3 years, there is still a ~7% chance of loss. "Always profits" is entirely unsupported in the short-term holding context.
  • Finding 3: Positive after inflation adjustment. 0050's 1-year real return averages 11.3% (nominal 12.6% minus ~1-2% inflation), with 5-year real win rate at 92.3%. Real returns for 3+ year periods are all statistically significant — long-term holding not only earns nominal returns but genuinely increases purchasing power.
  • Finding 4: Effect sizes increase with horizon. Cohen's d rises from 0.60 (medium) at 1 year to 1.33 at 3 years, then to 1.37 (very large) at 5 years. This increasing trend shows that with longer horizons, the 'signal' (positive mean return) grows relative to 'noise' (return volatility), raising the investor's 'certainty' of positive outcomes.

5.2 0050 與 0056 的差異解讀5.2 Interpreting 0050 vs 0056 Differences

在共同期間（2008-2025），0050 的長期 CAGR（12.3%）明顯高於 0056（7.9%），差距約 4.3 個百分點。這個差距並非偶然——0050 追蹤台灣 50 大市值公司，其中台積電在近年佔比超過 50%，受惠於全球半導體需求的結構性成長。0056 則採用高股息選股，成份股多為傳統產業或金融股，資本增值潛力較低。

然而，這並不代表 0056 是「劣質」選擇。對於需要穩定現金流的退休族群，0056 較高的殖利率可能更符合其需求。關鍵是投資人應理解自己的目標：追求長期資本增值應選 0050，追求穩定配息應選 0056，而非盲目跟風。此外，0050 的高集中度（台積電佔比超過一半）也意味著更高的個股風險——如果台積電的成長動能減弱，0050 的表現可能不如過去亮眼。

Over the common period (2008-2025), 0050's long-term CAGR (12.3%) significantly exceeds 0056 (7.9%), a gap of ~4.3 percentage points. This is no coincidence — 0050 tracks Taiwan's 50 largest companies by market cap, with TSMC accounting for over 50% in recent years, benefiting from structural growth in global semiconductor demand. 0056 selects stocks based on high dividend yields, with constituents skewing toward traditional industries and financials with lower capital appreciation potential.

However, this does not make 0056 an 'inferior' choice. For retirees needing stable cash flow, 0056's higher yield may better match their needs. The key is understanding one's investment objective: choose 0050 for long-term capital growth, 0056 for stable dividends, rather than blindly following trends. Moreover, 0050's high concentration (TSMC over half) also implies greater single-stock risk — if TSMC's growth momentum weakens, 0050's performance may not be as stellar as in the past.

4.2 注意事項與風險4.2 Caveats and Risks

  • 倖存者偏差：0050 和 0056 均為存續中的 ETF；已下市的基金未納入分析。如果將失敗的 ETF 也納入，整體報酬可能會更低。台灣曾有多檔 ETF 因規模過小而下市或清算，其投資人的實際經歷可能與本研究的結果截然不同。
  • 歷史有限：0050 約 22 年歷史；0056 僅約 18 年。長期持有（10-15 年）的非重疊樣本數非常少——例如 10 年期只有約 2 個獨立樣本（0050），統計推論的可信度因此受限。以 15 年期來說，獨立觀測值更是僅有約 1 個，任何基於如此少量獨立樣本的結論都應謹慎看待。
  • 重疊報酬與有效樣本數：滾動報酬具有高度自相關。例如今天進場和明天進場的 5 年持有報酬幾乎相同（重疊率 99.96%），使得名目樣本數（數千筆）遠大於有效獨立樣本數（僅數十甚至個位數）。Newey-West HAC 校正了標準誤，區塊拔靴法也保留了時間序列結構，但投資人仍不應被大樣本數的表象所迷惑。直觀地說：5,000 筆的「5 年滾動報酬」本質上大約只包含 4-5 個完全獨立的 5 年期觀測。
  • 過去不代表未來：台灣經濟在 2003-2025 年間整體表現良好（半導體產業蓬勃、GDP 成長穩定），但未來是否能延續此趨勢並不確定。全球經濟結構性轉變（如 AI 對產業的衝擊、地緣政治風險、氣候變遷）可能使未來數十年的股市表現與過去截然不同。歷史結果提供參考，但不構成保證。
  • 單一市場風險：本研究僅分析台灣市場的 ETF，結果無法直接推廣至其他市場。台灣的成功很大程度上依賴半導體產業——這是高度集中的產業風險。不同國家可能有截然不同的長期持有經驗（如日本 1990 年代至今的「失落」表現）。
  • 2025 年 0050 拆股事件：0050 在 2025 年 6 月進行約 4:1 拆股。本研究使用還原後的 adj_close 數據，已正確處理拆股對歷史價格的影響，確保跨期間比較的一致性。
  • Survivorship Bias: Both 0050 and 0056 are surviving ETFs; delisted funds are not analyzed. Including failed ETFs would likely lower overall returns. Taiwan has had multiple ETFs delisted or liquidated due to insufficient AUM, and those investors' actual experiences may differ dramatically from this study's findings.
  • Limited History: 0050 has ~22 years; 0056 only ~18 years. Long-period results (10-15Y) have very few non-overlapping samples — 10-year returns have only ~2 independent observations (0050). For 15-year periods, there are only ~1 independent observations, and any conclusions based on so few independent samples should be treated with caution.
  • Overlapping Returns and Effective Sample Size: Rolling returns are highly autocorrelated — 5-year returns starting today vs tomorrow overlap by 99.96%, making nominal sample sizes (thousands) vastly exceed effective independent sample sizes (dozens or single digits). Newey-West HAC corrects standard errors and block bootstrap preserves time-series structure, but investors should not be misled by large nominal sample counts. Intuitively: 5,000 '5-year rolling returns' essentially contain only about 4-5 truly independent 5-year observations.
  • Past Does Not Predict Future: Taiwan's economy performed well during 2003-2025 (booming semiconductor industry, stable GDP growth), but continuation is uncertain. Structural shifts (AI industry disruption, geopolitical risks, climate change) may make the next few decades look very different from the past. Historical results provide guidance but not guarantees.
  • Single-Market Risk: This study only analyzes Taiwan-listed ETFs; results cannot be directly generalized to other markets. Taiwan's success heavily depends on the semiconductor industry — a highly concentrated industry risk. Different countries may have vastly different long-term holding experiences (e.g., Japan's 'lost decades' since 1990).
  • 2025 Stock Split: 0050 underwent an approximately 4:1 stock split in June 2025. This study uses adjusted close (adj_close) data that correctly accounts for the split's impact on historical prices, ensuring consistency in cross-period comparisons.

4.3 對投資人的啟示4.3 Implications for Investors

對保守型投資人：數據顯示持有 7 年以上在歷史上確實達到 100% 正報酬率，平均年化約 10.1%。但投資人必須有心理準備承受持有期間可能超過 56% 的回檔（0050 歷史最大回檔 -55.7%）。如果 2008 年那樣的腰斬會讓你恐慌賣出，那麼你的實際持有期間可能遠短於計劃。建議措施：(1) 搭配債券或現金部位以降低整體波動；(2) 事先設定「忍受底線」——如果你確認自己能承受 30% 回檔但無法承受 50%，可調整股債比；(3) 以定期定額取代單筆投入，降低進場時機風險（詳見 V-002 研究）。

對積極型投資人：本研究不支持「閉著眼睛買就好」的論述。進場年份分析（Vintage Year）清楚顯示，2008 年底進場 vs 2007 年高點進場的 5 年期報酬差距可達 10 個百分點以上。此外，0050 的長期報酬明顯優於 0056（CAGR 差距約 4.3 個百分點），若目標是資本成長而非現金流，市值型 ETF 是更佳選擇。最關鍵的心態轉變：投資人應以「歷史上非常可能獲利，但需準備好承受過程波動」取代「一定獲利」的認知——後者是危險的過度自信。

For conservative investors: Data shows 7+ year holding historically achieved 100% positive returns, averaging ~10.1% annualized. However, investors must be prepared for drawdowns potentially exceeding 56% (0050 historical max drawdown: -55.7%). If a 2008-style crash would cause panic-selling, your actual holding period may fall far short of planned. Recommended measures: (1) pair with bonds or cash to reduce overall volatility; (2) pre-define your 'tolerance floor' — if you can handle 30% drawdown but not 50%, adjust your stock-bond ratio; (3) use dollar-cost averaging instead of lump-sum to reduce timing risk (see V-002 study).

For aggressive investors: This study does not support 'just buy with eyes closed.' The vintage year analysis clearly shows that entering at the 2008 bottom vs the 2007 peak can produce a 10+ percentage point difference in 5-year returns. Additionally, 0050 significantly outperforms 0056 (CAGR gap ~4.3 ppts); if the goal is capital growth rather than income, the market-cap ETF is the better choice. The most important mindset shift: replace 'guaranteed profit' with 'historically very likely to profit, but prepared for interim volatility' — the former is dangerously overconfident.
