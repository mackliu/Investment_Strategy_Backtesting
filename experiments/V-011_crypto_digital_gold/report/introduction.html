V-011：加密貨幣是數位黃金V-011: Cryptocurrency Is Digital Gold

緒論Introduction

1.1 背景1.1 Background

自 2009 年中本聰發布比特幣白皮書以來，「比特幣是數位黃金」已成為加密貨幣社群最常見的論述之一。支持者認為，BTC 具有與黃金類似的特性：(1) 固定供給上限（2100 萬枚）；(2) 去中心化，不受央行控制；(3) 可作為通膨對沖工具；(4) 在經濟危機時提供避險功能。

然而，這個論述值得嚴格的實證檢驗。BTC 的歷史僅十餘年，其市場結構從早期的「極客玩具」演變為機構投資者參與的主流資產類別。這段演變過程中，BTC 的統計特性是否支持「數位黃金」的定位？特別是在 2020 年 COVID 崩盤、2022 年 LUNA/FTX 危機等壓力測試中，BTC 表現如何？

Since Satoshi Nakamoto published the Bitcoin whitepaper in 2009, "Bitcoin is digital gold" has become one of the most common narratives in the crypto community. Proponents argue BTC shares key properties with gold: (1) fixed supply cap (21 million); (2) decentralized, free from central bank control; (3) serves as an inflation hedge; (4) provides safe-haven protection during crises.

However, this narrative deserves rigorous empirical testing. BTC has only ~15 years of history, and its market structure has evolved from a "geek toy" to an institutional asset class. Do BTC's statistical properties support the "digital gold" label, especially during stress tests like the 2020 COVID crash and 2022 LUNA/FTX crisis?

1.2 文獻回顧1.2 Literature Review

避險定義：Baur & Lucey (2010) 在 Financial Review 提出了嚴謹的避險（hedge）與安全港（safe haven）定義框架：hedge 是與目標資產長期負相關或不相關的資產，safe haven 則是在市場極端下跌時與目標資產負相關或不相關的資產。這兩個概念常被混淆。

BTC 非避險工具：Bouri et al. (2017) 在 Finance Research Letters 的研究指出，BTC 主要是一種分散化工具（diversifier），而非真正的避險資產或安全港。Klein, Thu & Walther (2018) 進一步發現，BTC 在市場壓力時的行為與黃金相反——黃金上漲，BTC 反而下跌。

COVID 實證：Conlon & McGee (2020) 在 Finance Research Letters 的研究顯示，2020 年 3 月 COVID 崩盤期間，BTC 與 S&P 500 同步暴跌，證明 BTC 在極端壓力下不具避險功能。

加密特定因子：Liu & Tsyvinski (2021) 發表於 Review of Financial Studies，發現 BTC 報酬主要由加密貨幣市場特有的因子驅動，而非傳統金融因子（市場、價值、動能），這從根本上質疑了「數位黃金」的類比。

投組分散效果：儘管 BTC 不是避險工具，Platanakis & Urquhart (2020) 在 British Accounting Review 發現，小比例配置加密貨幣（1-5%）確實能改善投資組合的風險調整報酬，前提是定期再平衡。

早期研究：值得注意的是，Dyhrberg (2016) 的早期研究認為 BTC 的 GARCH 特性類似黃金，但 Smales (2019) 從流動性和波動度角度否定了這一結論。隨著市場成熟，共識已轉向「BTC 非避險」。

Hedge definition: Baur & Lucey (2010, Financial Review) provided rigorous definitions: a hedge is an asset negatively correlated or uncorrelated with the target over long periods; a safe haven is negatively correlated/uncorrelated during extreme market downturns.

BTC is not a hedge: Bouri et al. (2017, Finance Research Letters) found BTC is primarily a diversifier, not a true hedge or safe haven. Klein, Thu & Walther (2018) further showed BTC behaves oppositely to gold during stress — gold rises while BTC falls.

COVID evidence: Conlon & McGee (2020, Finance Research Letters) showed BTC crashed alongside the S&P 500 during March 2020, proving it fails as a safe haven under extreme stress.

Crypto-specific factors: Liu & Tsyvinski (2021, Review of Financial Studies) found BTC returns are driven by crypto-specific factors, not traditional financial factors, fundamentally challenging the "digital gold" analogy.

Portfolio diversification: Despite not being a hedge, Platanakis & Urquhart (2020, British Accounting Review) found small crypto allocations (1-5%) can improve risk-adjusted portfolio returns with regular rebalancing.

Early studies: Dyhrberg (2016) initially found BTC GARCH properties similar to gold, but Smales (2019) refuted this from liquidity and volatility perspectives. As the market matured, consensus has shifted toward "BTC is not a hedge."

1.4 研究貢獻1.4 Research Contribution

本研究的貢獻在於：(1) 使用最新數據（至 2025 年）涵蓋多次重大加密貨幣事件；(2) 將「數位黃金」論述拆解為三個可量化的維度進行系統性檢驗；(3) 區分三個市場結構階段（早期/過渡/機構化）分析 BTC 特性演變；(4) 提供實務投組配置建議（1-5% BTC）的風險-報酬分析。

This study contributes by: (1) using latest data (through 2025) covering multiple major crypto events; (2) decomposing the "digital gold" thesis into three quantifiable dimensions; (3) analyzing BTC characteristics across three market structure phases (early/transition/institutional); (4) providing practical risk-return analysis for portfolio allocation (1-5% BTC).
