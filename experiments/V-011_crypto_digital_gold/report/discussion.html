V-011：加密貨幣是數位黃金V-011: Cryptocurrency Is Digital Gold

討論Discussion

4.1 主要發現4.1 Key Findings

發現一：BTC 不是避險資產。在 SPY 跌幅超過 2% 的日子，BTC 平均也下跌，與黃金的避險行為截然不同。BTC-Gold 相關性在危機與正常時期無穩定模式（Fisher z p=0.0000），否定了「危機時 BTC 如黃金般上漲」的預期。這與 Conlon & McGee (2020) 和 Klein et al. (2018) 的發現一致。

發現二：BTC 不具抗通膨特性。BTC 月報酬與 CPI 年增率的相關性接近零，OLS 迴歸 R-squared 幾乎為零。儘管 BTC 有固定供給上限，但其價格主要由投機情緒和加密市場特有因子驅動（Liu & Tsyvinski 2021），而非對通膨的對沖需求。

發現三：小量 BTC 確有分散效果。在 60/40 投組中加入 1-5% BTC 可以改善風險-報酬特性，但代價是尾部風險增加。這與 Platanakis & Urquhart (2020) 的研究一致。最佳配置取決於投資者的風險承受度。

Finding 1: BTC is not a hedge. On days when SPY falls more than 2%, BTC also declines on average, opposite to gold's hedging behavior. BTC-Gold correlation shows no stable pattern between crisis and normal periods (Fisher z p=0.0000), refuting the expectation that "BTC rises like gold during crises." This aligns with Conlon & McGee (2020) and Klein et al. (2018).

Finding 2: BTC does not hedge inflation. BTC monthly returns show near-zero correlation with CPI changes, with negligible OLS R-squared. Despite BTC's fixed supply cap, its price is driven primarily by speculative sentiment and crypto-specific factors (Liu & Tsyvinski 2021), not inflation hedging demand.

Finding 3: Small BTC allocation does diversify. Adding 1-5% BTC to a 60/40 portfolio can improve risk-return characteristics, at the cost of increased tail risk. This is consistent with Platanakis & Urquhart (2020). Optimal allocation depends on risk tolerance.

4.2 為什麼「數位黃金」論述不成立4.2 Why "Digital Gold" Fails

「數位黃金」論述的核心問題在於混淆了「稀缺性」與「避險性」。黃金的避險地位來自數千年的文化認同、央行儲備需求、以及在危機時的行為模式（與股市負相關）。BTC 雖然有數學上的稀缺性（2100 萬枚上限），但缺乏黃金的歷史積累和制度支撐。

更重要的是，BTC 的價格波動主要受以下加密特有因子影響：(1) 監管消息（如中國禁令）；(2) 加密產業事件（如 LUNA 崩盤、FTX 倒閉）；(3) 半減期周期；(4) 機構採用程度。這些因子與通膨或經濟周期幾乎無關，因此 BTC 無法發揮類似黃金的宏觀對沖功能。

The core problem with the "digital gold" narrative is confusing "scarcity" with "hedging ability." Gold's hedge status comes from millennia of cultural recognition, central bank reserve demand, and crisis behavior (negative stock correlation). BTC has mathematical scarcity (21M cap) but lacks gold's historical accumulation and institutional backing.

More importantly, BTC prices are driven by crypto-specific factors: (1) regulatory news (e.g., China ban); (2) crypto industry events (LUNA crash, FTX collapse); (3) halving cycles; (4) institutional adoption. These factors are largely unrelated to inflation or business cycles, so BTC cannot serve gold-like macro hedging functions.

4.3 市場結構演變的影響4.3 Impact of Market Structure Evolution

三階段分析揭示一個重要趨勢：隨著機構投資者進場，BTC 與傳統風險資產的相關性上升。這意味著 BTC 正變得更像「高 beta 科技股」而非「數位黃金」。如果這個趨勢持續，BTC 未來的分散化效益可能進一步減弱。

Smales (2019) 也指出，BTC 的流動性遠低於黃金，在極端市場條件下容易出現流動性枯竭，這進一步削弱了其作為避險資產的可信度。

Sub-period analysis reveals an important trend: as institutional investors enter, BTC correlation with traditional risk assets increases. This means BTC is becoming more like a "high-beta tech stock" than "digital gold." If this trend continues, BTC's diversification benefits may further diminish.

Smales (2019) also noted BTC's liquidity is far below gold's, and it is prone to liquidity dry-ups during extreme conditions, further undermining its hedge asset credibility.

4.4 投資啟示4.4 Investment Implications

保守型投資人：如果您的目標是避險和保值，BTC 不是適合的工具。黃金和長期公債仍然是更可靠的選擇。不要被「數位黃金」的行銷話術所迷惑——數據清楚顯示，BTC 在危機時的表現與黃金截然不同。建議配置：0% BTC，維持傳統的黃金+債券避險組合。

穩健型投資人：如果您理解 BTC 的本質（高風險投機資產，非避險工具），且願意用少量資金換取「非對稱報酬」的機會，可以考慮 1-2% BTC 配置。以基準 60/40 投組為例，加入 1% BTC 後 Sharpe 從 0.769 小幅改善，但最大回撤變化不大。重點是：(1) 用「賠光了也不心疼」的金額投入；(2) 嚴格年度再平衡，避免 BTC 因暴漲而佔比失控。

積極型投資人：如果您能承受高波動和大幅回撤，3-5% BTC 配置可以作為「彩券部位」——長期持有可能帶來超額報酬，但必須有承受 50-80% 回撤的心理準備。5% BTC 配置的 Sharpe 為 0.990，但最大回撤惡化至 -27.8%。定期再平衡至關重要，否則 BTC 的高波動會快速改變投組的風險配置。不建議超過 5%——邊際效益不足以補償急劇增加的尾部風險。

Conservative investors: If your goal is hedging and preservation, BTC is not suitable. Gold and long-term bonds remain more reliable. Do not be misled by 'digital gold' marketing -- data clearly shows BTC behaves very differently from gold during crises. Recommendation: 0% BTC, maintain traditional gold+bond hedging.

Moderate investors: If you understand BTC's true nature (high-risk speculative asset, not a hedge) and are willing to use small amounts for 'asymmetric return' opportunities, consider 1-2% BTC allocation. Using the base 60/40 portfolio, adding 1% BTC slightly improves Sharpe from 0.769, with minimal drawdown impact. Key: (1) invest only amounts you can afford to lose entirely; (2) strictly rebalance annually to prevent BTC from dominating due to surges.

Aggressive investors: If you can tolerate high volatility and deep drawdowns, 3-5% BTC allocation can serve as a 'lottery position' -- long-term holding may yield excess returns, but be prepared for 50-80% drawdowns. 5% BTC allocation Sharpe is 0.990, but max drawdown worsens to -27.8%. Regular rebalancing is critical. We do not recommend exceeding 5% -- marginal benefits cannot compensate for sharply increasing tail risk.

4.5 研究限制4.5 Limitations

  • 歷史數據有限：BTC 僅 ~11 年數據（ETH 更短），樣本量遠少於黃金（數十年）或股票（百年）。
  • 市場結構快速變化：BTC 現貨 ETF、機構採用等結構性變化可能改變未來的統計特性。
  • 24/7 交易對齊：加密貨幣全天交易，但本研究以傳統市場交易日對齊，可能遺漏週末波動。
  • CPI 滯後：CPI 數據有 1-2 個月的發布延遲，與即時市場價格的時間對齊不完美。
  • 單一避險定義：本研究主要使用 SPY 日跌 2% 作為壓力日門檻，不同定義可能產生不同結果。
  • Limited history: BTC has only ~11 years of data (ETH less), far fewer than gold (decades) or stocks (century).
  • Rapid structural change: BTC spot ETFs, institutional adoption may alter future statistical properties.
  • 24/7 alignment: Crypto trades continuously but this study aligns with traditional market days, potentially missing weekend volatility.
  • CPI lag: CPI data has 1-2 month publication delay, imperfect alignment with real-time market prices.
  • Single stress definition: Using SPY -2% as stress threshold; different thresholds may yield different results.
