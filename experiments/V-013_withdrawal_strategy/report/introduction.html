緒論Introduction

1.1 研究背景1.1 Background

1994 年，財務規劃師 William Bengen 發表了一篇開創性論文，分析美國 1926-1976 年的股債報酬數據後發現：只要退休時將年提領金額設為投資組合的 4%，之後每年依通膨調整，即使遭遇最差的歷史時期（如 1966 年退休遇到滯脹），資金也能撐過 30 年。這就是「4% 法則」的起源。

1998 年，Cooley, Hubbard & Walz 三位學者以更嚴謹的方法擴充了 Bengen 的研究，分析 1926-1995 年數據，考慮不同股債配置比例（從 100% 股票到 100% 債券），發現 50/50 股債配置在 4% 提領率下有約 95% 的 30 年存活率。這份被稱為「Trinity Study」的研究成果，至今仍是退休理財最被廣泛引用的學術依據。

2010 年代，FIRE（Financial Independence, Retire Early）運動在全球興起，提倡年輕人透過高儲蓄率提早累積「25 倍年支出」的退休金（即隱含 4% 提領率），在 30-40 歲提前退休。這股風潮也吹入台灣，許多網路社群與 YouTuber 以「存股 + 4% 法則」為核心，推廣「財務自由不是夢」的概念。然而，大多數宣傳者並未深入理解 4% 法則的前提假設——它基於美國特定的歷史資產報酬，是否能直接套用到台灣市場，是一個嚴肅的研究問題。

台灣投資社群更發展出一套獨特的退休敘事：「買 0056 領配息退休」。這個說法假設每年 4-5% 的配息率就等於安全提領率，彷彿只要不賣股票、只領配息，退休金就永遠花不完。然而，V-003 的研究已經指出，配息本質上只是資產重新分配（股價除息後會等額下降），並不會神奇地創造額外報酬。那麼，「靠配息退休」是否真的比「賣股提領」更安全？這正是本研究要回答的核心問題之一。

此外，2022 年 Morningstar 的研究根據當時的低利率環境，將安全提領率下修至 3.3%；2023 年雖因利率回升調高至 3.8%，但仍低於傳統的 4%。2011 年 Trinity Study 原作者的更新研究也建議採用動態提領策略，而非固守原始的固定金額法則。這些發展使得重新檢驗 4% 法則變得更加重要。

In 1994, financial planner William Bengen published a seminal paper analyzing US stock and bond returns from 1926-1976. He found that setting the initial annual withdrawal at 4% of the portfolio (inflation-adjusted thereafter) would sustain a retiree through even the worst historical periods (e.g., 1966 retirement amid stagflation). This became the "4% rule."

In 1998, Cooley, Hubbard & Walz expanded Bengen's work with more rigorous methods, analyzing 1926-1995 data across various stock/bond allocations (from 100% stocks to 100% bonds), finding that a 50/50 portfolio at 4% withdrawal had ~95% survival over 30 years. This "Trinity Study" remains the most widely cited academic reference for retirement planning.

In the 2010s, the FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early) movement went global, advocating high savings rates to accumulate "25x annual expenses" (implying a 4% withdrawal rate) for early retirement in one's 30s-40s. This trend reached Taiwan, where online communities and YouTubers promote "stock accumulation + 4% rule" as the path to financial freedom. However, most advocates do not deeply understand the rule's underlying assumptions — whether US-derived parameters apply to the Taiwan market is a serious research question.

Taiwan's investment community has developed a unique retirement narrative: "Buy 0056 and live off dividends." This assumes a 4-5% dividend yield equals a safe withdrawal rate, as if not selling shares means the money never runs out. Yet V-003 demonstrated that dividends are merely asset redistribution (price drops by the dividend amount). Is "living off dividends" truly safer than selling shares? This is one of our core questions.

Furthermore, Morningstar's 2022 research lowered the safe withdrawal rate to 3.3% given low rates; 2023 raised it to 3.8% as rates recovered, but still below the traditional 4%. The 2011 Trinity Study update by the original authors recommended dynamic withdrawal strategies over the original fixed-amount approach. These developments make re-examining the 4% rule imperative.

1.2 文獻回顧1.2 Literature Review

Bengen (1994) 最早提出「SAFEMAX」概念——使投資組合在所有歷史 30 年窗口中都能存活的最高初始提領率。以 50/50 股債配置，SAFEMAX 約為 4.15%。此研究奠定了整個提領率文獻的基礎。

Cooley, Hubbard & Walz (1998, 2011) 的 Trinity Study 以機率框架取代 Bengen 的「最差情境」方法，計算不同配置和提領率的歷史存活率。2011 年更新研究延伸數據至 2009 年，特別建議退休者應考慮動態調整提領金額以因應市場波動。

Pfau (2012) 指出 4% 法則的成功很大程度上依賴於美國市場的優異歷史報酬。以其他已開發國家的數據測試時，安全提領率往往更低（英國約 3.4%、日本低於 2%）。這提醒我們不能將美國經驗直接套用到其他市場。

Estrada (2017) 以全球 21 個國家的數據進行跨國比較，發現許多國家的安全提領率遠低於美國的 4%，部分國家甚至低於 3%。這進一步支持了「4% 法則是美國特殊例外而非全球通則」的觀點。

Morningstar (2022, 2023) 以蒙地卡羅模擬結合當前市場條件（低利率、高估值），將安全提領率下修至 3.3%（2022）後又調升至 3.8%（2023）。其報告強調提領率應隨市場環境動態調整，而非固定不變。

Guyton & Klinger (2006) 提出「決策規則」（Decision Rules）策略，根據投資組合表現動態調整提領金額，被視為 Guardrails 類策略的先驅。後來的研究普遍證實，動態調整策略能顯著提高投資組合的存活率。

關於台灣市場的退休提領研究相對稀少。國內文獻多聚焦於退休金制度（勞退、勞保）的可持續性，鮮少以 Trinity Study 的方法論檢驗台灣 ETF 的安全提領率。本研究填補了這個缺口。

Bengen (1994) introduced the "SAFEMAX" concept — the highest initial withdrawal rate that sustains the portfolio through all historical 30-year windows. For 50/50 stock/bond, SAFEMAX was ~4.15%. This paper established the foundation for the entire withdrawal rate literature.

Cooley, Hubbard & Walz (1998, 2011) Trinity Study replaced Bengen's worst-case approach with a probability framework, computing historical survival rates across allocations and rates. The 2011 update extended data to 2009 and recommended dynamic withdrawal adjustments.

Pfau (2012) showed the 4% rule's success largely depends on exceptional US market returns. Testing with other developed market data yielded lower safe rates (UK ~3.4%, Japan <2%). This cautions against applying US parameters to other markets.

Estrada (2017) compared 21 countries and found many have safe withdrawal rates well below the US 4%, some below 3% — supporting the view that the 4% rule is a US exception rather than a global norm.

Morningstar (2022, 2023) used Monte Carlo simulation with current market conditions (low rates, high valuations) to revise the safe rate to 3.3% (2022), later 3.8% (2023). Their reports emphasize rates should be market-adaptive, not fixed.

Guyton & Klinger (2006) proposed "Decision Rules" — dynamically adjusting withdrawals based on portfolio performance — pioneering guardrails-type strategies. Subsequent research consistently confirms that dynamic strategies improve portfolio survival.

Taiwan-specific retirement withdrawal research is scarce. Domestic literature focuses on pension system sustainability (Labor Pension, Labor Insurance) rather than applying Trinity Study methodology to Taiwan ETFs. This study fills that gap.

1.3 研究問題與假說1.3 Research Questions & Hypotheses

基於上述背景與文獻，本研究提出四個核心研究問題，並建立對應的統計假說：

#研究問題虛無假說 H0對立假說 H1
Q1台灣 4% 提領 30 年存活率是否達 95%？台灣 4% 存活率 >= 95%台灣 4% 存活率 < 95%
Q260/40 是否比 100% 股票更適合提領？60/40 存活率 = 100% 股票存活率60/40 存活率 > 100% 股票
Q3動態調整是否改善存活率？Guardrails 存活率 = 固定金額存活率Guardrails 存活率 > 固定金額
Q40050 vs 0056 誰更適合退休提領？0050 與 0056 存活率無差異存活率有顯著差異

Based on the above background and literature, we propose four core research questions with corresponding hypotheses:

#Research QuestionNull H0Alternative H1
Q1Does Taiwan 4% achieve 95% 30-year survival?Taiwan 4% survival >= 95%Taiwan 4% survival < 95%
Q2Is 60/40 better than 100% stock for withdrawal?60/40 survival = 100% stock survival60/40 survival > 100% stock
Q3Do guardrails improve survival?Guardrails survival = fixed amount survivalGuardrails survival > fixed amount
Q40050 vs 0056 for retirement withdrawal?No difference in survivalSignificant difference in survival

Q1 的意義：若台灣 4% 存活率顯著低於 95%，代表台灣投資人不應直接套用美國的 4% 法則，需要更保守的提領率。這對近年流行的 FIRE 運動和「存股退休」策略有重要警示意義。

Q2 的意義：退休提領階段與累積階段的最佳配置可能不同。高股票比例在累積階段有利（更高的期望報酬），但提領階段需要承受「序列風險」（早期虧損對提領者傷害特別大），債券的穩定性可能更重要。

Q3 的意義：若動態調整能顯著改善存活率，代表退休者不應死守固定 4% 的提領金額，而是根據市場表現適度調整，以延長退休金壽命。

Q4 的意義：台灣市場獨特的「配息迷思」使許多退休者偏好高股息 ETF。若 0050 和 0056 的提領存活率無顯著差異（或 0050 反而更佳），代表「靠配息退休」的偏好缺乏數據支持。

Q1 significance: If Taiwan 4% survival is significantly below 95%, Taiwan investors should not directly apply the US 4% rule — a critical warning for the FIRE movement and "stock accumulation retirement" strategy.

Q2 significance: Optimal allocation may differ between accumulation and withdrawal phases. High stock allocation benefits accumulation (higher expected returns), but in withdrawal, "sequence-of-returns risk" (early losses disproportionately harm withdrawers) may make bond stability more valuable.

Q3 significance: If dynamic adjustment significantly improves survival, retirees should not rigidly maintain a fixed 4% amount but adjust based on market performance to extend portfolio longevity.

Q4 significance: Taiwan's unique "dividend myth" leads many retirees to prefer high-dividend ETFs. If 0050 and 0056 show no survival difference (or 0050 is better), the preference for "living off dividends" lacks empirical support.

1.4 研究貢獻1.4 Research Contributions

  • 首次以 Trinity Study 方法論系統性檢驗台灣市場。過去研究多聚焦於美國市場，本研究是少數以相同嚴謹標準測試台灣 ETF 安全提領率的實證研究。
  • 直接比較「賣股提領」vs「配息提領」。針對台灣市場獨特的 0050 vs 0056 退休策略爭論，提供客觀數據。
  • 同時測試歷史路徑與 Bootstrap 兩種方法。歷史路徑受限於樣本量（僅 68 條 30 年窗口），Bootstrap 提供統計推論所需的大樣本。
  • 比較三種主流提領策略。固定金額、固定比例、動態 Guardrails 的正面對決，為退休者提供策略選擇依據。
  • First systematic application of Trinity Study methodology to Taiwan. Most withdrawal research focuses on the US; this is among the few rigorous studies testing Taiwan ETF safe withdrawal rates.
  • Direct comparison of "sell shares" vs "dividend-based" withdrawal. Provides objective data for Taiwan's unique 0050 vs 0056 retirement debate.
  • Dual methodology: historical paths + bootstrap. Historical paths are limited in sample size ({n_hist_paths} 30-year windows); bootstrap provides the large samples needed for statistical inference.
  • Head-to-head comparison of three mainstream strategies. Fixed amount, fixed ratio, and dynamic guardrails directly compared to guide retiree strategy selection.
