V-013：4% 提領法則真的安全嗎？V-013: Is the 4% Rule Really Safe?

討論Discussion

4.1 核心發現與國際比較4.1 Key Findings & International Comparison

發現一：4% 法則在美國大致成立，但非萬無一失

我們的 Bootstrap 模擬顯示，60/40 配置在 4% 提領率下的 30 年存活率為 90.2%（歷史路徑為 94.1%）。這個結果接近但略低於 Trinity Study 原始報告的 95%，反映了兩方面的差異：(1) 我們的數據延伸至 2020 年代，涵蓋了 2008 金融危機和 2020 疫情衝擊；(2) Bootstrap 模擬產生的情境更多元、包含更極端的組合。

約 10% 的破產機率看似不高，但若將其轉化為實際意義：每 10 位採用 4% 法則退休的人中，約有 1 位可能在 30 年內耗盡資金。考慮到破產的後果極為嚴重（晚年沒有收入），這個風險不容忽視。

安全提領率（SWR）方面，60/40 的 SWR 約 3.0%，與 Morningstar (2022) 的 3.3% 和 Pfau (2012) 的建議方向一致。100% 股票的 SWR 更低，僅 2.5%，印證了「退休提領階段，波動度比報酬率更重要」的核心觀點。

Finding 1: The 4% Rule Roughly Holds in the US, but Is Not Foolproof

Our bootstrap simulation shows 60/40 at 4%: 90.2% 30-year survival (historical paths: 94.1%). This is close to but slightly below the Trinity Study's original 95%, reflecting: (1) our data extends to the 2020s, covering the 2008 crisis and 2020 pandemic; (2) bootstrap generates more diverse and extreme scenarios.

~10% ruin probability seems modest, but in practical terms: roughly 1 in 10 retirees using the 4% rule could deplete funds within 30 years. Given that ruin means no income in old age, this risk cannot be dismissed.

SWR: 60/40 at ~3.0%, consistent with Morningstar (2022) at 3.3% and Pfau (2012). 100% stock SWR is even lower at 2.5%, confirming that "in withdrawal, volatility matters more than returns."

發現二：台灣市場的安全提領率須更保守

0050 以 4% 提領的存活率為 94.9%，雖然統計上無法拒絕 >= 95%（p=0.3294），但我們認為有幾個理由支持更保守的態度：

  • 數據長度不足：0050 僅 22 年歷史，等同於只經歷了約 1 個完整的經濟週期。美國 150 年的數據涵蓋了兩次世界大戰、大蕭條、滯脹、網路泡沫、金融危機等眾多黑天鵝事件，台灣的 22 年可能尚未充分取樣到「最差情境」。
  • 單一市場風險：台灣股市集中度高（台積電佔 0050 權重約 50%），面臨地緣政治風險等美國市場較少遭遇的系統性威脅。
  • 缺乏債券配置：台灣沒有與美國公債同等的長期穩定配息資產，退休者難以建構有效的 60/40 配置。
  • 國際比較佐證：Estrada (2017) 的跨國研究顯示，非美國已開發市場的安全提領率普遍在 2.5-3.5% 之間，低於美國的 4%。台灣作為新興市場，波動度更高，安全提領率可能更低。

Finding 2: Taiwan Requires a More Conservative Withdrawal Rate

0050 at 4%: 94.9% survival. While we cannot statistically reject >= 95% (p=0.3294), several reasons support a more conservative approach:

  • Insufficient data length: 0050 has only 22 years — roughly one economic cycle. The US 150 years covers two World Wars, the Great Depression, stagflation, dot-com bust, and GFC. Taiwan's 22 years may not have sampled "worst cases."
  • Single-market risk: Taiwan's stock market is highly concentrated (TSMC is ~50% of 0050 weight), facing geopolitical risks uncommon in the US.
  • No bond allocation: Taiwan lacks a US Treasury equivalent for stable long-term income, making effective 60/40 portfolios difficult to construct.
  • International comparison: Estrada (2017) shows non-US developed market SWRs generally range 2.5-3.5%, below the US 4%. Taiwan, as an emerging market with higher volatility, likely has an even lower SWR.

發現三：動態提領是最關鍵的改進

在所有檢定結果中，Guardrails 策略 vs 固定金額的效果量最大（4.58 百分點），z 統計量最顯著（z=8.51）。這個發現與 Guyton & Klinger (2006)、Morningstar (2023) 的建議完全一致：退休提領最重要的改進不是調整資產配置或提領率，而是從「固定金額」轉向「彈性調整」。

Guardrails 策略的核心邏輯：市場好的時候（投資組合漲超過一定幅度），可以適度增加提領（上限 120%），享受更好的生活品質；市場差的時候（投資組合跌超過一定幅度），暫時減少提領（下限 80%），保護資產壽命。這僅需 20% 的支出彈性，就能將存活率從 89.9% 提升至 94.4%。

值得注意的是，固定比例策略雖然存活率為 100%（數學上永不歸零），但最低月提領金額僅 30,532 元，實際上可能無法滿足基本生活需求。「帳面上不破產」和「退休生活有品質」是兩回事。相比之下，Guardrails 策略在保持較高存活率的同時，最低月提領金額 33,333 元，更接近實際可行的生活水準。

Finding 3: Dynamic Withdrawal Is the Most Critical Improvement

Among all test results, Guardrails vs. fixed amount shows the largest effect (4.58 pp) and strongest z-statistic (z=8.51). This aligns perfectly with Guyton & Klinger (2006) and Morningstar (2023): the most important improvement is not adjusting allocation or rate, but switching from "fixed amount" to "flexible adjustment."

The guardrails logic: when markets are good, increase withdrawal up to 120% of base for better lifestyle; when markets are bad, reduce to 80% to protect longevity. Just 20% spending flexibility raises survival from 89.9% to 94.4%.

Note: fixed ratio has 100% mathematical survival but minimum monthly withdrawal drops to NTD 30,532 — potentially inadequate for basic living. "Not bankrupt on paper" and "dignified retirement" are different things. Guardrails maintain a minimum of NTD 33,333/month, closer to a viable living standard.

發現四：0056「靠配息退休」缺乏數據支持

這是對台灣投資社群最具衝擊性的發現。0050 的提領存活率（97.1%）顯著高於 0056（95.8%），z=3.45，p=0.0006。不僅如此，0050 的期末餘額中位數（13,135 萬）幾乎是 0056（7,322 萬）的兩倍。

為什麼？因為提領退休金的本質是「每年從投資組合中取出一定金額」，不管這個金額來自配息還是賣股，投資組合都減少了相同的金額。真正決定提領存活率的是投資組合的總報酬——0050 年化 12.3% 遠高於 0056 的 7.7%。在 30 年的複利效果下，這個差距被極度放大。

「靠配息退休不需要賣股」這個心理安慰，掩蓋了一個簡單的數學事實：低總報酬的投資組合，無論提領方式為何，更容易提前耗盡。投資人不應被「配息殖利率 5%」的數字所迷惑，而是應該關注「含資本增值的總報酬率」。

Finding 4: 0056 "Living Off Dividends" Lacks Data Support

This is the most impactful finding for Taiwan's investment community. 0050 survival (97.1%) is significantly higher than 0056 (95.8%), z=3.45, p=0.0006. Moreover, 0050's median terminal balance (13,1350K) is nearly double 0056's (7,3220K).

Why? Because retirement withdrawal fundamentally means "removing a fixed amount from the portfolio each year" — whether from dividends or share sales, the portfolio decreases by the same amount. What determines withdrawal survival is total return: 0050 at 12.3% far exceeds 0056 at 7.7%. Over 30 years of compounding, this gap becomes enormous.

The psychological comfort of "not selling shares" obscures a simple math fact: lower total-return portfolios deplete faster regardless of withdrawal mechanism. Investors should not be misled by "5% dividend yield" — focus on "total return including capital appreciation."

4.2 序列風險深度分析4.2 Sequence-of-Returns Risk Analysis

餘額路徑圖揭示的最重要現象是「序列風險」（Sequence-of-Returns Risk）：即使兩個退休者面對相同的 30 年平均報酬，但因報酬出現的順序不同，結局可能天差地別。

為什麼序列風險對退休提領者特別致命？

  • 累積階段 vs 提領階段的不對稱：在累積階段（定期投入），早期的下跌反而是好事（可以便宜買入更多單位，即「微笑曲線」）。但在提領階段，早期的下跌是災難——投資組合縮水的同時還在被提領，雙重打擊使資產以加速度消耗。
  • 「死亡螺旋」機制：固定金額提領時，投資組合越小，提領佔比就越大。例如 1,000 萬的 4%（40 萬）vs 500 萬的 40 萬（8%），後者的提領壓力是前者的兩倍。這種正回饋循環一旦啟動就難以逆轉。
  • 回顧歷史上最危險的退休時機：1929 年（大蕭條前夕）、1966 年（滯脹開端）、2000 年（網路泡沫）——這些年份退休的人恰好在提領初期遭遇連續多年的市場下跌。

動態調整策略（Guardrails）之所以有效，正是因為它直接對抗序列風險：市場不好時減少提領，讓投資組合有更多「喘息空間」等待反彈。這 20% 的彈性空間，就是對付序列風險最有效的「保險」。

The most important phenomenon revealed by balance path charts is "sequence-of-returns risk": even if two retirees face identical 30-year average returns, different ordering of those returns can lead to vastly different outcomes.

Why is sequence risk especially lethal for retirees?

  • Accumulation vs. withdrawal asymmetry: During accumulation (regular investing), early declines are actually beneficial (buy more units cheaply, the "smile curve"). But during withdrawal, early declines are catastrophic — the portfolio shrinks while being withdrawn from, a double hit that accelerates depletion.
  • "Death spiral" mechanism: With fixed-amount withdrawal, as the portfolio shrinks, the withdrawal ratio increases. E.g., 4% of 10M (400K) vs 400K from 5M (8%) — the latter has twice the withdrawal pressure. This positive feedback loop, once started, is nearly irreversible.
  • History's worst retirement timing: 1929 (pre-Depression), 1966 (stagflation onset), 2000 (dot-com peak) — retirees in these years faced consecutive years of decline at the start of withdrawal.

Dynamic guardrails work precisely because they counter sequence risk: reducing withdrawals when markets decline gives the portfolio "breathing room" to recover. That 20% flexibility is the most effective "insurance" against sequence risk.

4.3 研究限制4.3 Limitations

本研究有以下重要限制，讀者在解讀結論時應納入考量：

  • 台灣數據長度不足：0050 僅 22 年（2003-2025）、0056 僅 18 年（2007-2025）。Bootstrap 從短歷史中重抽組合成 30 年路徑，隱含假設未來報酬的分佈特性與過去相似。若台灣市場的報酬特性在未來出現結構性改變（如經濟成長放緩），模擬結果可能過度樂觀。
  • 未計入交易成本和稅務：實際提領時需考慮買賣手續費、交易稅、以及可能的所得稅。這些成本雖然較小（每年約 0.01-0.05%），但 30 年累積效果不可忽略。
  • 0056 配息模擬的精確度：0056 的歷史配息數據可能存在不完整或估計值的問題，實際的配息提領策略表現可能與模擬結果有偏差。
  • Bootstrap 方法的限制：Block Bootstrap 假設報酬的時間結構可被打亂重組，這可能低估極端序列風險（如連續 10 年的熊市）。Block size 為 12 個月，保留了年內的相關性，但無法捕捉跨年的長期趨勢（如 1970 年代的長期滯脹）。
  • 通膨模型簡化：台灣模擬使用固定 1.5%/年的通膨假設，但實際通膨會隨經濟環境波動。高通膨環境下，固定金額提領的購買力侵蝕更嚴重。
  • 行為因素未納入：模擬假設退休者嚴格遵守提領計畫，但現實中的恐慌賣出、過度消費、或因市場下跌而完全停止提領等行為偏差，可能顯著影響實際結果。
  • 單一退休期間：本研究僅模擬 30 年退休期間。對於 FIRE 族群（30-40 歲退休），可能需要 50-60 年的退休期間，安全提領率會更低。

This study has several important limitations that readers should consider when interpreting conclusions:

  • Insufficient Taiwan data length: 0050 has 22 years (2003-2025), 0056 only 18 (2007-2025). Bootstrap resamples from short history to create 30-year paths, assuming future return distributions resemble the past. Structural changes (e.g., slowing economic growth) could make simulations overly optimistic.
  • No transaction costs or taxes: Actual withdrawals involve commissions, transaction taxes, and potential income taxes. While small (0.01-0.05% annually), 30-year cumulative effects are not negligible.
  • 0056 dividend simulation accuracy: Historical dividend data may be incomplete or estimated, and actual dividend-based withdrawal performance could deviate from simulation.
  • Bootstrap methodology limitations: Block Bootstrap assumes return sequences can be reshuffled, potentially underestimating extreme sequence risk (e.g., 10 consecutive bear years). 12-month blocks preserve intra-year correlation but miss long-term trends (e.g., 1970s stagflation).
  • Simplified inflation model: Taiwan simulations use fixed 1.5%/year inflation. Actual inflation fluctuates; high-inflation periods erode fixed-amount purchasing power more severely.
  • Behavioral factors excluded: Simulations assume strict adherence to plans, but real-world panic selling, overspending, or withdrawal freezes could significantly alter outcomes.
  • Single retirement horizon: Only 30-year horizons are simulated. FIRE retirees (retiring at 30-40) may need 50-60 year horizons, requiring even lower withdrawal rates.

4.4 實務啟示4.4 Practical Implications

對保守型退休者的建議

  • 提領率設定 3-3.5%：而非 4%。以 1,000 萬退休金為例，年提領 30-35 萬（月提領 2.5-2.9 萬）。這個金額可能不高，但確保 30 年幾乎不會破產。
  • 保留 1-2 年的現金緩衝：在投資組合之外另外持有 1-2 年的生活費現金，熊市時從現金提領，避免在低點賣出。
  • 考慮年金搭配：將部分退休金購買即期年金，確保基本生活費有保障，剩餘部分才進行投資提領。

對積極型退休者（FIRE 族群）的建議

  • 採用 Guardrails 策略：願意在市場不好時減少 20% 支出，就能大幅降低破產風險。存活率從 89.9% 提升至 94.4%。
  • 保持收入彈性：FIRE 並不等於「完全不工作」。保留兼職或接案能力，在市場低迷時補充收入，是最實際的風險管理。
  • 全球分散配置：不要只投資台灣或美國單一市場。全球股票指數 + 全球債券的配置，能有效降低單一市場風險。

對所有退休者的共同建議

  • 不要迷信配息：選擇投資標的時，應以「總報酬」（資本增值 + 配息）為依據，而非只看配息率。
  • 定期檢視（至少每年一次）：評估剩餘資產、調整提領金額、考慮是否需要改變策略。「設定即忘記」是退休理財最危險的態度。
  • 注意通膨：固定金額提領的購買力會隨時間下降。確保提領金額有隨 CPI 調整的機制。

For Conservative Retirees

  • Set withdrawal rate at 3-3.5%: Not 4%. For a NTD 10M portfolio, that is NTD 300K-350K/year (NTD 25K-29K/month). Modest, but virtually ensures 30-year survival.
  • Maintain 1-2 year cash buffer: Hold 1-2 years of living expenses in cash outside the investment portfolio. Withdraw from cash during bear markets to avoid selling low.
  • Consider annuities: Purchase an immediate annuity for basic living expenses, then invest-and-withdraw only the remainder.

For Aggressive Retirees (FIRE Community)

  • Adopt Guardrails: Willingness to reduce spending by 20% in bad markets dramatically reduces ruin risk. Survival rises from 89.9% to 94.4%.
  • Maintain income flexibility: FIRE does not mean "never work again." Keeping part-time or freelance capacity to supplement income during downturns is the most practical risk management.
  • Diversify globally: Do not invest solely in Taiwan or the US. Global stock + global bond allocation effectively reduces single-market risk.

For All Retirees

  • Do not chase dividends: Choose investments based on total return (capital gains + dividends), not dividend yield alone.
  • Review regularly (at least annually): Assess remaining assets, adjust withdrawal amounts, consider strategy changes. "Set and forget" is the most dangerous retirement mindset.
  • Mind inflation: Fixed-amount purchasing power erodes over time. Ensure withdrawals have a CPI adjustment mechanism.
