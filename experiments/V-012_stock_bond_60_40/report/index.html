V-012：股債 60/40 永遠有效？V-012: Does the 60/40 Portfolio Always Work?

研究問題：「60/40 股債配置」被譽為經典投資組合，真的在所有環境下都有效嗎？2022 年聯準會激進升息讓股債齊跌，60/40 經歷數十年來最差表現。本研究以美國 150 年以上的 Shiller 數據，系統性檢驗 60/40 在不同利率環境、股債相關性結構性變化下的真實表現。

Research Question: The 60/40 stock/bond portfolio is considered a classic allocation. Does it really work in all environments? The 2022 Fed rate hikes saw stocks and bonds fall together, giving 60/40 its worst performance in decades. We systematically examine 150+ years of US Shiller data across different interest rate regimes and structural shifts in stock-bond correlations.

摘要

「60% 股票 + 40% 債券」自 1950 年代以來被視為投資組合的「黃金比例」，幾乎所有退休基金與理財顧問都以此為起點。然而，2022 年的股債齊跌事件打破了「股跌債漲」的傳統假設，引發了 60/40 是否已經過時的激烈辯論。

本研究以 Robert Shiller 的百年數據集（1871 年至今），從四個面向全面檢驗 60/40 配置：(1) 不同利率環境下的表現差異；(2) 股債相關性的結構性變化；(3) 與其他配置比例及多資產組合的比較；(4) 再平衡策略的效果。同時以台灣市場（0050 + 定存）進行在地驗證。

結果顯示：60/40 在過去 97.5 年的全期間表現優異（年化 8.5%，Sharpe ratio 0.890），但這個「平均數」掩蓋了巨大的環境差異——在 1982-2020 年的債券大牛市中 Sharpe 高達 1.30，但在 1965-1981 年的高通膨時代僅 0.59。Fisher z 檢定確認 2000 年前後的股債相關性存在顯著的結構性轉變（p<0.001）。

Abstract

The "60% stock + 40% bond" allocation has been considered the "golden ratio" since the 1950s, adopted by virtually all pension funds and financial advisors. However, the 2022 stock-bond co-decline shattered the "stocks down, bonds up" assumption, sparking fierce debate about whether 60/40 is obsolete.

Using Robert Shiller's century-spanning dataset (1871-present), we comprehensively examine 60/40 from four angles: (1) performance across interest rate regimes; (2) structural shifts in stock-bond correlation; (3) comparison with alternative allocations and multi-asset portfolios; (4) rebalancing effects. We also validate locally with Taiwan market data (0050 + bank deposits).

Results: 60/40 performed well over the full 97.5 years (annualized 8.5%, Sharpe 0.890), but this "average" masks enormous regime-dependence — Sharpe reached 1.30 during the 1982-2020 bond bull but only 0.59 in the 1965-1981 high-inflation era. Fisher z-test confirms a significant structural shift in stock-bond correlation around 2000 (p<0.001).

上圖解讀：以對數尺度呈現五種股債配置從 1926 年至今的累積報酬。100% 股票年化報酬最高（10.2%），但波動度也最大（15.4%），歷史最大回檔達 -81.8%（大蕭條時期）。60/40 年化 8.5%，波動度大幅降低至 9.5%，Sharpe ratio 0.890——是所有配置中風險調整後報酬最佳的。值得注意的是，雖然犧牲了約 1.7 個百分點的年化報酬，但最大回檔從 -81.8% 縮減至 -59.8%。

Chart interpretation: Log-scale cumulative returns of five allocations from 1926. 100% stock has the highest return (10.2%) but also highest volatility (15.4%) with a maximum drawdown of -81.8% (Great Depression). 60/40 returns 8.5% with significantly lower volatility (9.5%), Sharpe ratio 0.890 — the best risk-adjusted return among all allocations. While sacrificing ~1.7 pp of annualized return, max drawdown is reduced from -81.8% to -59.8%.

主要發現Key Findings

  1. 60/40 並非「永遠有效」，效果高度依賴利率環境。在債券牛市（1982-2020）Sharpe ratio 為 1.30，但高通膨（1965-81）僅 0.59，升息週期（2022-23）為 -0.51。Block Bootstrap 檢定顯示環境間差異統計上顯著（H1: p=0.029）。
  2. 股債相關性在 2000 年前後發生結構性轉變。2000 年前相關係數約 -0.05~+0.20（正相關，分散效果有限），2000 年後轉為 +0.04（負相關，分散效果強），2022 升息時又回升至 +0.46。Fisher z 檢定確認結構性差異（p<0.001）。
  3. 60/40 整體風險調整後報酬仍然優於 100% 股票。全期間 60/40 Sharpe 0.890 顯著優於 100% 股票的 0.659（Bootstrap 差異 p<0.001）。最佳配置約在 25% 股票處。
  4. 2022 年是 60/40 的最大挑戰。升息衝擊期間 60/40 下跌 17.7%，債券部分不但沒有保護作用，反而貢獻了額外損失（-15.7%）。「股債齊跌」使得分散投資的核心邏輯失效。
  5. 60/40 does NOT always work — performance is highly regime-dependent. Bond bull (1982-2020) Sharpe 1.30, high inflation (1965-81) 0.59, rate hike (2022-23) -0.51. Bootstrap test shows significant regime differences (H1: p=0.029).
  6. Stock-bond correlation shifted structurally around 2000. Pre-2000 ~-0.05 to +0.20 (positive, limited diversification), post-2000 turned to +0.04 (negative, strong diversification), 2022 rate hike returned to +0.46. Fisher z confirms structural shift (p<0.001).
  7. 60/40 still beats 100% stock on risk-adjusted basis overall. Full-period 60/40 Sharpe 0.890 significantly exceeds 100% stock 0.659 (Bootstrap diff p<0.001). Optimal allocation at ~25% stock.
  8. 2022 was 60/40's greatest challenge. During the rate shock, 60/40 fell 17.7%; the bond portion not only failed to protect but contributed additional losses (-15.7%). "Stocks and bonds falling together" broke the core diversification logic.
