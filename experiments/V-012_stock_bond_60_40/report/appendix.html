A.1 各配置月報酬分布A.1 Monthly Return Distribution

為什麼要看報酬分布？常態分布假設是許多投資模型的基礎（包括 Markowitz 均值-變異數最佳化），但實際報酬往往偏離常態——瞭解實際分布的形狀有助於正確評估風險。

下圖呈現 100% 股票、100% 債券、60/40 的月報酬分布直方圖。虛線為平均值。幾個值得注意的特徵：

  • 股票報酬：呈現輕微右偏（skewness = 0.54），但峰態（kurtosis）高達 18.6——遠超常態分布的 3.0，意味著極端月份（大漲或大跌）的發生頻率遠超「鐘形曲線」的預測。最佳月份報酬超過 +51%，最差月份接近 -26%。
  • 債券報酬：集中在零附近，但同樣具有高峰態（11.2），且右偏（skewness = 1.03）。債券的尾端風險主要集中在利率劇烈變動的月份。
  • 60/40 組合：分布形狀介於兩者之間，尾部風險有所降低但未消除。

Jarque-Bera 和 Shapiro-Wilk 常態性檢定都強烈拒絕了常態假設（p < 0.001），這也是本研究選用 Block Bootstrap（無需常態假設）的重要原因。

Why look at return distributions? Normal distribution is the foundation of many models (including Markowitz mean-variance), but actual returns often deviate — understanding actual distribution shapes helps correctly assess risk.

Histograms of monthly returns for 100% stock, 100% bond, and 60/40. Dashed line = mean. Notable characteristics:

  • Stock returns: Slight positive skew (0.54), but extremely high kurtosis (18.6) — far exceeding the normal distribution's 3.0, meaning extreme months occur far more often than the bell curve predicts. Best month > +51%, worst near -26%.
  • Bond returns: Centered near zero but also high kurtosis (11.2) with positive skew (1.03). Tail risk concentrates in months of dramatic rate changes.
  • 60/40 mix: Distribution shape between both; tail risk reduced but not eliminated.

Both Jarque-Bera and Shapiro-Wilk normality tests strongly reject normality (p < 0.001), justifying our use of Block Bootstrap (no normality assumption required).

A.2 滾動 10 年報酬比較A.2 Rolling 10-Year Return Comparison

為什麼用滾動 10 年報酬？單期間的平均報酬可能具有誤導性——滾動分析揭示了報酬隨時間的變化軌跡，讓我們看到「同樣的配置，在不同時期可能有天壤之別」。10 年窗口常被視為中長期投資的最低合理持有期間。

滾動 10 年年化報酬顯示，100% 股票在大多數時期報酬較高，但在三個關鍵時期，60/40 的表現接近甚至優於純股票：

  • 1930 年代大蕭條：股票跌幅超過 80%，10 年滾動報酬一度轉負。60/40 憑藉債券部位提供了更穩定的 10 年報酬。
  • 1970 年代滯漲：通膨侵蝕實質報酬，股票名目報酬雖正但實質接近零。不過在這個時期，債券同樣受到通膨衝擊，60/40 的優勢有限。
  • 2000 年代失落十年：經歷網路泡沫和金融海嘯兩次重大回調，100% 股票的 10 年報酬接近零甚至為負。60/40 在這個時期的表現明顯更穩定。

Why rolling 10-year returns? Single-period averages can be misleading — rolling analysis reveals how returns evolve, showing that "the same allocation can perform vastly differently across periods." 10-year windows are commonly considered the minimum reasonable holding period.

Rolling 10-year returns show 100% stock usually leads, but in three key periods, 60/40 was close to or better than pure stock:

  • 1930s Great Depression: Stocks fell >80%, 10-year rolling returns turned negative. 60/40 provided more stable 10-year returns via the bond component.
  • 1970s Stagflation: Inflation eroded real returns; stocks nominally positive but real near zero. However, bonds were also hit by inflation, limiting 60/40's advantage.
  • 2000s Lost Decade: Dot-com crash and GFC caused two major drawdowns. 100% stock 10-year returns approached zero or negative. 60/40 was notably more stable.

A.3 擴展配置比較A.3 Extended Allocation Comparison

分析目的：探索「後 60/40」時代，加入第三類資產是否能改善投資組合。由於黃金 ETF（GLD）自 2004 年 11 月才開始交易，擴展分析僅涵蓋 ETF 時代。

下圖為 ETF 時代（GLD 上市後）的擴展配置風險-報酬散佈圖。永久投資組合（Permanent Portfolio, 25/25/25/25）在此期間的 Sharpe ratio 達到 1.126，最大回檔僅 -12.3%——兩者均為所有配置中最優。四資產配置（60/20/10/10）Sharpe 1.025，最大回檔 -26.1%。

解讀注意：此期間包含 2008-2011 年的黃金歷史性大牛市（金價從約 $800 漲至 $1,900），以及 2009-2020 年全球央行量化寬鬆帶來的低利率環境。永久投資組合的優異表現可能部分反映了這些特殊條件，不應直接外推至未來。

Purpose: Explore whether adding a third asset class improves portfolios in the "post-60/40" era. Since GLD ETF started trading November 2004, extended analysis covers only the ETF era.

The chart shows risk-return scatter for ETF-era allocations. Permanent Portfolio (25/25/25/25) achieves Sharpe 1.126 with max DD only -12.3% — both best among all allocations. Four-asset (60/20/10/10) has Sharpe 1.025, max DD -26.1%.

Interpretation note: This period includes gold's historic bull (2008-2011, ~$800 to ~$1,900) and the 2009-2020 QE low-rate environment. Permanent Portfolio's outperformance may partly reflect these special conditions and should not be directly extrapolated.

A.4 台灣驗證A.4 Taiwan Validation

分析目的：檢驗 60/40 概念能否「在地化」——以台灣 0050 ETF 代替美國股票、台灣定存代替美國公債，模擬台灣投資人可實際執行的 60/40 配置。

結果顯示，台灣在地版 60/40 的分散效果遠不如美國版本。0050（100% 股票）年化報酬 12.3%，Sharpe 0.663，最大回檔 -53.9%。加入 40% 定存後，年化報酬降至 8.6%，Sharpe 微升至 0.771，最大回檔改善至 -34.0%。

定存部位僅提供約 1.4% 的年化報酬，幾乎不波動。它扮演的是「現金減震器」而非「債券避風港」的角色——在 0050 大跌時，定存不會像美國公債一樣上漲來「對沖」股票虧損，它只是用零報酬的部位來稀釋虧損幅度。這是台灣投資人在建構配置時必須理解的根本差異。

Purpose: Test if 60/40 can be "localized" — using Taiwan 0050 ETF as stock proxy and bank deposits as bond proxy to simulate a 60/40 allocation executable by Taiwan investors.

Results show Taiwan's local 60/40 provides far less diversification than the US version. 0050 (100% stock): 12.3% return, Sharpe 0.663, max DD -53.9%. Adding 40% deposits: return drops to 8.6%, Sharpe rises slightly to 0.771, max DD improves to -34.0%.

Deposits yield only ~1.4% annually with near-zero volatility — acting as a "cash dampener" rather than a "bond safe haven." When 0050 drops sharply, deposits don't rally like US Treasuries to offset losses — they merely dilute losses with zero-return allocation. This fundamental difference is critical for Taiwan investors to understand.

A.6 數據來源與方法論說明A.6 Data Source & Methodology Notes

核心數據：Robert Shiller CAPE Dataset，由耶魯大學 Robert Shiller 教授維護。數據涵蓋 1871 年至今的 S&P 綜合指數月度報酬、股利率、10 年期國庫券殖利率、CPI 等。這是全球歷史最悠久的金融數據集之一，廣泛被學術研究引用。

參考數據：台灣 0050 ETF 日線數據來自 data/reference/（已做除權息調整），台灣定存利率來自央行公告，黃金 GLD ETF 來自 yfinance。

再現性：所有分析使用固定隨機種子（random_state=42），數據預處理和分析腳本均包含在本實驗的 src/ 目錄中。完整的分析流程可透過 python run_all.py 一鍵重現。

Core data: Robert Shiller CAPE Dataset, maintained by Professor Robert Shiller at Yale. Covers S&P Composite monthly returns, dividend yields, 10-year Treasury yields, and CPI since 1871. One of the world's longest financial datasets, widely cited in academic research.

Reference data: Taiwan 0050 ETF daily prices from data/reference/ (dividend-adjusted), Taiwan deposit rates from central bank announcements, gold GLD ETF from yfinance.

Reproducibility: All analyses use fixed random seed (random_state=42). Preprocessing and analysis scripts are in this experiment's src/ directory. Full pipeline is reproducible via python run_all.py.

資料來源：Robert Shiller CAPE Dataset, yfinance, data/reference/
