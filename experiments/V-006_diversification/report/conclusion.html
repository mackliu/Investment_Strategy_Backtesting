V-006：分散投資就能降低風險？V-006: Does Diversification Always Reduce Risk?

結論Conclusion

回到最初的研究問題：「不要把雞蛋放在同一個籃子裡」這個建議，在真實數據面前站得住腳嗎？答案是：大致上是對的，但需要重要的修正。

Returning to our original research question: does the advice "don't put all eggs in one basket" hold up against real data? The answer: mostly yes, but with important qualifications.

5.1 核心結論5.1 Core Conclusions

  1. 分散投資確實降低風險，但有限度。五資產組合波動度 10.0%（vs 台股 18.8%），MaxDD -24.2%（vs -53.9%），Sharpe Ratio 0.92（vs 0.63）。在正常市場環境中，分散投資顯著改善風險調整後報酬。但在系統性危機中，保護效果大幅衰減——分散投資是「有效的減震器」而非「無敵護盾」。
  2. Correlation Breakdown 是分散投資的結構性限制。0/10 對資產在危機時相關性顯著上升（平均變化 -0.031）。在最需要保護的時候，保護效果反而最弱——這是所有分散投資策略都無法完全克服的根本性問題。
  3. 跨資產分散遠優於同市場分散。在同一個市場裡買再多股票（10-20 檔即達飽和），也只能消除個股風險；只有跨入不同資產類別（特別是與股票呈負相關或低相關的債券和黃金），才能實質降低系統性風險暴露。「買更多台股」和「配置到不同資產」是兩個層次完全不同的分散。
  4. 邊際效益遞減，5 個資產類別已足夠。前 2-3 個資產類別提供絕大部分分散效益，之後的改善幅度遞減。追求過度複雜的配置（如 10+ 種資產）不太可能帶來重大改善，反而增加管理成本和認知負擔。
  5. 「分散投資能降低風險」需要重新表述。更準確的表述是：「分散投資能降低正常市場的波動，但在極端市場中保護效果因相關性飆升而大幅衰減；跨資產分散遠比同市場分散有效；邊際效益遞減；5 個資產類別已能捕捉大部分效益。」
  1. Diversification reduces risk, but with limits. Five-asset volatility 10.0% (vs Taiwan 18.8%), MaxDD -24.2% (vs -53.9%), Sharpe 0.92 (vs 0.63). In normal markets, diversification significantly improves risk-adjusted returns. But during systemic crises, protection weakens substantially — diversification is an 'effective shock absorber' not an 'invincible shield.'
  2. Correlation Breakdown is a structural limitation. 0/10 asset pairs show significantly higher correlations during crises (avg change -0.031). Protection weakens precisely when needed most — a fundamental problem no diversification strategy can fully overcome.
  3. Cross-asset far superior to same-market diversification. Buying more stocks in the same market (saturating at 10-20) only eliminates idiosyncratic risk; only different asset classes (especially bonds and gold with negative/low stock correlations) meaningfully reduce systematic risk. 'Buying more Taiwan stocks' and 'allocating to different assets' are entirely different levels of diversification.
  4. Diminishing marginal benefits; 5 asset classes suffice. First 2-3 classes provide the vast majority of benefits, with diminishing returns thereafter. Overly complex allocations (10+ assets) unlikely to significantly improve outcomes while increasing management costs and cognitive burden.
  5. 'Diversification reduces risk' needs restating. More accurately: 'Diversification reduces normal market volatility but protection weakens significantly during extreme events due to correlation surges; cross-asset is far more effective than same-market; marginal benefits diminish; 5 asset classes capture most benefits.'

一句話總結：分散投資是有效的「減震器」而非「無敵護盾」——它能讓你在大多數時候睡得更安穩，但遇到系統性海嘯時，所有船隻仍會一起搖晃。

One-line summary: Diversification is an effective "shock absorber" not an "invincible shield" — it helps you sleep better most of the time, but during systemic tsunamis, all boats still rock together.

5.2 研究限制5.2 Limitations

  • 期間偏差：分析期間 2006-05 ~ 2025-10，涵蓋美國史上最長多頭市場（2009-2020）和前所未有的央行量化寬鬆政策，可能導致結果無法完全推廣到其他歷史時期。
  • 資產類別有限：僅使用 5 個資產類別，未納入新興市場股票、原物料、加密貨幣、通膨連結債券（TIPS）等。更廣泛的資產覆蓋可能改變結論。
  • 交易成本與稅務：年度再平衡忽略了交易成本（台股手續費+交易稅、海外匯款費）和稅務影響（海外所得課稅）。在實際執行中，這些成本會侵蝕部分分散效益。
  • 匯率未避險：所有海外資產以台幣未避險計價。匯率避險會改變跨國分散的風險-報酬特性。
  • 存活者偏差：個股模擬使用當前存在的大型股，已下市或縮小規模的公司被排除，可能低估實際風險。
  • 靜態權重：所有組合使用固定目標權重，未考慮動態調整策略（如風險平價、趨勢跟隨）的可能優勢。
  • Period bias: Analysis period 2006-05 ~ 2025-10 includes the longest US bull market (2009-2020) and unprecedented central bank QE, potentially limiting generalizability to other historical periods.
  • Limited asset classes: Only 5 classes; excludes EM equities, commodities, crypto, TIPS, etc. Broader coverage might alter conclusions.
  • Transaction costs and taxes: Annual rebalancing ignores trading costs (Taiwan fees+tax, overseas wire fees) and tax implications (overseas income tax). These erode diversification benefits in practice.
  • Unhedged FX: All overseas assets in unhedged TWD terms. FX hedging would change cross-border diversification risk-return characteristics.
  • Survivorship bias: Stock simulation uses currently existing large caps; delisted or shrunken companies are excluded, potentially underestimating actual risk.
  • Static weights: All portfolios use fixed target weights, not considering dynamic strategies (risk parity, trend following) that might perform better.

5.3 未來研究方向5.3 Future Research

  • 尾部風險對沖策略：在分散投資的基礎上加入 PUT options overlay，量化「花多少成本」能換到「極端情境下多少額外保護」。
  • 動態 vs 靜態配置：比較風險平價（Risk Parity）、動量配置（Momentum-based）和本研究的靜態等權配置，哪一種在含交易成本後表現更好？
  • 擴展資產類別：納入新興市場股票、原物料、加密貨幣、通膨連結債券（TIPS），觀察分散效益是否能進一步提升，以及邊際效益遞減的「拐點」在哪裡。
  • 匯率避險效果：系統性比較「有避險」和「無避險」的跨國分散效果，找出對台灣投資人而言的最適避險比率。
  • 再平衡頻率最佳化：比較月度、季度、年度、觸發式（偏離超過 5% 時）四種再平衡策略在含交易成本後的淨效果。
  • 個人化因素：將投資人的年齡、風險承受度、稅務身分等納入考量，研究「最適分散程度」是否因人而異。例如，退休族群可能需要更多債券配置，而年輕人可能承受得起更高的股票比重。
  • Tail risk hedging: Add PUT options overlay to diversified portfolios; quantify "how much cost" buys "how much additional extreme scenario protection."
  • Dynamic vs static allocation: Compare Risk Parity, Momentum-based, and this study's static equal-weight — which performs better net of transaction costs?
  • Expand asset classes: Add EM equities, commodities, crypto, TIPS; observe whether diversification benefits improve further and where the diminishing returns "inflection point" lies.
  • FX hedging effects: Systematically compare hedged vs unhedged international diversification; find the optimal hedging ratio for Taiwanese investors.
  • Rebalancing frequency optimization: Compare monthly, quarterly, annual, and threshold-based (when deviation exceeds 5%) rebalancing strategies net of transaction costs.
  • Personalization factors: Incorporate investor age, risk tolerance, tax status; study whether "optimal diversification" varies by individual. Retirees may need more bonds; young investors may tolerate higher equity allocation.
