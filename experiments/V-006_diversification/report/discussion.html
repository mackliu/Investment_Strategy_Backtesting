V-006：分散投資就能降低風險？V-006: Does Diversification Always Reduce Risk?

討論Discussion

4.1 主要發現4.1 Key Findings

發現一：分散投資在正常市場確實有效。五資產等權組合年化波動度 10.0%，較 100% 台股的 18.8% 降低 8.8 個百分點。Sharpe Ratio 從 0.63 提升至 0.92——每承受一單位風險獲得的報酬更多。用生活化比喻：100% 台股像是只穿一件外套出門，天氣好的時候很舒服，但突然下雨就全身濕透；五資產分散就像帶了外套 + 雨傘 + 帽子——不是每件都一直用得到，但整體而言你更不容易被天氣「傷到」。

發現二：在極端市場中保護效果大幅衰減。危機期間 0/10 對資產的相關性顯著上升（平均變化 -0.031），五資產組合在最嚴重的情境中仍遭受 -24.2% 的最大回撤。這與 Longin & Solnik (2001) 的發現完全一致：相關性在「壞日子」飆升。用比喻來說：分散投資就像防震設計——小地震時房子穩穩的，但遇到 921 等級的大地震，防震設計能減輕損害但不能完全免疫。

發現三：跨資產分散遠優於同市場分散。從 1 檔台股增加到 50 檔，波動度的降幅有限——因為你只是在消除「某一家公司出問題」的風險，而所有台股都暴露在相同的台灣經濟和全球連動風險下。但加入債券和黃金等不同資產類別，能實質降低系統性風險暴露，因為這些資產對不同的經濟情境有不同的反應。這支持了 Ilmanen (2011) 的觀點：真正的分散是持有「對不同經濟情境有不同反應」的資產。

發現四：邊際效益遞減。前 2-3 個資產類別（特別是加入債券的那一步）提供最大的分散效益，之後的改善幅度遞減。5 個資產類別已能捕捉大部分可獲得的分散效益。追加第 6、7 個類別（如原物料、加密貨幣）不太可能帶來重大改善，卻會增加管理的複雜度和交易成本。就像烹飪時加調味料——鹽和胡椒讓味道大幅提升，但繼續加第 10 種調味料時，改善幅度已微乎其微，還可能弄巧成拙。

發現五：債券和黃金是關鍵分散工具。SPY 與 TLT 呈負相關（約 -0.12），SPY 與 GLD 低相關（約 -0.01）。含有這些資產的組合在危機中表現相對較好——不是「不虧」，而是「虧比較少」。特別是黃金在 2020 年 COVID 危機和 2022 年通膨危機中都展現了良好的避險功能。

Finding 1: Diversification works in normal markets. Five-asset EW portfolio volatility 10.0%, down 8.8 pp from 100% Taiwan's 18.8%. Sharpe improved from 0.63 to 0.92 — more return per unit of risk. Analogy: 100% Taiwan is like going out in just a jacket — comfortable in nice weather but soaked in sudden rain; five-asset diversification is like jacket + umbrella + hat — not all used constantly, but overall you're better protected.

Finding 2: Protection weakens significantly in extreme markets. During crises, 0/10 asset pairs show significantly higher correlations (avg change -0.031). The five-asset portfolio still suffered -24.2% max drawdown in the worst scenario. Consistent with Longin & Solnik (2001): correlations surge on 'bad days.' Diversification is like earthquake-resistant design — the building holds during small quakes but a magnitude-9 earthquake still causes damage despite the protection.

Finding 3: Cross-asset far superior to same-market diversification. Adding Taiwan stocks from 1 to 50 has limited volatility reduction — you're only eliminating 'one company goes wrong' risk while remaining exposed to Taiwan's economic and global linkage risks. Adding bonds and gold genuinely reduces systematic risk exposure because these assets respond differently to economic scenarios. This supports Ilmanen (2011): true diversification means holding assets with 'different responses to different economic scenarios.'

Finding 4: Diminishing marginal benefits. The first 2-3 asset classes (especially adding bonds) provide the largest diversification benefit, with diminishing returns thereafter. Five asset classes capture most achievable benefits. A 6th or 7th class (commodities, crypto) likely adds minimal improvement while increasing complexity and costs. Like adding spices in cooking — salt and pepper dramatically improve flavor, but by the 10th spice, improvement is negligible and may backfire.

Finding 5: Bonds and gold are key diversifiers. SPY-TLT negative correlation (~-0.12), SPY-GLD low correlation (~-0.01). Portfolios containing these assets perform relatively better in crises — not 'no loss' but 'less loss.' Gold particularly demonstrated strong hedging during both the 2020 COVID crisis and 2022 inflation crisis.

4.2 Correlation Breakdown：分散投資的阿基里斯腱4.2 Correlation Breakdown: Diversification's Achilles Heel

本研究最重要的發現是：分散投資的保護效果在最需要它的時候（市場崩盤）反而最弱。這不是分散投資「失敗」，而是它的本質限制。用醫學比喻：分散投資就像疫苗——能大幅降低日常感冒（正常波動）的機率，但遇到全新變種病毒（前所未見的系統性危機）時，保護力會下降。這不代表疫苗沒用，而是要對它的保護範圍有正確的理解。

為什麼會發生 Correlation Breakdown？根本原因是：

  • 恐慌的傳染性：在系統性風險事件（如全球金融危機）中，投資人的行為從「理性評估個別資產」切換為「不管三七二十一先賣再說」（flight to safety）。這種恐慌性拋售是不分青紅皂白的——好公司和壞公司、股票和商品，一律被賣出換成現金或短期公債。
  • 槓桿的連鎖效應：許多機構投資人使用槓桿（借錢投資）。當某一類資產大跌觸發追繳保證金時，他們必須賣出其他資產來補繳——這導致原本不相關的資產被「強制連動」。2008 年的雷曼破產就是典型案例。
  • 「安全資產」也有例外：即使是傳統避險資產（如公債），在某些特殊危機中也可能同步下跌。2022 年是最好的例子：央行暴力升息同時打壓了股票（因為未來獲利被更高利率折現）和債券（因為利率上升導致債券價格下跌），讓 60/40 這個「黃金配置」遭遇了歷史性的雙殺。

因此，「分散投資就能降低風險」這個論點需要加上重要的但書：分散投資能降低正常市場的波動，但無法完全防禦系統性崩盤。投資人不應將分散投資視為「保證不虧」的魔法，而是「大多數時候有效、極端情況下保護力會打折」的風險管理工具。

The study's most important finding: diversification protection weakens precisely when needed most (during crashes). This isn't diversification 'failing' but its inherent limitation. Medical analogy: diversification is like a vaccine — greatly reducing everyday colds (normal volatility) but losing efficacy against novel virus variants (unprecedented systemic crises). This doesn't mean vaccines are useless, but we must correctly understand their protection scope.

Why does Correlation Breakdown occur? Root causes:

  • Contagion of panic: During systematic risk events (e.g., GFC), investor behavior switches from "rationally evaluating individual assets" to "sell everything regardless" (flight to safety). This panic selling is indiscriminate — good and bad companies, stocks and commodities, all sold for cash or short-term bonds.
  • Leverage chain reaction: Many institutional investors use leverage (borrowed money). When one asset class crashes and triggers margin calls, they must sell other assets to meet requirements — forcing previously uncorrelated assets into "forced correlation." Lehman's 2008 bankruptcy was a textbook case.
  • "Safe assets" have exceptions: Even traditional safe havens (bonds) can decline simultaneously in some crises. 2022 was the prime example: aggressive rate hikes simultaneously hurt stocks (higher discount rates for future earnings) and bonds (rising rates lower bond prices), dealing the "golden" 60/40 allocation a historic double blow.

Therefore, "diversification reduces risk" needs an important caveat: diversification reduces normal market volatility but cannot fully protect against systematic crashes. Investors should not view diversification as "guaranteed no loss" magic, but rather a risk management tool that "works most of the time but offers discounted protection in extreme scenarios."

4.3 注意事項4.3 Caveats

  • 匯率風險：美元資產以台幣計價後，匯率波動可能放大或抵消報酬。台幣長期升值趨勢對美元資產不利。本研究未進行匯率避險（FX hedging），若有避險成本，海外資產的報酬會進一步降低。但反過來看，台幣在全球衰退時傾向貶值（出口導向經濟），此時美元資產的匯率效果反而有利——形成一種天然的「不對稱保護」。
  • 再平衡假設：年度再平衡忽略了交易成本和稅務影響。台灣的海外所得課稅門檻為 100 萬元，頻繁再平衡可能觸發不必要的稅務負擔。此外，再平衡的時間點（年初 vs 年中）可能影響結果。
  • 歷史期間偏差：分析期間 2006-05 ~ 2025-10，恰好涵蓋美國史上最長的多頭市場（2009-2020）。這段期間美股的卓越表現可能誇大了「加入美股」的分散效益。如果未來進入美股長期低迷期，分散到美股的效果可能大打折扣。
  • 個股模擬的局限：使用當前存在的大型股池進行模擬，存在存活者偏差。那些因為表現太差而被下市的公司不在我們的模擬範圍內，但真實投資人很可能會不幸選中它們。因此，實際的「隨機選股」風險可能高於我們的模擬結果。
  • 流動性風險未納入：某些資產在危機中可能面臨流動性枯竭（例如 2020 年 3 月的公司債 ETF、2008 年的不動產相關資產）。在流動性枯竭時，你可能無法以合理價格出售資產，實際損失可能大於模型預測。
  • 僅含 5 個資產類別：本研究的「分散」僅限於 5 個資產類別，未涵蓋商品、新興市場股票、加密貨幣等。更廣泛的分散可能提供額外效益，但也會增加管理成本和複雜度。
  • 靜態配置 vs 動態調整：本研究使用固定權重的靜態配置。實務上，許多投資人會根據市場環境動態調整（如「趨勢跟隨」或「風險預算」策略），這些動態方法的效果不在本研究範圍內。
  • Currency risk: TWD-denominated USD asset returns are affected by FX volatility. Long-term TWD appreciation trend is unfavorable for USD assets. This study does not include FX hedging; hedging costs would further reduce overseas returns. However, TWD tends to depreciate during global recessions (export-oriented economy), creating favorable "asymmetric protection" from USD assets.
  • Rebalancing assumption: Annual rebalancing ignores transaction costs and taxes. Taiwan's overseas income tax threshold is NT$1M; frequent rebalancing may trigger unnecessary tax burdens. Rebalancing timing (year-start vs mid-year) may also affect results.
  • Historical period bias: Analysis period 2006-05 ~ 2025-10 coincides with the longest US bull market in history (2009-2020). US stocks' exceptional performance may overstate 'adding US stocks' diversification benefit. If US stocks enter a prolonged slump, diversification to US may be less effective.
  • Stock simulation limitations: Uses currently surviving large-cap pool, introducing survivorship bias. Delisted underperformers are excluded from simulation, but real investors might have selected them. Actual random stock selection risk may be higher than simulated.
  • Liquidity risk excluded: Some assets face liquidity drought during crises (e.g., corporate bond ETFs in March 2020, real estate assets in 2008). When liquidity dries up, you may not sell at reasonable prices, with actual losses exceeding model predictions.
  • Only 5 asset classes: Our "diversification" is limited to 5 classes, excluding commodities, EM equities, crypto, etc. Broader diversification may offer additional benefits but increases management costs and complexity.
  • Static vs dynamic allocation: This study uses fixed-weight static allocation. In practice, many investors dynamically adjust (e.g., trend-following or risk-budget strategies), whose effects are outside this study's scope.

4.4 投資啟示4.4 Investment Implications

對保守型投資人：股債混合（如 60/40 或三分法）在正常市場中能有效降低波動。如果你是對虧損非常敏感、晚上看到帳面虧損 10% 就睡不著覺的投資人，分散投資應該是你的基本功——至少配置一部分到債券和黃金。但不要期待分散投資在股災時能完全保護你的投資組合。應保留足夠的現金或短期定存部位（建議至少 6 個月生活費），作為應對極端情境的「最後防線」，而不是依賴分散投資本身。

對穩健型投資人：本研究的數據支持「核心-衛星」策略——以台股或美股大盤 ETF 為核心（60-70%），搭配債券和黃金作為衛星配置（30-40%）。年度再平衡一次即可，不需要頻繁調整。這種策略能在大部分時間降低波動度和回撤，同時維持合理的報酬水準。最重要的是：在危機發生時，你的組合會跌，但跌幅比 100% 股票小——這個「少跌的部分」可能就是讓你能堅持住、不在最低點恐慌賣出的關鍵差異。

對積極型投資人：即使你對某個市場或某類資產非常有信心，集中持有單一市場（如 100% 台股）承受的波動度和回撤顯著高於分散配置。數據顯示 100% 台股的最大回撤達 -53.9%——你的 1000 萬一度只剩 461 萬。即使對台股前景樂觀，配置 20-40% 至海外資產和非股票資產仍可改善風險調整後報酬，而且在你「判斷錯誤」的情境中提供保護——畢竟，沒有人能保證自己的判斷永遠正確。

核心認知：分散投資不是「免費的午餐」也不是「萬靈丹」。它是一個有效但有限的風險管理工具。正確的期望是：正常市場降低波動 + 極端市場減緩（非消除）損失。如果你需要在任何市場環境下都絕對不虧損，你需要的不是分散投資，而是銀行定存——但代價是放棄長期的成長機會。

For conservative investors: Stock-bond mixes (60/40 or three-way) effectively reduce volatility in normal markets. If you are highly loss-averse — losing sleep over a 10% paper loss — diversification should be your foundation: allocate at least some portion to bonds and gold. But don't expect full crash protection. Maintain sufficient cash or short-term deposits (at least 6 months of living expenses) as a 'last line of defense' against extreme scenarios, rather than relying solely on diversification.

For moderate investors: Our data supports a 'core-satellite' strategy — Taiwan/US broad-market ETFs as core (60-70%), with bonds and gold as satellite (30-40%). Annual rebalancing is sufficient; no need for frequent adjustments. This strategy reduces volatility and drawdowns most of the time while maintaining reasonable returns. Most importantly: during crises, your portfolio will decline but less than 100% stocks — this 'reduced decline' may be the critical difference preventing panic selling at the bottom.

For aggressive investors: Even with high conviction in a specific market, concentrated single-market holdings (100% Taiwan) face significantly higher volatility and drawdowns. Data shows 100% Taiwan max drawdown reached -53.9% — your NT$10M portfolio once shrank to NT$461M. Even with optimistic Taiwan outlook, allocating 20-40% overseas and non-equity improves risk-adjusted returns and provides protection when your 'judgment is wrong' — after all, no one is always right.

Key insight: Diversification is neither a 'free lunch' nor a 'silver bullet.' It is an effective but limited risk management tool. Correct expectations: reduce volatility in normal markets + mitigate (not eliminate) extreme losses. If you need absolute zero loss in any market environment, you need bank deposits not diversification — but the cost is forgoing long-term growth opportunities.
