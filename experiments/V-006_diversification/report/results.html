V-006：分散投資就能降低風險？V-006: Does Diversification Always Reduce Risk?

以下依序呈現各項分析結果。每個小節先解釋「這張圖/這個數據在看什麼」，再報告關鍵發現，最後連結回研究問題。建議讀者搭配互動圖表（附錄 A.5）一起閱讀，可以用滑鼠懸停查看精確數值。

Results are presented sequentially below. Each subsection first explains what the chart/data shows, then reports key findings, and finally connects back to the research question. Readers are encouraged to use the interactive charts (Appendix A.5) in parallel for precise hover-to-view values.

3.1 各資產特性3.1 Asset Characteristics

這組圖在看什麼：五類資產在 2006-05 ~ 2025-10 期間各自的報酬和風險特性。累積報酬走勢圖顯示各資產長期走勢（對數尺度，讓早期和晚期的漲跌幅度視覺上一致），年度報酬柱狀圖則呈現每一年各資產的表現——有的年份股票大賺、債券虧損，有的年份剛好反過來。

關鍵觀察：

  • 台灣50 (0050) 年化報酬 11.9%、波動度 18.8%——報酬不錯但上下震盪劇烈。S&P 500（台幣計價）年化報酬 10.5%、波動度 14.1%。注意台幣計價後的波動比美元計價更高，因為匯率波動疊加了上去。
  • 美國長期公債 (TLT) 波動度 14.8%——出乎意料地不低！長期公債對利率極為敏感，2022 年暴力升息時 TLT 曾重挫超過 30%。「公債 = 安全」是一個需要修正的直覺。
  • 黃金 (GLD) 波動度 16.5%。黃金不像股票有基本面支撐，價格完全由供需和市場情緒驅動，因此波動模式與股票截然不同——這正是它能提供分散效益的原因。

常態性檢定顯示所有資產的月報酬均顯著偏離常態分佈（Jarque-Bera p<0.05），具有負偏態和厚尾特性。白話說就是：極端暴跌的頻率比「鐘形曲線」預測的要高得多。如果你用常態分佈模型預測風險，會嚴重低估真正的危險。這也是為什麼我們選用 MaxDD 和 CVaR 來衡量風險，而不只是看波動度。

What these charts show: Risk and return characteristics of five assets during 2006-05 ~ 2025-10. The cumulative return chart shows long-term trends (log scale for visual consistency across periods), while the annual return bar chart shows year-by-year performance — some years stocks surge while bonds decline, other years the reverse.

Key observations:

  • Taiwan 50 (0050): 11.9% annualized return, 18.8% volatility — decent returns but volatile. S&P 500 (TWD): 10.5% return, 14.1% volatility. Note that TWD-denominated volatility exceeds USD-denominated due to layered FX fluctuations.
  • US Long Treasuries (TLT): 14.8% volatility — surprisingly not low! Long-term bonds are highly interest-rate sensitive; TLT plunged over 30% during 2022 aggressive rate hikes. 'Bonds = safe' is an intuition that needs correcting.
  • Gold (GLD): 16.5% volatility. Unlike stocks with fundamental support, gold prices are driven purely by supply-demand and sentiment — this different volatility pattern is precisely why it provides diversification benefits.

Normality tests show all assets significantly deviate from normal distribution (Jarque-Bera p<0.05), with negative skew and fat tails. In plain terms: extreme crashes happen far more often than the "bell curve" predicts. Risk models assuming normality seriously underestimate true danger. This is why we use MaxDD and CVaR alongside volatility.

上方累積報酬走勢圖以對數尺度呈現，讓讀者能同時觀察到早期（金額較小時）和近期（金額較大時）的報酬變化。留意 2008 年金融海嘯時幾乎所有資產同步下滑（除了黃金和公債），以及 2020-2021 年各資產的強勁反彈。

The cumulative return chart above uses log scale so readers can observe both early (smaller amounts) and recent (larger amounts) return changes. Note how nearly all assets declined simultaneously during the 2008 GFC (except gold and bonds), and the strong rebound across assets in 2020-2021.

年度報酬柱狀圖讓讀者一目瞭然每年的贏家和輸家。關鍵觀察：沒有任何一類資產「永遠贏」——不同年份的冠軍總在輪替，這正是分散投資的理論基礎：如果你不知道明年誰會贏，那就每個都買一些。

The annual return chart clearly shows each year's winners and losers. Key observation: no single asset "always wins" — champions rotate across years, which is the theoretical basis for diversification: if you don't know next year's winner, buy a bit of everything.

3.2 相關性結構3.2 Correlation Structure

這組圖在看什麼：資產之間的「連動程度」。相關性是分散投資的核心——如果兩個資產總是同漲同跌（相關性 = 1），把它們放在一起沒有分散效果；如果一個漲另一個跌（相關性 = -1），風險就能大幅降低。我們不只看全期間的平均相關性，更要比較「平時」和「危機時」的差異。

關鍵觀察：

  • 0050 與 SPY 相關性最高（約 0.54）：兩者都是股票，全球股市連動性高。這意味著「台股 + 美股」的分散效果有限——就像把同一批雞蛋分別放在兩個相鄰的籃子裡，但搖晃時兩個籃子會一起晃。
  • SPY 與 TLT 呈負相關（約 -0.12）：這是分散投資的黃金組合！股市下跌時，投資人湧入公債避險，推升公債價格——兩者形成天然的「蹺蹺板」效應。
  • SPY 與 GLD 低相關（約 -0.01）：黃金的漲跌與股市幾乎無關，受驅動的因素不同（通膨預期、地緣政治、美元走勢），因此能提供獨立的分散效益。

危機時的相關性變化：Fisher z-transform 檢定顯示 0/10 對資產的相關性在危機時顯著高於正常期間（平均變化 -0.031）。這個「相關性崩潰」（correlation breakdown）是分散投資最大的隱憂——平時各走各的路，大難臨頭時卻一起倒下。用比喻來說：你以為自己的幾筆投資分散在不同地方，結果發現它們都建在同一條斷層帶上。

What these charts show: The degree of co-movement between assets. Correlation is diversification's core — if two assets always rise and fall together (correlation = 1), combining them provides no diversification; if one rises when the other falls (correlation = -1), risk drops dramatically. We examine both full-period average correlation and differences between normal and crisis periods.

Key observations:

  • 0050-SPY has highest correlation (~0.54): Both are equities with high global linkage. This means 'Taiwan + US stocks' diversification is limited — like putting the same eggs in two adjacent baskets that shake together.
  • SPY-TLT is negatively correlated (~-0.12): The golden diversification pair! When stocks fall, investors flee to bonds, pushing bond prices up — a natural 'seesaw' effect.
  • SPY-GLD is low correlation (~-0.01): Gold moves largely independently of stocks, driven by different factors (inflation expectations, geopolitics, USD strength), providing independent diversification.

Crisis correlation changes: Fisher z-transform shows 0/10 pairs have significantly higher correlations during crises (mean change -0.031). This 'correlation breakdown' is diversification's biggest concern — assets that walk separate paths in normal times fall together in crises. Analogy: you think your investments are diversified across locations, only to discover they're all built on the same fault line.

熱力圖中顏色越深代表相關性越高（正向為紅色、負向為藍色）。注意左上角的「股票區塊」（0050/SPY/VNQ）相關性普遍較高，而 TLT 那一列/行偏藍，代表公債與大多數資產呈低相關或負相關。

In the heatmap, deeper colors indicate higher correlation (red = positive, blue = negative). Note the "equity block" (0050/SPY/VNQ) showing generally higher correlations, while the TLT row/column tends blue, indicating low or negative correlation with most assets.

危機相關性比較圖直接呈現「正常期間」vs「危機期間」各資產對的相關性變化。多數配對在危機時相關性上升（靠近右上方），尤其是原本低相關的配對——原本的分散效果在危機中被「壓縮」。

The crisis correlation comparison chart directly shows correlation changes between normal and crisis periods for each asset pair. Most pairs see rising crisis correlations (toward upper right), especially those that are normally low-correlated — their diversification benefit gets "compressed" during crises.

滾動相關係數圖是本研究最具資訊量的互動圖表之一。它顯示各資產對的相關性如何隨時間變化——在正常時期相對穩定，但在危機期間（圖中紅色陰影區）急遽上升。特別是 2008 年金融海嘯和 2020 年 COVID 期間，幾乎所有資產配對的相關性都趨近 1，代表所有資產同步下跌、分散保護幾乎完全失效。建議讀者使用互動功能，將滑鼠懸停在不同時點上，觀察各配對的精確相關係數。

The rolling correlation chart is one of the most informative interactive charts in this study. It shows how each asset pair's correlation evolves over time — relatively stable in normal periods but surging during crises (red shaded areas). During the 2008 GFC and 2020 COVID in particular, nearly all asset pair correlations approached 1, meaning all assets fell in unison and diversification protection essentially vanished. Readers are encouraged to use the interactive hover feature to examine precise correlations at different time points.

3.3 投資組合回測結果3.3 Portfolio Backtest Results

這組圖在看什麼：七個投資組合的全期間績效比較——誰賺得多？誰震盪小？誰的風險調整後報酬最佳？累積報酬互動圖讓你直觀比較各組合的「致富速度」和「坐雲霄飛車的程度」，風險-報酬散佈圖則在一張圖上同時呈現報酬和波動度，讓你一眼看出哪個組合「CP 值」最高（越往左上越好）。

關鍵發現：

  • 100% 台股：年化 11.9%、波動 18.8%、MaxDD -53.9%。報酬不差，但坐雲霄飛車的感覺很強烈。如果你在 2008 年高點進場，帳面虧損一度超過 -54%。
  • 五資產等權：年化 9.2%、波動 10.0%、MaxDD -24.2%。報酬雖然不是最高，但波動度和最大回撤都是最低的。Sharpe Ratio 0.92，代表「每承受一單位風險，能獲得更多報酬」。
  • 實務配置：年化 10.1%、波動 10.7%、MaxDD -30.3%。因為 50% 台股的本土偏好，分散效果不如五資產等權。
  • 股60/債40：年化 8.5%、波動 9.7%、MaxDD -18.3%。經典配置的表現穩定，但 2022 年股債同跌時受創不輕。

整體而言，分散程度越高的組合，波動度和最大回撤越低，但報酬也可能略低。這並不意味著分散「不好」——相反，大多數人高估了自己面對帳面大幅虧損時的心理承受能力。一個「年化少賺 2%，但最大回撤少 15%」的組合，對多數投資人而言可能是更好的選擇。

What these charts show: Full-period performance comparison of seven portfolios — who earns more? Who fluctuates less? Who has the best risk-adjusted return? The cumulative return interactive chart lets you visually compare each portfolio's "wealth growth" and "roller coaster intensity," while the risk-return scatter plot simultaneously shows return and volatility, making it easy to spot which portfolio offers the best "value" (upper-left is best).

Key findings:

  • 100% Taiwan: Ann. 11.9%, Vol 18.8%, MaxDD -53.9%. Decent returns but an intense roller coaster. If you entered at the 2008 peak, paper losses once exceeded {gp('tw_only','max_drawdown')*100:.0f}%.
  • Five-Asset EW: Ann. 9.2%, Vol 10.0%, MaxDD -24.2%. Not the highest return but lowest volatility and drawdown. Sharpe Ratio {gp('five_asset','sharpe_ratio'):.2f} means 'more return per unit of risk.'
  • Practical: Ann. 10.1%, Vol 10.7%, MaxDD -30.3%. 50% Taiwan home bias limits diversification benefit vs five-asset EW.
  • Stock 60/Bond 40: Ann. 8.5%, Vol 9.7%, MaxDD -18.3%. Classic allocation performs steadily but was hurt during 2022 simultaneous stock-bond decline.

Overall, more diversified portfolios have lower volatility and drawdowns but potentially slightly lower returns. This does not mean diversification is "bad" — most people overestimate their psychological tolerance for large paper losses. A portfolio earning "2% less annually but with 15% less max drawdown" may be the better choice for most investors.

累積報酬互動圖清楚顯示各組合的成長軌跡。建議點選圖例（圖上方的名稱）來隱藏/顯示特定組合進行兩兩比較。特別值得注意的是 2008 年的「分歧點」——各組合在崩盤中的跌幅差異，以及之後的復原速度。

The cumulative return interactive chart clearly shows each portfolio's growth trajectory. Try clicking legend names to hide/show specific portfolios for pairwise comparison. Pay special attention to the 2008 "divergence point" — the varying crash depths and subsequent recovery speeds across portfolios.

風險-報酬散佈圖中，每個點代表一個投資組合，橫軸是波動度（風險）、縱軸是年化報酬。理想的位置是「左上角」——低風險、高報酬。可以明顯看到分散組合大多集中在比純台股或純美股更左邊的位置（波動更低），而報酬差異不大。這正是分散投資的核心價值：用較少的風險換取差不多的報酬。

In the risk-return scatter plot, each point represents a portfolio: x-axis is volatility (risk), y-axis is annualized return. The ideal position is "upper-left" — low risk, high return. Diversified portfolios clearly cluster to the left of pure Taiwan or pure US (lower volatility), with similar returns. This is diversification's core value: similar returns for less risk.

3.4 危機期間表現3.4 Crisis Period Performance

這組圖在看什麼：分散投資在「最糟的日子」表現如何？我們特別挑出六次重大危機事件（2008 金融海嘯、2011 歐債危機、2015 中國股災、2018 升息恐慌、2020 COVID、2022 升息+通膨），觀察各投資組合在這些極端情境下的最大回撤。

關鍵觀察：

  • 即使是五資產等權組合，在 2008 金融海嘯中仍遭受嚴重損失。這印證了前面的發現：在系統性危機中，所有資產同步下跌，分散保護大幅衰減。
  • 含有債券和黃金的組合，在多數危機中回撤較淺——這不是說「不虧」，而是「虧比較少」。在投資的世界裡，「少虧」本身就是一種優勢，因為從 -30% 回到原點只需要漲 43%，但從 -50% 回來需要漲 100%。
  • 不同危機的「傷害分佈」不同：2008 年是全面性的（所有資產都跌），2022 年則特殊（股債同跌，但黃金相對穩定），2020 年雖然暴跌但復原極快。

這些觀察的實務意義是：分散投資能「減輕」危機的衝擊，但不能「免疫」。如果你的退休金在崩盤時一定不能虧超過 20%，單純的分散投資可能不夠——你可能需要額外的尾部風險對沖策略。

What these charts show: How does diversification perform on the worst days? We isolated six major crises (2008 GFC, 2011 European debt, 2015 China crash, 2018 rate scare, 2020 COVID, 2022 rate hikes + inflation) and observed each portfolio's max drawdown during these extreme scenarios.

Key observations:

  • Even the five-asset EW portfolio suffered significant losses during the 2008 GFC. This confirms correlation breakdown: all assets decline in systemic crises, severely weakening diversification protection.
  • Portfolios with bonds and gold generally had shallower drawdowns — not "no loss" but "less loss." In investing, "losing less" is itself an advantage: recovering from -30% requires a +43% gain, but recovering from -50% requires +100%.
  • Different crises have different "damage patterns": 2008 was universal (everything fell), 2022 was unique (stocks and bonds fell together while gold held steady), 2020 crashed hard but recovered quickly.

Practical implication: diversification "mitigates" crisis impact but doesn't provide "immunity." If your retirement fund absolutely cannot lose more than 20% in a crash, simple diversification may be insufficient — you may need additional tail risk hedging strategies.

危機回撤柱狀圖讓你直觀比較各組合在不同危機中的「受傷程度」。柱子越長代表跌越多。注意 2008 年那一列的柱子整體最長——那是近代史上最嚴重的金融危機。

The crisis drawdown bar chart lets you visually compare each portfolio's "damage" across crises. Longer bars mean deeper drawdowns. Note the 2008 column's universally long bars — it was the most severe financial crisis in modern history.

互動版危機分析圖可以讓你將滑鼠懸停在各個危機區段，查看各組合在該危機中的精確回撤數字。試著比較 100% 台股和五資產等權在同一次危機中的差異——這就是分散投資在「最壞的日子」能為你做的事。

The interactive crisis analysis chart lets you hover over each crisis period to see precise drawdown figures. Try comparing 100% Taiwan vs five-asset EW in the same crisis — this is what diversification can do for you on the "worst days."

3.5 漸進式分散效益3.5 Incremental Diversification Benefit

這組圖在看什麼：如果你從 100% 台股出發，依序加入美股、美債、黃金、不動產，每加一個資產類別，風險降低多少？這是回答「我到底需要分散到幾種資產」的關鍵圖表。

關鍵觀察：

  • 前兩步效果最大：從 100% 台股加入美股，然後再加入美債——這兩步帶來的波動度和回撤降低最為顯著。特別是加入美債的效果驚人，因為債券與股票的負相關性直接抵消了部分股市波動。
  • 後續加入的效果遞減：第四、第五個資產（黃金、不動產）的改善幅度越來越小。這符合數學上的「邊際效益遞減」——就像在空房間裡放家具，第一件傢俱讓房間變得很不一樣，但放到第十件時，差異已經不大了。
  • 5 個資產類別已經足夠：再增加更多類別（如原物料、加密貨幣）的邊際效益可能微乎其微，但會增加管理的複雜度和成本。

What these charts show: Starting from 100% Taiwan stocks, how much does risk decrease as you add US stocks, US bonds, gold, and REITs one by one? This is the key chart for answering "how many asset classes do I actually need?"

Key observations:

  • First two steps have the largest effect: Adding US stocks to 100% Taiwan, then adding US bonds — these two steps provide the most significant volatility and drawdown reduction. Adding bonds is especially impactful because their negative correlation with stocks directly offsets market volatility.
  • Subsequent additions have diminishing effects: The 4th and 5th assets (gold, REITs) show progressively smaller improvements. This follows mathematical "diminishing marginal returns" — like furnishing an empty room: the first piece makes a huge difference, but by the tenth, the change is barely noticeable.
  • Five asset classes are sufficient: Adding more classes (commodities, crypto) likely provides negligible marginal benefit while increasing management complexity and costs.

漸進分散圖的每一步對應一個新增的資產類別。讀者可以清楚看到波動度的「階梯式」下降——前面的台階大、後面的台階小。

Each step in the incremental chart corresponds to a newly added asset class. Readers can clearly see the "staircase" decline in volatility — larger steps at first, smaller steps later.

風險指標比較圖以三個維度（波動度、MaxDD、CVaR）同時呈現各組合的風險輪廓。越靠近原點代表風險越低。可以明顯看到：分散組合整體向原點靠攏，而單一市場組合（尤其是 100% 台股）離原點最遠。

The risk comparison chart presents each portfolio's risk profile across three dimensions (volatility, MaxDD, CVaR). Closer to the origin means lower risk. Diversified portfolios clearly cluster toward the origin, while single-market portfolios (especially 100% Taiwan) are furthest away.

3.6 個股分散模擬3.6 Individual Stock Simulation

這組圖在看什麼：前面的分析都是「跨資產分散」（不同類別的資產），這裡則是「同市場分散」——如果你只買台股，但增加持有的股票數量（從 1 檔到 50 檔），風險能降低多少？答案揭示了分散投資的另一個面向：個股風險 vs 市場風險。

關鍵觀察：

  • 持有 1 檔個股的中位數波動度為 25.3%——這是「全部押在一家公司」的風險程度。想像你把所有積蓄投入一家公司的股票：如果那家公司出了大問題（如財務造假、產品失敗），你可能血本無歸。
  • 增加到 5 檔時降至 18.6%，10 檔降至 16.6%，50 檔降至 14.8%。從 1 到 10 檔的降幅最大，之後越來越平緩。
  • 這條遞減曲線完美符合理論預測：個股風險（非系統性風險）在 10-20 檔時已被消除大半，之後再增加檔數的效果極為有限。剩下的是「市場風險」（系統性風險）——無論你持有多少檔台股，只要是台股就會受到台灣經濟和全球連動的影響。
  • 這也解釋了為什麼「跨資產分散」比「同市場分散」更有效：前者能降低系統性風險（透過不同類別資產的低相關性），後者只能降低個股風險（已被 10-20 檔消除）。

What these charts show: Previous analyses examined cross-asset diversification (different asset classes); here we look at same-market diversification — if you only buy Taiwan stocks but increase the number held (from 1 to 50), how much does risk decrease? The answer reveals another dimension: idiosyncratic vs systematic risk.

Key observations:

  • Single stock median volatility is 25.3% — the risk of going 'all-in on one company.' Imagine putting all savings into one stock: if that company has major problems (fraud, product failure), you could lose everything.
  • With 5 stocks: 18.6%, 10 stocks: 16.6%, 50 stocks: 14.8%. The largest drop is from 1 to 10, then it flattens.
  • This decay curve perfectly matches theory: idiosyncratic risk is mostly eliminated by 10-20 stocks, with minimal further benefit. The remaining is "systematic risk" — no matter how many Taiwan stocks you hold, all are exposed to Taiwan's economy and global linkages.
  • This explains why cross-asset diversification is far more effective than same-market: the former reduces systematic risk (via low correlations between different asset classes), the latter only reduces idiosyncratic risk (already eliminated by 10-20 stocks).

波動度遞減曲線圖是理解「為什麼 20 檔就夠了」的關鍵。陰影區域代表模擬的分佈範圍（因為隨機選不同的股票組合，結果會有差異），實線代表中位數。曲線在 10-20 檔後幾乎水平——代表繼續加股票已經沒什麼用了。

The volatility decay curve is key to understanding "why 20 stocks is enough." Shaded area shows simulation distribution range (different random stock combinations yield different results); the solid line is the median. The curve flattens after 10-20 stocks — adding more is essentially useless.

最大回撤的遞減模式與波動度類似，但衰減速度更慢——即使持有 50 檔股票，最大回撤仍可能很大，因為在全市場大跌時，所有台股都會跟著跌。這再次強調：同市場分散無法保護你免受系統性風險的衝擊。

Max drawdown's decay pattern is similar to volatility but slower — even with 50 stocks, max drawdown can still be severe because all Taiwan stocks fall during market-wide crashes. This reinforces: same-market diversification cannot protect against systematic risk.
