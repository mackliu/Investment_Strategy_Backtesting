V-006：分散投資就能降低風險？V-006: Does Diversification Always Reduce Risk?

緒論Introduction

1.1 研究背景1.1 Background

「不要把所有雞蛋放在同一個籃子裡」——這句話幾乎是每位理財顧問開口的第一句建議。在台灣，隨著海外投資管道日益便利（如複委託和國際券商），越來越多投資人開始將資金配置到美股、債券、黃金等不同資產。根據金管會統計，台灣人持有的境外基金規模已超過 3 兆新台幣，顯示「分散投資」已從教科書理論走入一般投資人的日常實踐。

然而，「分散投資」的概念看似簡單，實際操作卻充滿了隱藏的陷阱。用生活化的比喻來說：如果你在不同超市各存了一箱水果，確實能降低「某一箱壞掉就全部沒水果吃」的風險。但如果整個城市停電（系統性風險），所有超市的冰箱都會壞掉——你分散存放的水果照樣全部腐壞。2008 年金融海嘯就是那場「全城大停電」：原本不太相關的股票、房地產、商品全部同步暴跌，讓許多自以為「已經分散」的投資人措手不及。

對台灣投資人而言，分散投資還有一個額外的維度：跨國投資涉及匯率風險。你買了美股賺了 10%，但如果同期新台幣升值 5%，換回台幣後你的實際報酬只剩約 5%。反過來，如果台幣貶值，匯率還會幫你多賺一些。這就像出國旅遊時的匯率——有時候會讓你的旅費變便宜（台幣升值），有時候讓你多花錢（台幣貶值），而你無法提前知道方向。因此，本研究堅持以台幣計價的角度，完整檢驗分散投資在包含匯率風險後的真實效果。

'Don't put all your eggs in one basket' is perhaps the first piece of advice any financial advisor gives. In Taiwan, as overseas investing channels become increasingly accessible (via sub-brokerage and international brokers), more investors are allocating to US stocks, bonds, gold, and other assets. According to FSC statistics, Taiwanese hold over NT$3 trillion in offshore funds, showing that diversification has moved from textbook theory to everyday practice.

However, diversification is deceptively simple in concept but full of hidden pitfalls. Here is a real-life analogy: if you store fruit boxes in different supermarkets, you reduce the risk of losing everything if one box goes bad. But if the entire city loses power (systematic risk), all refrigerators fail simultaneously — your distributed fruit spoils anyway. The 2008 GFC was that 'citywide blackout': previously uncorrelated stocks, real estate, and commodities all crashed simultaneously, catching many 'diversified' investors off guard.

For Taiwanese investors, there is an additional dimension: cross-border investing involves currency risk. If you earn 10% on US stocks but TWD appreciates 5% in the same period, your TWD return is only about 5%. Conversely, TWD depreciation can boost returns. This is like exchange rates when traveling abroad — sometimes your trip gets cheaper (TWD appreciation), sometimes more expensive (TWD depreciation), and you can't predict the direction. This study therefore examines diversification effectiveness in TWD terms, including currency risk.

1.2 文獻回顧1.2 Literature Review

Markowitz (1952): 現代投資組合理論（MPT）的奠基者。Markowitz 證明了一個違反直覺的結論：只要資產間的相關性低於 1，將它們組合在一起後，整體風險就能低於各資產風險的加權平均。這就像一支棒球隊——每個球員都有弱點，但如果他們的弱點出現在不同時機，整支球隊的表現就會比「平均球員」更穩定。不過，Markowitz 的原始模型假設報酬呈常態分佈且相關性穩定，這些假設在實際市場中經常被違反。

Longin & Solnik (2001): 這篇經典研究發現了一個令人不安的現象——股票市場在下跌時的相關性顯著高於上漲時（不對稱相關性）。用白話說就是：好日子的時候各走各的路，壞日子的時候卻一起倒楣。這意味著分散投資在最需要保護的熊市中，效果反而最差——好比你買的保險，在你最需要理賠的時候卻說「這不在保障範圍內」。

Statman (1987): 研究指出約 30-40 檔隨機選取的股票即可消除大部分「個股風險」（如某家公司爆發財務醜聞）。但再怎麼增加股票數量，也消除不了「市場風險」（如整體經濟衰退）。想像一下：你在同一條街上開了 30 家不同類型的店，可以降低「某家店經營不善」的風險，但如果這條街發生洪水（系統性風險），所有店都會受災。

Campbell, Koedijk & Kuo (2002): 分析了國際資產配置中的匯率效應。研究發現匯率波動會增加跨國投資的波動度，但同時也提供了額外的分散效益。對台灣投資人而言，美元資產在台幣貶值時提供天然的避險效果（台灣出口導向經濟在全球衰退時台幣傾向貶值），形成一種有利的「非對稱保護」。

Ang & Chen (2002): 進一步量化了「不對稱相關性」的程度：下跌市場中的相關性比上漲市場高出約 11.6%。這對分散投資的實務意義重大——用傳統（全期間平均）相關性來建構投資組合，會高估在熊市中的分散保護效果。就像用晴天時測量的防水能力來預測暴風雨中的表現——結果一定會令人失望。

Ilmanen (2011) "Expected Returns": 系統性地回顧了各資產類別的長期預期報酬和分散效益。Ilmanen 強調「真正的分散」不僅是持有不同股票，更是持有對不同經濟情境有不同反應的資產——例如在通膨時黃金上漲、在衰退時公債上漲。這種「全天候」的思維方式，是 Ray Dalio 的橋水基金廣為人知的策略基礎。

Driessen & Laeven (2007): 利用 DCC-GARCH 模型研究國際分散投資效益。發現已開發國家間的分散效益因相關性上升而逐年縮小，但納入新興市場仍能提供顯著的分散效益。對台灣投資人的啟示是：即使台股與美股的連動性在上升，跨入債券、黃金等非股票資產類別仍能提供有意義的分散保護。

本土研究：台灣學者如林蒼祥等（2015）研究台灣投資人的資產配置行為，發現「本土偏差」(home bias) 顯著——多數台灣投資人將超過 80% 的金融資產配置在台灣市場。這可能部分源自對本土市場的熟悉感（「我比較了解台積電」），部分源自對匯率風險的擔憂。本研究正是要量化這種「本土偏差」的風險代價。

Markowitz (1952): The founder of Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT). Markowitz proved a counterintuitive result: as long as asset correlations are below 1, combining them yields lower risk than the weighted average of individual risks. Like a baseball team — each player has weaknesses, but if those weaknesses occur at different times, the team performs more consistently than the 'average player.' However, the original model assumes normal returns and stable correlations — assumptions often violated in practice.

Longin & Solnik (2001): This seminal study found a troubling asymmetry — equity correlations are significantly higher during downturns than upturns. In plain terms: in good times, assets go their own ways; in bad times, they fall together. This means diversification works worst when protection is needed most — like insurance that says 'this isn't covered' precisely when you need to claim.

Statman (1987): Found that 30-40 randomly selected stocks eliminate most idiosyncratic risk (e.g., a company's fraud scandal), but no amount of stocks can eliminate systematic risk (e.g., economy-wide recession). Imagine opening 30 different shops on the same street — you reduce 'bad management' risk, but a flood (systematic risk) hits all shops equally.

Campbell, Koedijk & Kuo (2002): Analyzed currency effects in international allocation. FX volatility adds to cross-border investment risk but also provides diversification benefits. For Taiwanese investors, USD assets offer natural hedging during TWD depreciation (Taiwan's export-oriented economy sees TWD weaken during global slowdowns), creating favorable 'asymmetric protection.'

Ang & Chen (2002): Quantified asymmetric correlations: downside market correlations are ~11.6% higher than upside. This has major practical implications — using full-period average correlations to build portfolios overestimates bear market protection. Like testing waterproofing in sunshine and expecting it to hold in a hurricane — the result will disappoint.

Ilmanen (2011) 'Expected Returns': Systematically reviewed long-term expected returns and diversification benefits across asset classes. Ilmanen emphasized that 'true diversification' means holding assets that respond differently to economic scenarios — gold rises during inflation, bonds rise during recession. This 'all-weather' approach underpins Ray Dalio's Bridgewater strategy.

Driessen & Laeven (2007): Used DCC-GARCH models to study international diversification benefits. Found that benefits among developed markets have shrunk due to rising correlations, but emerging markets still offer significant diversification. For Taiwanese investors: even as Taiwan-US stock correlation rises, adding non-equity assets (bonds, gold) still provides meaningful diversification.

Local research: Taiwanese scholars such as Lin et al. (2015) documented significant 'home bias' — most Taiwanese investors allocate over 80% of financial assets domestically. This may stem partly from familiarity ('I know TSMC better') and partly from currency risk concerns. This study quantifies the risk cost of such home bias.

1.3 研究假說1.3 Research Hypotheses

H1: 五資產分散組合的最大回撤顯著小於單一資產（Bootstrap CI）

H2: 危機期間資產相關性顯著高於正常期間（Fisher z-transform）

H3: 個股分散的風險遞減符合 sigma(N) = sigma_sys + sigma_idio/sqrt(N) 模型

H1: Five-asset diversified portfolio has significantly smaller max drawdown than any single asset (Bootstrap CI)

H2: Asset correlations during crises are significantly higher than normal periods (Fisher z-transform)

H3: Risk reduction from stock diversification follows sigma(N) = sigma_sys + sigma_idio/sqrt(N)

H1 的邏輯：如果分散投資真的有效，那麼將資金分配到五種不同資產後，最慘的跌幅應該比全壓一種資產來得小。我們用 Bootstrap 模擬（類似抽籤重複實驗一萬次）來建立信賴區間，避免被單一歷史事件左右結論。這就像判斷一位學生的程度——不是只看一次考試成績，而是模擬他參加一萬次考試後的成績分佈。

H2 的邏輯：我們特別想知道：在市場崩盤時，「分散保護」是否會「失靈」？如果危機時相關性飆升到接近 1（所有資產同步下跌），那分散投資就像晴天的雨傘——好看但沒用。Fisher z-transform 是一種特別適合比較兩個時期相關係數差異的統計方法。

H3 的邏輯：理論上，持有越多股票，風險應該越低——但降低的速度會越來越慢（邊際效益遞減）。我們用數學模型來估計：到底需要幾檔股票才能消除大部分的個股風險？以及還有多少「市場風險」是怎麼加股票都無法消除的底線？

H1 logic: If diversification works, spreading capital across five different assets should result in smaller max drawdowns than concentrating in one. We use Bootstrap simulation (like drawing lots 10,000 times) to build confidence intervals, avoiding conclusions driven by single historical events. Like judging a student's ability — not by one exam, but by simulating 10,000 exams to see the score distribution.

H2 logic: We specifically want to know: does 'diversification protection' fail during crashes? If crisis correlations surge to near 1 (all assets falling together), diversification is like a sunny-day umbrella — nice to look at but useless. Fisher z-transform is particularly suited for comparing correlation coefficients between two periods.

H3 logic: Theoretically, more stocks mean lower risk — but the reduction slows down (diminishing marginal returns). We use mathematical modeling to estimate: how many stocks are needed to eliminate most idiosyncratic risk? And how much irreducible 'market risk' remains as the floor regardless of stock count?

1.4 研究貢獻1.4 Research Contribution

本研究的貢獻在於：(1) 以台灣投資人的視角（台幣計價、含匯率風險），完整檢驗跨資產分散投資在正常市場與危機時期的效果差異；(2) 同時比較「跨資產分散」（不同類別）和「同市場分散」（增加個股數量）的風險降低效果，幫助投資人理解「買更多檔台股」和「配置到不同資產」這兩種策略的差別；(3) 以生活化的方式呈現學術研究結果，讓一般投資人能理解分散投資的真實效果與限制。

This study contributes by: (1) examining cross-asset diversification from a Taiwanese investor's perspective (TWD-denominated, including FX risk) across normal and crisis periods; (2) comparing 'cross-asset diversification' (different classes) with 'same-market diversification' (adding more stocks), helping investors understand the difference between 'buying more Taiwan stocks' and 'allocating to different asset classes'; (3) presenting academic findings in accessible language so general investors can understand diversification's true effects and limitations.
